The applicant's representative of the Ministry of Property and Land Relations of the Donetsk People's Republic did not appear at the preparation for the court hearing, was duly notified of the date and place of the hearing, and in a statement submitted to the court, asked to consider the case without her participation.
The representative of the interested party of the Office of the Federal Service for State Registration of Cadastre and Cartography for the Donetsk People's Republic did not appear at the preparation for the court hearing, was duly notified of the date and place of the consideration of the case, and in the application submitted to the court asked to consider the case without his participation.
The representative of the interested party of the Ministry of Youth, Sports and Tourism of the Donetsk People's Republic did not appear at the court hearing, was duly notified of the date and place of the hearing of the case, and did not provide a valid reason for the failure to appear to the court.
Having studied the case materials, the judge comes to the following conclusions.
In accordance with paragraph 6 of part 1 of Article 262 of the Civil Procedure Code of the Russian Federation, in a special proceeding, the court considers cases of recognizing a movable thing as ownerless and recognizing the right of municipal ownership of an ownerless immovable thing.
According to Part 1 of Article 290 of the Civil Procedure Code of the Russian Federation, an application to recognize a movable thing as ownerless is submitted to the court by the person who took possession of it at the place of residence or location of the applicant.
However, Article 30 of the Civil Procedure Code of the Russian Federation establishes the exclusive jurisdiction of claims for rights to land plots, subsoil plots, isolated water bodies, forests, perennial plantings, buildings, including residential and non-residential premises, structures, structures, and other objects firmly connected with the land, which are brought to court at the location of these objects.
As follows from the materials of the civil case, the property is located at the address:
<address>
The jurisdiction of the Primorsky District Court of the city of Mariupol does not extend to the specified address.
By virtue of clause 3, part 2, article 33 of the Civil Procedure Code of the Russian Federation, the court transfers the case to another court of general jurisdiction if, during the consideration of the case in this court, it was revealed that it was accepted for proceedings in violation of the rules of jurisdiction.
In connection with the above, the court comes to the conclusion that the application of the Ministry of Property and Land Relations of the Donetsk People's Republic to recognize the right of state ownership of an ownerless immovable property was accepted by the Primorsky District Court of Mariupol in violation of the rules of jurisdiction, and therefore the case is subject to transfer of jurisdiction to the Zhovtnevyi District Court of the city of Mariupol of the Donetsk People's Republic.
Guided by Article 33, 224-225 of the Civil Procedure Code of the Russian Federation, the court
DEFINED:
a civil case upon an application from the Ministry of Property and Land Relations of the Donetsk People's Republic for recognition of the right of state ownership of an ownerless immovable property shall be transferred with jurisdiction to the Zhovtnevyi District Court of the city of Mariupol of the Donetsk People's Republic.
A private complaint against the ruling may be filed with the Supreme Court of the Donetsk People's Republic through the Primorsky District Court of the city of Mariupol within 15 days from the date of its issuance.
