According to Art. 262 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation, in the order of special proceedings, the court considers cases: 1) on the establishment of facts of legal significance; 2) about the adoption of a child; 3) on declaring a citizen missing or declaring a citizen dead; 4) on limiting the legal capacity of a citizen, on declaring a citizen incompetent, on limiting or depriving a minor aged fourteen to eighteen years of the right to independently manage their income; 5) on declaring a minor fully capable (emancipation); 6) on recognition of a movable thing as ownerless and recognition of the right of municipal ownership to an ownerless immovable thing; 7) on the restoration of rights to lost bearer securities or order securities (call proceedings); 9) on making corrections or changes in civil status records; 10) on applications for notarial acts performed or refusal to perform them; 11) on applications for the restoration of lost judicial proceedings. Federal laws may include other cases for consideration as special proceedings.
According to Part 1 of Art. 225 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, an ownerless thing is a thing that does not have an owner or the owner of which is unknown, or a thing for which the owner has renounced the right of ownership.
The procedure for accepting ownerless real estate into municipal ownership is regulated by Resolution of the People's Council dated March 21, 2024 No. 66-RZ “On the peculiarities of identifying, using and recognizing the right of municipal property of municipalities of the Donetsk People's Republic to residential premises with signs of ownerless property located on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic.”
As follows from Art. 2 of the said Resolution, signs of ownerless property in relation to residential premises are: failure to pay for residential premises and utilities within one year, lack of information about registered ownership, non-use of residential premises.

In such circumstances, the court considers a dispute about the law.
According to paragraph 3 of Art. 225 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, ownerless real estate is accepted for registration by the body that carries out state registration of rights to real estate, upon application from the local government body in whose territory they are located.
As corresponds to paragraph 5 of Art. 60 of the Law of the Donetsk People's Republic of August 14, 2023 "On Local Self-Government" No. 468-IINS, local government bodies formed in accordance with the legislation of the Russian Federation are the legal successors of the bodies that exercised on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic on the day of its admission to the Russian Federation and the formation of a new subject within the Russian Federation the powers attributed by the legislation of the Russian Federation to the powers of local bodies self-government in terms of: 1) property rights and obligations of such bodies; 2) contractual obligations, as well as other documents concluded by such bodies.
Guided by paragraph 1 of Article 10 of the People's Council Resolution No. 66-RZ of March 21, 2024, after three months from the date of registration of the residential premises as ownerless by the territorial body of the federal executive body carrying out state cadastral registration, state registration of rights, maintaining the Unified State Register of Real Estate, the authorized body applies to the court with an application for recognition of the right of municipal property of the municipality to this residential premises.
The body authorized to manage municipal property may apply to the court to recognize the right of ownership of ownerless real estate in a special proceeding, as defined in clause 6, part 1, art. 262 Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation.
According to clause 3.2. The standard procedure for identifying, recording and accepting into municipal ownership ownerless real estate and escheat property, approved by Decree of the Head of the Donetsk People's Republic No. 116 of April 28, 2021 “On identifying, recording and accepting into municipal ownership ownerless real estate and escheat property”, if before the ownerless real estate is accepted into municipal ownership, a person (persons) appears, considering (considering) themselves to be its owner, proof of ownership lies with such a person in court.
According to Part 3 of Art. 263 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation, if, when filing an application or considering a case in a special proceeding, it is established that there is a dispute about the right, the court issues a ruling to leave the application without consideration, in which it explains to the applicant and other interested parties their right to resolve the dispute in the manner of claim proceedings.
The main difference between special judicial proceedings and claim proceedings is the absence of a dispute about the law, which entails the absence of a substantive legal claim of one party against the other.
Thus, since the court hearing established the existence of a dispute about the right, the court considers it necessary to make a statement from the Administration of the Snezhnoye urban district of the Donetsk People's Republic, interested parties: Office of the Federal Service for State Registration, Cadastre and Cartography for the Donetsk People's Republic (Rosreestr Office for the Donetsk People's Republic),
Full name1
, on recognition of the right of municipal ownership of ownerless real estate - to be left without consideration.
Explain to the applicant and interested parties their right to resolve the dispute through litigation.
