According to Part 1 of Art. 1216 of the Civil Code of Ukraine, inheritance is the transfer of rights and obligations (inheritance) from an individual who has died (testator) to other persons.
According to Art. 1220 of the Civil Code of Ukraine, an inheritance opens as a result of the death of a person or his declaration as deceased.
Parts 2, 3 tbsp. 1223 of the Civil Code of Ukraine provides that in the absence of a will, its recognition as invalid, non-acceptance of the inheritance or refusal to accept it by the heirs under the will, as well as in the case of the will not covering the entire inheritance, the right to inherit by law is received by the persons defined in Articles 1261-1265 of the Civil Code of Ukraine. The right to inheritance arises on the opening day of the inheritance.
In accordance with Art. 1258 of the Civil Code of Ukraine, heirs by law receive the right to inherit one by one.
According to Art. 1261 of the Civil Code of Ukraine, first of all, the children of the testator, including those conceived during the life of the testator and those born after his death, the surviving spouse, and parents, have the right to inherit by law.
Part 1 art. 1268 of the Civil Code of Ukraine determines that the heir by will or by law has the right to accept the inheritance or not to accept it.
According to Art. 1297 of the Civil Code of Ukraine, an heir who has accepted an inheritance that includes real estate is obliged to contact a notary for the issuance of a certificate of the right to inheritance of real estate.
