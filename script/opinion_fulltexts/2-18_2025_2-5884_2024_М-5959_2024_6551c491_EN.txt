According to clause 6, part 1, art. 262, part 2 art. 290 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation, an application for recognition of ownership of an ownerless immovable thing is considered by the court in a special proceeding.
Paragraph 3 of Article 218 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation establishes that in cases and in the manner provided for by the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, a person can acquire ownership of property that does not have an owner, property whose owner is unknown, or property that the owner has abandoned or to which he has lost ownership on other grounds provided by law.
In accordance with Article 225 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, an ownerless thing is a thing that does not have an owner or the owner of which is unknown or, unless otherwise provided by law, the right of ownership to which the owner has refused.
Ownerless real estate is accepted for registration by the body that carries out state registration of rights to real estate, upon application from the local government body in whose territory they are located.
According to Part. 1 and 3 tbsp. 1 of the Federal Constitutional Law of the Russian Federation of October 4, 2022 No. 5-FKZ “On the admission into the Russian Federation of the Donetsk People’s Republic and the formation within the Russian Federation of a new subject - the Donetsk People’s Republic” (hereinafter Law No. 5-FKZ), the Donetsk People’s Republic was admitted to the Russian Federation in accordance with the Constitution of the Russian Federation and Art. 4 of the Federal Constitutional Law of the Russian Federation of December 17, 2001 No. 6-FKZ “On the procedure for admission to the Russian Federation and the formation of a new subject of the Russian Federation as a whole.” The Donetsk People's Republic is considered accepted into the Russian Federation from the date of signing the Agreement between the Russian Federation and the Donetsk People's Republic on the admission of the Donetsk People's Republic to the Russian Federation and the formation of a new entity within the Russian Federation.
The agreement between the Russian Federation and the Donetsk People's Republic on the admission of the Donetsk People's Republic to the Russian Federation and the formation of a new entity within the Russian Federation dated September 30, 2022, was ratified by the Federal Law of the Russian Federation of October 4, 2022 No. 372-FZ.
In accordance with Part 7 of Art. 12 of the Federal Constitutional Law of the Russian Federation dated 04.10.2022 No. 5-FKZ, management of property located on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic, which has signs of ownerlessness and is not classified as federal property, the property of a constituent entity of the Russian Federation or municipal property, is carried out by the authorities of the Donetsk People's Republic in the manner established by the Government of the Russian Federation, unless otherwise provided by this Federal Constitutional Law. The procedure for recognizing such property as ownerless, established by the regulatory legal acts of the Donetsk People's Republic, is applied until January 1, 2028, in agreement with the federal executive body exercising the functions of managing state property.
According to clause 2, part 5 of the Federal Constitutional Law of the Russian Federation of October 4, 2022 No. 5-FKZ, the Government of the Russian Federation for the transition period establishes the procedure and cases for harmonizing legal acts and other decisions of public authorities of the Donetsk People's Republic in relation to the management and disposal of individual property.
In accordance with the powers defined in clause 2, part 5 of the Federal Constitutional Law of the Russian Federation of October 4, 2022 No. 5-FKZ, the Government of the Russian Federation adopted Resolution No. 2502 of December 29, 2022 “On the procedure and cases of coordination of legal acts and other decisions of public authorities of the Donetsk People's Republic and Lugansk People's Republic, public authorities of the Zaporozhye region and Kherson region in relation to the management and disposal of individual property objects.”
According to clause 2 of the Rules for the coordination of legal acts and other decisions of public authorities of the Donetsk People's Republic and Lugansk People's Republic, public authorities of the Zaporozhye region and the Kherson region in relation to the management and disposal of individual objects of property, approved by Decree of the Government of the Russian Federation of December 29, 2022 No. 2502. public authorities of the Donetsk People's Republic and Lugansk People's Republic, public authorities authorities of the Zaporozhye region and the Kherson region, authorized to adopt legal acts and other decisions regarding the management and disposal of individual objects of property specified in paragraph 2 of the Decree of the Government of the Russian Federation dated December 29, 2022 No. 2502 “On the procedure and cases of coordination of legal acts and other decisions of public authorities of the Donetsk People's Republic and Lugansk People's Republic, public authorities of the Zaporozhye region and Kherson region in relation to the management and disposal of individual property objects" (hereinafter referred to as acts and other decisions, respectively), send acts and other decisions for approval to the Federal Agency for State Property Management (its territorial body).
In order to implement Art. Art. 12, 21 of the Federal Constitutional Law of the Russian Federation, Decree of the Head of the Donetsk People’s Republic dated May 10, 2023 No. 140 “On the peculiarities of acquiring the right of state and municipal property to ownerless real estate on the territory of the Donetsk People’s Republic” was adopted, which established that the body and officials of the Donetsk People’s Republic exercising powers classified by the legislation of the Russian Federation as the powers of local bodies self-government, before the completion of the formation of local self-government bodies, the local government body of a municipal formation in the Donetsk People's Republic, authorized to manage municipal property, has the right to apply to the court with a demand for recognition of the right of municipal ownership of ownerless buildings, structures (except for linear objects), premises, objects of unfinished construction that do not have an owner or the owner of which is unknown or the ownership of which the owner has refused, located on the territory of such a municipal education, after three months from the date of their registration as ownerless real estate by the body carrying out state registration of rights to real estate, villages, otherwise not provided for by this Decree (clause 1.1 of Decree No. 140).
On the date of admission of the Donetsk People's Republic to the Russian Federation, Decree of the Head of the Donetsk People's Republic dated April 28, 2021 No. 116 “On the identification, registration and acceptance into municipal ownership of ownerless real estate and escheatable property” was in force on the territory of the subject, which approved the Standard procedure for identifying, recording and accepting ownerless real estate and escheatable property into municipal ownership, currently in force time in the edition taking into account the changes made. By Decree of the Head of the Donetsk People's Republic No. 64 of February 27, 2023, which came into force on August 18, 2023.
Clause 1 of this Standard Procedure establishes that it was adopted to determine a unified procedure for identifying, registering, managing and accepting into municipal ownership ownerless real estate and escheat property identified on the territory of the corresponding administrative-territorial unit of the Donetsk People's Republic.
The subject of regulation of this Procedure are residential premises, garden houses, outbuildings intended to satisfy citizens' household and other needs, having signs of ownerless things, escheated property in the form of residential premises, land plots, as well as buildings, structures, and other real estate objects located on them, shares in the right of common shared ownership of these real estate objects, as well as movable property identified on such real estate objects.
The specified Standard Procedure provides for the registration as ownerless property of residential premises, garden houses, outbuildings intended to satisfy citizens' household and other needs, that is, real estate that does not have an owner or the owner of which is unknown or the ownership of which the owner has refused, including by ceasing to bear the burden of maintaining such property (in particular through non-payment of utilities for the last three years), provided that the owner has been present for the last six months outside the territory of the Russian Federation (clause 2.3 of the Standard Procedure).
According to para. 2 clause 1.3 of this Standard Procedure, an individual or legal entity may renounce the right of ownership of the property belonging to him by declaring this or taking other actions that definitely indicate his removal from the possession, use and disposal of the property without the intention of retaining any rights to this property, including by ceasing to bear the burden of maintaining such property (in particular through non-payment of utilities for the last three years), provided that the owner has been present for the last six months. outside the territory of the Russian Federation.
By the Resolution of the People's Council of the Donetsk People's Republic of March 21, 2024, in accordance with the Federal Constitutional Law of the Russian Federation of October 4, 2022 No. 5-FKZ, Law No. 66-RZ was adopted “On the specifics of identifying, using and recognizing the right of municipal property of municipalities of the Donetsk People's Republic to residential premises with signs of ownerless property located on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic” (hereinafter referred to as Law No. 66-RZ), which establishes the specifics of the procedure for identifying, recording and using residential premises with signs of ownerless property located on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic, recognizing the right of municipal ownership of municipal entities of the Donetsk People's Republic to this property, as well as the procedure for disposing of this property.
In accordance with Part 2 of Art. 1 of this Law, its effect will apply to residential premises in the form of residential buildings, apartments, rooms.
According to Art. 2 of Law No. 66-RZ, the signs of ownerless property in relation to residential premises are: failure to pay for residential premises and utilities within one year before the date of identification of a residential premises that has signs of ownerlessness; lack of information about the registered ownership of residential premises in the Unified State Register of Real Estate; non-use of residential premises, which, among other things, creates a threat to its safety (or an apartment building and in the case where the premises are an apartment, a room in an apartment building), safety for the life and health of other persons (including the owner of other premises in an apartment building in the case where the premises are an apartment, a room in an apartment building).
In case of receiving information about residential premises that have signs of ownerless property, the authorized body in accordance with Part 3 of Art. 5 of Law No. 66-RZ, within 10 calendar days from the date of discovery of a residential premises that has signs of ownerlessness, places on the official website of the local government body on the Internet, as well as in accessible places at the entrance to the residential premises, an announcement about the identification of a residential premises that has signs of ownerlessness, and about the need for personal appearance before the authorized body of a person who considers himself to be its owner or has ownership of it. rights, within 30 calendar days from the date of placement of such an advertisement with the following documents: a passport of a citizen of the Russian Federation or other identification document; title documents for residential premises.
Part 4 of the same article provides that after 30 calendar days from the date of publication of the announcement, in the event of the absence of a person (persons) who considers themselves the owner of the residential premises, and after receiving information indicating that the residential premises, which have signs of ownerless property, do not have an owner:
- within 5 working days, draws up a report on the impossibility of identifying the owner of this residential premises;
- organizes, if necessary, in the prescribed manner, the carrying out of cadastral work in relation to residential premises that have signs of being ownerless, as well as assigning a postal address to it;
- carries out, if necessary, the collection of other documents confirming the presence of signs of ownerless property in the residential premises, including documents indicating the absence of residents in such residential premises (inspection reports, extracts from house books, personal accounts, etc.):
- organizes a commission inspection of the residential premises. having signs of being ownerless (with an on-site visit), with the involvement of the necessary representatives of the executive bodies of the Donetsk People's Republic and territorial bodies of the federal executive authorities, organizations (as agreed) and with the drawing up of an appropriate act, which indicates the details of the act, the date of inspection, information about the persons involved, the location, technical and other condition of the residential premises, an inventory of furniture, household appliances and other movable property located in the residential premises premises, as well as other information indicating the presence of signs of neglect of the residential premises.
By virtue of Art. 9 of Law No. 66-RZ, the procedure for recognizing ownerless residential premises as municipal property is terminated upon personal announcement of the owner of such premises and confirmation of ownership of such premises on the basis of the documents provided for in subsection. “a”, “b” clause 3 of Art. 5 of this Law (passport of a citizen of the Russian Federation or other identification document; title documents for residential premises).
In accordance with Part 1 of Art. 10 of Law No. 66-RZ, after three months from the date of registration of the residential premises as ownerless by the territorial body of the federal executive body carrying out state cadastral registration, state registration of rights, maintenance of the Unified State Register of Real Estate, the authorized body applies to the court with an application for recognition of the right of municipal ownership of the municipality to this residential premises in the manner prescribed by the legislation of the Russian Federation.
Similar provisions are set out in paragraph 3.1 of the above Standard Procedure, approved by Decree of the Head of the Donetsk People's Republic No. 116 dated April 28, 2021, which establishes that after three months from the date of registration of an ownerless immovable property, the administration is obliged to file a claim in court to invoke the right of municipal ownership of this thing.
Moreover, by virtue of clause 3.2 of the same Standard Procedure, if before the acceptance of an ownerless immovable property into municipal ownership, a person (persons) who considers themselves its owner appears, proving the right of ownership lies with such a person in court.
By-laws of the Donetsk People's Republic regulating the issues of identifying, staging, accounting for ownerless property are aimed at resolving issues of introducing state management of ownerless real estate, ensuring their safety and determining the optimal way to manage them in accordance with established standards before the owner is declared or before the form of ownership of the said property is established in court.
The legislative regulation itself, which establishes as a general rule the preservation of property rights for individuals and legal entities and ensuring continuity between the property rights of Ukraine and the property rights of the People's Republic, indicating the moment of termination of the property rights of the previous copyright holder and its emergence in the Donetsk People's Republic, is acceptable and is due to the peculiarities of the transition period, the introduction of which was aimed, among other things, at streamlining property relations during the integration of new subjects of the Russian Federation into the Russian legal and economic systems and thus most importantly, maintaining legal certainty and stability of civil circulation in conditions new to the Donetsk People's Republic.
