The court, having examined the case materials and assessed the evidence presented in its entirety, comes to the following conclusions.
In accordance with Article 4 of the Federal Constitutional Law of the Russian Federation dated October 4, 2022 No. 5-FKZ “On the admission of the Donetsk People's Republic to the Russian Federation and the formation of a new subject within the Russian Federation - the Donetsk People's Republic” (hereinafter referred to as Law No. 5-FKZ), legislative and other regulatory legal acts of the Russian Federation are valid on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic from the date of admission to the Russian Federation Donetsk People's Republic and the formation of a new subject within the Russian Federation, unless otherwise provided by this Federal Constitutional Law. Regulatory legal acts of the Donetsk People's Republic are valid on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic until the end of the transition period or until the adoption of the corresponding regulatory legal act of the Russian Federation and (or) the regulatory legal act of the Donetsk People's Republic. Regulatory legal acts of the Donetsk People's Republic that contradict the Constitution of the Russian Federation are not applied.
Article 21 of Law No. 5-FKZ establishes the specifics of regulating relations in the sphere of real estate turnover on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic.
By virtue of Part 7 of Article 12 of Law No. 5-FKZ, the procedure for recognizing property that is not classified as federal property, the property of a constituent entity of the Russian Federation or municipal property as ownerless, established by the regulatory legal acts of the Donetsk People's Republic, is applied until January 1, 2028, in agreement with the federal executive body performing the functions of managing state property.
In accordance with Article 225 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, an ownerless thing is a thing that does not have an owner or the owner of which is unknown or, unless otherwise provided by law, the right of ownership to which the owner has refused.
Ownerless real estate is accepted for registration by the body that carries out state registration of rights to real estate, upon application from the local government body in whose territory they are located.
Paragraph 3 of Article 218 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation establishes that in cases and in the manner provided for by this Code, a person may acquire ownership of property that does not have an owner, property whose owner is unknown, or property that the owner has abandoned or to which he has lost ownership on other grounds provided by law.
Decree of the Acting Head of the Donetsk People's Republic dated 05/10/2023 No. 140 (hereinafter referred to as Decree No. 140 dated 05/10/2023) established the specifics of acquiring state and municipal property rights to ownerless real estate on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic.
According to clause 1.1 of Decree No. 140 dated 10.05.2023, the body and officials of the Donetsk People's Republic exercising the powers classified by the legislation of the Russian Federation as the powers of local government bodies, before the completion of the formation of local government bodies, the local government body of the municipality in the Donetsk People's Republic, authorized to manage municipal property, has the right to apply to the court with a demand for recognition of the right municipal ownership of ownerless buildings, structures (except for linear objects), premises, objects of unfinished construction that do not have an owner or the owner of which is unknown or the owner of which has refused ownership, located on the territory of such a municipal entity, after three months from the date of their registration as ownerless real estate by the body carrying out state registration of rights to real estate, unless otherwise provided by this Decree.
In accordance with paragraph 2.12 of the Decree of the Head of the Donetsk People's Republic of April 28, 2021 No. 116 “On the identification, registration and acceptance into municipal ownership of ownerless real estate and escheat property”, in order to register a real estate object as ownerless, the administration issues a corresponding order and applies to the territorial body of the federal executive body that carries out state cadastral registration, state registration of rights, maintenance of the Unified State Register of Real Estate, with an application for registration of an immovable property as ownerless and other documents provided for by current legislation.
According to paragraph 4 of the Procedure for registering ownerless real estate, established by Order of the Federal Service for State Registration, Cadastre and Cartography dated March 15, 2023 No. P/0086, registration of ownerless real estate is carried out in the following order:
acceptance of an application for registration of an ownerless real estate object, for the acceptance of a real estate object again into possession, use and disposal (hereinafter referred to as the application) and the documents provided for in paragraph 10 of the Procedure for registering ownerless real estate;
checking documents for compliance with the requirements of Federal Law No. 218-FZ for documents received by the rights registration authority;
making entries in the Unified State Register of Real Estate on the registration of ownerless real estate.

By virtue of Part 3 of Article 263 of the Civil Procedure Code of the Russian Federation, if, when filing an application or considering a case in a special proceeding, it is established that there is a dispute about the right within the jurisdiction of the court, the court issues a ruling to leave the application without consideration, in which it explains to the applicant and other interested parties their right to resolve the dispute in the manner of litigation.
Chapter 33 of the Civil Procedure Code of the Russian Federation regulates the procedure for considering cases on recognizing a movable thing as ownerless or recognizing ownership of an ownerless immovable thing.
According to Part 2 of Article 293 of the said Code, the court, having recognized that the immovable thing does not have an owner or the owner of the immovable thing is unknown and it has been registered in the prescribed manner, makes a decision to recognize the right of municipal property.
In this case, there is no need to resolve the dispute about the subjective right itself, the existence of which depends on the presence or absence of this fact, and therefore the said application is subject to consideration in a special proceeding.
In accordance with Part 1 of Article 55 of the Civil Procedure Code of the Russian Federation, evidence in the case is information obtained in the manner prescribed by law about the facts, on the basis of which the court establishes the presence or absence of circumstances justifying the demands and objections of the parties, as well as other circumstances that are important for the proper consideration and resolution of the case.
This information can be obtained from explanations of the parties and third parties, testimony of witnesses, written and material evidence, audio and video recordings, and expert opinions.
Evidence obtained in violation of the law has no legal force and cannot be used as the basis for a court decision (Part 2 of Article 55 of the Civil Procedure Code of the Russian Federation).
By virtue of Part 1 of Article 67 of the Civil Procedure Code of the Russian Federation, the court evaluates evidence according to its internal conviction, based on a comprehensive, complete, objective and direct examination of the evidence available in the case. No evidence has predetermined value for the court. The court evaluates the relevance, admissibility, reliability of each evidence separately, as well as the sufficiency and interconnection of the evidence in its entirety.
Thus, analyzing the evidence collected in the case in its entirety, the court comes to the conclusion that the application of the administration of the Mariupol urban district must be satisfied.
