The case was considered in the absence of the parties in accordance with Art. 167 Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation.
The court, having studied the materials of the case and, having assessed, according to its internal conviction, based on a comprehensive, complete and direct examination of the evidence available in the case, the relevance, admissibility, reliability of each evidence separately, as well as the sufficiency and interconnection of the evidence in their totality, comes to the following.
In accordance with paragraph 1 of Art. 225 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, ownerless is a thing that does not have an owner or the owner of which is unknown or, unless otherwise provided by law, the owner of which has renounced the right of ownership.
According to paragraph 3 of Art. 225 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, ownerless real estate is accepted for registration by the body that carries out state registration of rights to real estate, upon application from the local government body in whose territory they are located.
After a year has passed from the date of registration of an ownerless immovable property, the body authorized to manage municipal property may apply to the court with a request to recognize the right of municipal ownership of this thing.
By virtue of clause 6, part 1, art. 262 of the Civil Procedure Code of the Russian Federation, cases of recognizing a movable thing as ownerless and recognizing the right of municipal ownership of an ownerless immovable thing are subject to consideration in a special proceeding.
Based on Part 3 of Art. 263 of the Civil Procedure Code of the Russian Federation, if, when filing an application or considering a case in a special proceeding, it is established that there is a dispute about the right within the jurisdiction of the court, the court issues a ruling to leave the application without consideration, in which it explains to the applicant and other interested parties their right to resolve the dispute in the manner of litigation.
As established in Part 1 of Art. 292 of the Civil Procedure Code of the Russian Federation, when preparing a case for trial, a judge finds out which persons (owners, actual owners and others) can provide information about the ownership of property, and also requests relevant organizations about the information available about it.
By virtue of Part 2 of Art. 293 of the Civil Procedure Code of the Russian Federation, the court, having recognized that the immovable thing does not have an owner or the owner of the immovable thing is unknown and it was registered in the prescribed manner, makes a decision to recognize the right of municipal property.
From the above norms in their totality it follows that ownerless is an abandoned, abandoned thing that no one actually owns and which no one claims. The lack of ownership rights in itself does not mean that the thing is ownerless. Recognition of ownership of an ownerless thing in a special proceeding is impossible if other persons have legal claims to this thing, since in this case a dispute inevitably arises about the right of these persons to this thing.
