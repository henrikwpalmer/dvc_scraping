In accordance with paragraphs. 1 and 4 tbsp. 225 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, ownerless is a thing that does not have an owner or the owner of which is unknown or, unless otherwise provided by law, the owner of which has renounced the right of ownership.

Article 292 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation stipulates that when preparing a case for trial, a judge finds out which persons (owners, actual owners and others) can provide information about the ownership of property, and also requests relevant organizations about the information available about it. An application to recognize a thing as ownerless or to recognize the right of ownership to an ownerless immovable thing is considered by the court with the participation of interested persons.
Part 3 of Art. 263 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation establishes that if, when filing an application or considering a case in a special proceeding, the existence of a dispute about the right is established, the court issues a ruling to leave the application without consideration, in which it explains to the applicant and other interested parties their right to resolve the dispute in the manner of claim proceedings.
