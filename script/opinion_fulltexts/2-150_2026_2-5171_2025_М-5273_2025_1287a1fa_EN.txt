<address>

The representative of the interested party of the Office of the Federal Service for State Registration, Cadastre and Cartography for the Donetsk People's Republic did not appear at the court hearing and did not inform the court about the reasons for his absence.
Having studied the application submitted by the plaintiff’s representative and the case materials, the court comes to the following conclusion.
Part 1 of Article 39 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation provides that the plaintiff has the right to change the basis or subject of the claim, increase or decrease the size of the claim, or abandon the claim.
In accordance with Part 2 of Art. 39 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation, the court does not accept the plaintiff’s refusal of the claim if this is contrary to the law or violates the rights and legitimate interests of other persons.
According to Art. 220 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation, the court terminates the proceedings if the plaintiff renounces the claim and the refusal is accepted by the court.
According to Part 3 of Art. 173 Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation in conjunction with Art. 220 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation, if the plaintiff refuses the claim and the court accepts it, the court issues a ruling, which simultaneously terminates the proceedings.
As stated in Art. 221 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation, the proceedings in the case are terminated by a court ruling, which states that a repeated appeal to the court in a dispute between the same parties, about the same subject and on the same grounds is not allowed.
Due to the fact that the refusal of the stated demands does not contradict the law and does not violate anyone’s rights and interests protected by law, the court considers it possible to accept the applicant’s refusal of the stated demands and terminate the proceedings.
