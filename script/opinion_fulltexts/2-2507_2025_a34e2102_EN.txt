The Ilyichevsky District Court of Mariupol, Donetsk People's Republic, is processing a civil case based on an application from the administration of the city district of Mariupol, Donetsk People's Republic, to recognize the right of municipal ownership of an ownerless immovable property - an apartment located at the address:
<address>
.
By a court decision dated July 4, 2025, the demands of the administration of the Mariupol urban district to recognize the right of municipal ownership of ownerless property were satisfied.
By a court ruling dated October 7, 2025, the court decision dated July 4, 2025 was canceled in accordance with Art. 396 Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation at the request of an interested person
S.S.V.
The applicant's representative for the administration of the Mariupol urban district did not appear; he was notified of the day, time and place of the court hearing.
The representative of the interested party of the Rosreestr Office for the Donetsk People's Republic did not appear at the court hearing and was duly notified of the date, time and place of consideration of the civil case.
Interested person
S.S.V.
did not appear at the court hearing, sent an application to consider the case in her absence.
The case against the parties was considered in accordance with Art. 167 Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation.
Having examined the case materials, the court comes to the following conclusion.
According to paragraph 1 of Article 225 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, an ownerless thing is a thing that does not have an owner or the owner of which is unknown or, unless otherwise provided by law, the right of ownership to which the owner has refused.
According to paragraph 1 of Article 235 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, the right of ownership terminates when the owner alienates his property to other persons, the owner renounces the right of ownership, the destruction or destruction of property and when the right of ownership to property is lost in other cases provided for by law
The list of cases considered by the courts in special proceedings is indicated in Art. 262 Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation.
According to Part 3 of Art. 263 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation, if, when filing an application or considering a case in a special proceeding, the existence of a dispute about the right is established, the court issues a ruling to leave the application without consideration, in which it explains to the applicant and other interested parties their right to resolve the dispute in the manner of claim proceedings.
Accordingly, the court establishes facts of legal significance only if it is impossible for the applicant to otherwise obtain the appropriate documents certifying these facts, the impossibility of restoring lost documents (Article 265 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation) and in the absence of a dispute about the law within the jurisdiction of the court (Part 3 of Article 263 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation).
From the copies of title and other documents presented in the case materials, it follows that in relation to the disputed real estate object, which is the subject of the stated claims, ownership was declared by both the applicant and the interested party
S.S.V.
Under such circumstances, the court comes to the conclusion that it is necessary to leave the application without consideration.
Based on the above and guided by Art. Art. 225, 263 Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation, court
defined:
the application of the administration of the city district of Mariupol of the Donetsk People's Republic on the recognition of the right of municipal ownership of ownerless property shall be left without consideration.
Explain that the applicant has the right to apply to the court to resolve the dispute through litigation.
A private complaint against a court ruling may be filed within 15 days to the Supreme Court of the Donetsk People's Republic through the Ilyichevsky District Court of Mariupol, Donetsk People's Republic from the date of its issuance.
