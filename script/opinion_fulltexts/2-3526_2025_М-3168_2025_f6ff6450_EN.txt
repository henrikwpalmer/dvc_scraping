By virtue of the requirements of Part 1 of Article 3 of the Civil Procedure Code of the Russian Federation, an interested person has the right, in the manner established by the legislation on civil proceedings, to apply to the court for the protection of violated or disputed rights, freedoms or legitimate interests.
According to paragraph 6 of part 1 of Article 262 of the Civil Procedure Code of the Russian Federation, the court, in special proceedings, considers cases of recognizing a movable thing as ownerless and recognizing the right of municipal ownership of an ownerless immovable thing.
Part 2 of Article 290 of the Civil Procedure Code of the Russian Federation provides that an application for recognition of the right of municipal ownership of an ownerless immovable property is submitted to the court at its location by the body authorized to manage municipal property.
Article 292 of the Civil Procedure Code of the Russian Federation establishes that the judge, when preparing a case for trial, finds out which persons (owners, actual owners and others) can provide information about the ownership of property, and also requests relevant organizations about the information available about it.
An application to recognize a thing as ownerless or to recognize the right of municipal ownership of an ownerless immovable thing is considered by the court with the participation of interested parties. By virtue of Part 2 of Article 293 of the Civil Procedure Code of the Russian Federation, the court, having recognized that the immovable thing does not have an owner or the owner of the immovable thing is unknown and it has been registered in the prescribed manner, makes a decision to recognize the right of municipal property or the property of a federal city of Moscow, St. Petersburg or Sevastopol to this thing. According to paragraphs 1 and 3 of Article 225 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, an ownerless thing is one that does not have an owner or the owner of which is unknown or, unless otherwise provided by law, the right of ownership to which the owner has renounced. An ownerless immovable property, not recognized by a court decision as having come into municipal ownership, may be again taken into possession, use and disposal by the owner who abandoned it, or acquired into ownership by virtue of acquisitive prescription.
By virtue of Article 236 of the Civil Code, a citizen or legal entity may renounce ownership of property owned by him by declaring this or taking other actions that definitely indicate his removal from ownership, use and disposal of property without the intention of retaining any rights to this property.
From the content of these norms it follows that the current legislation, while providing for the possibility of terminating the right of ownership of a particular property by the owner performing actions indicating his renunciation of his ownership right, allows for the possibility of acquiring the right of ownership of the same property by another person.
At the same time, actions indicating the owner’s renunciation of the right of ownership may include, among other things, the removal of the owner from the possession and use of property belonging to him, and failure to take measures to maintain this property.
The method of acquiring municipal ownership rights to real estate objects, provided for in Articles 218, 225 of the Civil Code, is possible in the absence of legal claims to the object, as well as the actual possession of this property by any person.
