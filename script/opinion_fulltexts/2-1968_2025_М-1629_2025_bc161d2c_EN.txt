Recognition of ownership of an ownerless immovable property is carried out in a special proceeding upon the application of the body authorized to manage municipal property, in accordance with Articles 290-293 of the Civil Procedure Code of the Russian Federation.
By virtue of Part 3 of Article 263 of the Civil Procedure Code of the Russian Federation, if, when filing an application or considering a case in a special proceeding, it is established that there is a dispute about the right within the jurisdiction of the court, the court issues a ruling to leave the application without consideration, in which it explains to the applicant and other interested parties their right to resolve the dispute in the manner of litigation.
According to the meaning of these norms, the specified application is considered in special proceedings, when there is no need to resolve the dispute about the subjective right itself.
According to paragraphs 1 and 3 of Article 225 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, an ownerless thing is a thing that does not have an owner or the owner of which is unknown or, unless otherwise provided by law, the right of ownership to which the owner has refused. If this is not excluded by the rules of this Code on the acquisition of ownership of things that the owner has abandoned (Article 226), on finds (Articles 227 and 228), on stray animals (Articles 230 and 231) and treasure (Article 233), the right of ownership of ownerless movable things can be acquired by virtue of acquisitive prescription.

According to Article 1 of the Law

This Law applies to residential premises in the form of houses, apartments, and rooms.
Article 2 of the Law
№
-RZ stipulates that the signs of ownerless property in relation to residential premises are:
1) failure to pay for residential premises and utilities within one year before the day of identification of residential premises that have signs of ownerlessness;
2) lack of information about the registered ownership of residential premises in the Unified State Register of Real Estate;
3) non-use of residential premises, which, among other things, creates a threat to its safety (or an apartment building if the premises is an apartment, a room in an apartment building), safety for the life and health of other persons (including other rights holders in an apartment building if the premises is an apartment, a room in an apartment building).
According to Article 3 of the Law
№
-RZ local government bodies of the Donetsk People's Republic, represented by bodies authorized by them (hereinafter referred to as authorized bodies), independently carry out actions to identify, record and accept residential premises that have signs of ownerless property into the municipal property of the municipalities of the Donetsk People's Republic, on the territory of which they are located, on the grounds provided for by law.
In accordance with Part 1 of Article 4 of the Law
№
-RZ information about residential premises that have signs of ownerlessness may come from individuals and legal entities, including public authorities, who have become aware of such property.
According to paragraphs 1, 2 of Article 5 of the Law
№
- The RZ authorized body, in connection with the information received in accordance with Article 4 of this Law, requests information on the official website of the Federal Tax Service of the Russian Federation - about the availability in the Unified State Register of Legal Entities of information about a legal entity that is a possible owner of a residential premises (if it is necessary to establish the status of a legal entity); sends appropriate requests to confirm information about identified residential premises that have signs of ownerlessness.
Clause 3 of Article 5 of the Law
№
-RZ stipulates that within 10 calendar days from the date of discovery of a residential premises that has signs of being ownerless, an announcement is placed on the official website of the local government body on the Internet, as well as in accessible places at the entrance to the premises, about the identification of a residential premises that has an ownerless property, and about the need for personal appearance before the authorized body of the person (persons) who considers themselves its owner or has (have) rights to it, within 30 calendar days from the date of placement of such an advertisement with the following documents:
a) a passport of a citizen of the Russian Federation or other identification document;
b) title documents for residential premises.
Clause 4 of Article 5 of the Law
№
-RZ provides that after 30 calendar days from the date of publication of the announcement, in the event of the absence of a person (persons) who considers themselves the owner of the residential premises, and after receiving information from the website (if necessary, obtaining information) specified in paragraph 1 of this article, responses from the authorities specified in paragraph 2 of this article, indicating that the residential premises, which has signs of ownerless property, do not have an owner:
a) within 5 working days, draws up a report on the impossibility of identifying the owner of this residential premises;
b) organizes, if necessary, in the prescribed manner, the carrying out of cadastral work in relation to residential premises that have signs of being ownerless, as well as assigning a postal address to it;
c) if necessary, collects other documents confirming the presence of signs of ownerless property in the residential premises, including documents indicating the absence of residents in such residential premises (inspection reports, extracts from house books, personal accounts, etc.);
d) organizes a commission inspection of a residential premises that has signs of ownerlessness (with an on-site visit), with the involvement of the necessary representatives of the executive bodies of the Donetsk People's Republic and territorial bodies of the federal executive authorities, organizations (as agreed) and with the drawing up of an appropriate act, which indicates the details of the act, the date of the inspection, information about the persons involved, the location, technical and other condition of the residential premises, an inventory of furniture, household appliances and other movable property located in the residential premises, as well as other information indicating the presence of signs of ownerlessness of the residential premises
