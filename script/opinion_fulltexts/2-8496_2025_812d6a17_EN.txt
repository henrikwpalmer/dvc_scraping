In accordance with paragraph 6 of part 1 of Article 262 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation, cases considered by a court of general jurisdiction in special proceedings include cases of recognition of a movable thing as ownerless and recognition of the right of municipal ownership of an ownerless immovable thing.
According to Art. 1110 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, upon inheritance, the property of the deceased (inheritance, hereditary property) passes to other persons in the order of universal succession, that is, unchanged as a single whole and at the same moment, unless otherwise follows from the rules of this Code.
According to paragraph 2 of Art. 1152 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, acceptance by an heir of a part of the inheritance means acceptance of the entire inheritance due to him, no matter what it is and no matter where it is located.
In accordance with paragraph 34 of the Resolution of the Plenum of the Supreme Court of the Russian Federation dated
DD.MM.YYYY
№
“On judicial practice in inheritance cases”, the heir who accepted the inheritance, regardless of the time and method of its acceptance, is considered the owner of the inherited property, the bearer of property rights and obligations from the date of opening of the inheritance, regardless of the fact of state registration of rights to the inherited property and its moment.
By virtue of Part 3 of Art. 263 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation, if, when filing an application or considering a case in a special proceeding, it is established that there is a dispute about the right within the jurisdiction of the court, the court issues a ruling to leave the application without consideration, in which it explains to the applicant and other interested parties their right to resolve the dispute in the manner of adjudication proceedings.
According to the meaning of these norms, the specified application is considered in special proceedings, when there is no need to resolve the dispute about the subjective right itself.
