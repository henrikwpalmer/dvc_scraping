Paragraph 3 of Article 218 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation (hereinafter referred to as the Civil Code of the Russian Federation) establishes that in cases and in the manner provided for by the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, a person may acquire ownership rights to property that does not have an owner, to property whose owner is unknown, or to property that the owner has refused or to which he has lost ownership on other grounds provided by law.
In accordance with Article 225 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, an ownerless thing is a thing that does not have an owner or the owner of which is unknown or, unless otherwise provided by law, the right of ownership to which the owner has refused.
Ownerless real estate is accepted for registration by the body that carries out state registration of rights to real estate, upon application from the local government body in whose territory they are located. After a year has passed from the date of registration of an ownerless immovable property, the body authorized to manage municipal property may apply to the court with a request to recognize the right of municipal ownership of this thing.

Based on Part 3 of Article 1 of the Federal Law of the Russian Federation dated
DD.MM.YYYY
No. 218-FZ “On State Registration of Real Estate” (hereinafter referred to as Law No. 218-FZ), state registration of rights to real estate is a legal act of recognition and confirmation of the emergence, change, transition, termination of the right of a certain person to real estate or restrictions on such a right and encumbrance of real estate (hereinafter referred to as state registration of rights).
In accordance with Part 3 of Article 3 of Law No. 218-FZ, the competence of the rights registration authority when carrying out state cadastral registration and state registration of rights includes, among other things:
1) checking the validity of the documents submitted by the applicant and the availability of the corresponding rights of the person or authority who prepared the document;
2) checking the presence of previously registered and previously declared rights;
3) registration of ownerless real estate in the manner established by the regulatory body.
Thus, by placing real estate at the address:
<address>
to be registered as ownerless, the territorial body of the federal executive body carrying out state cadastral registration, state registration of rights, maintaining the Unified State Register of Real Estate, confirms the fact that state registration as ownerless was carried out lawfully.
When considering the issue of recognizing the right of municipal ownership of ownerless real estate, the court also takes into account that, by virtue of Art. 210 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, the owner bears the burden of maintaining the property belonging to him, unless otherwise provided by law or contract.
The Constitutional Court of the Russian Federation in paragraph 3.1 of the ruling dated
DD.MM.YYYY
№
explained that this obligation includes, among other things, the incurrence of expenses for maintaining the property in proper condition (major and current repairs, insurance, registration, security, etc.) and the fulfillment of other requirements emanating from state and local government bodies authorized by law.
According to Art. 12 Federal Law dated
DD.MM.YYYY
N 390-FZ “On Security”, local government bodies, within their competence, ensure the implementation of the legislation of the Russian Federation in the field of security.
According to paragraph 5 of Section I of Procedure No. I/32-4, the main goals and objectives of identifying ownerless real estate objects and registering municipal ownership rights to them are: involving unused property in free civil circulation; ensuring normal and safe technical operation of the property; improvement and proper maintenance of the territory of the Donetsk urban district.
