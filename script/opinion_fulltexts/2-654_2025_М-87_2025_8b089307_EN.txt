By virtue of Art. 209 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, the owner has the rights to own, use and dispose of his property.
The subject of a claim for recognition of a right is a statement of the fact that the subject has a proprietary right to property, and the basis for the claim is the circumstances confirming that the plaintiff has such a right. The use of this method of protection requires proof of the legal basis for the emergence of the right of ownership of the specified property by the person applying to the court.
According to Part 3 of Article 218 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, in the cases and in the manner provided for by this Code, a person may acquire ownership of property that does not have an owner, property whose owner is unknown, or property that the owner has abandoned or to which he has lost ownership on other grounds provided by law.
In accordance with Part 1, Part 3 of Article 225 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, an ownerless thing is one that does not have an owner or the owner of which is unknown, or, unless otherwise provided by law, the owner of which has renounced the right of ownership. Ownerless real estate is accepted for registration by the body that carries out state registration of rights to real estate, upon application from the local government body in whose territory they are located.
In accordance with Part 1 of Article 4 of the Federal Constitutional Law of October 4, 2022 No. 5-FKZ “On the admission of the Donetsk People's Republic into the Russian Federation and the formation of a new subject within the Russian Federation - the Donetsk People's Republic”, legislative and other regulatory legal acts of the Russian Federation are in force on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic from the date of admission of the Donetsk People's Republic into the Russian Federation and the formation of a new entity within the Russian Federation subject, unless otherwise provided by this Federal Constitutional Law.
Regulatory legal acts of the Donetsk People's Republic are valid until the end of the transition period or until the adoption of the corresponding regulatory legal act of the Russian Federation and (or) the regulatory legal act of the Donetsk People's Republic.
According to Part 7 of Article 12 of the Federal Constitutional Law of October 4, 2022 No. 5-FKZ “On the admission of the Donetsk People’s Republic to the Russian Federation and the formation of a new subject within the Russian Federation - the Donetsk People’s Republic”, management of property located on the territory of the Donetsk People’s Republic, which has signs of ownerlessness and is not classified as federal property, the property of a constituent entity of the Russian Federation or municipal property, is carried out by the authorities of the Donetsk of the People's Republic in the manner established by the Government of the Russian Federation, unless otherwise provided by this Federal Constitutional Law. The procedure for recognizing such property as ownerless, established by the regulatory legal acts of the Donetsk People's Republic, is applied until January 1, 2028, in agreement with the federal executive body performing the functions of managing state property.
Decree of the Head of the Donetsk People's Republic dated April 28, 2021 No. 116 established the Standard Procedure for identifying, recording and accepting ownerless real estate and escheatable property into municipal ownership (hereinafter referred to as the Standard Procedure).

According to clause 4.4 of Article 1 of Federal Law No. 218-FZ of July 13, 2015 “On State Registration of Real Estate,” state registration of rights is carried out by entering into the Unified State Register of Real Estate an entry about the right to real estate, information about which is included in the Unified State Register of Real Estate. State registration of the transfer of the right to a real estate object, restriction of the right to a real estate object, encumbrance of a real estate object or a transaction with a real estate object is carried out subject to the existence of state registration of the right to this object in the Unified State Register of Real Estate, unless otherwise established by Federal Law.
The grounds for the implementation of state cadastral registration and (or) state registration of rights are, in particular, acts issued by state authorities or local government bodies within their competence and in the manner established by the legislation in force at the place of publication of such acts at the time of their publication, and establishing the existence, emergence, transfer, termination of a right or restriction of a right and encumbrance of a property (clause 1, part 2, article 14 of Law 218-FZ dated July 13, 2015).

According to the provisions of Art. Art. 55, 56, 67 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation, evidence in the case is information obtained in the manner prescribed by law about the facts, on the basis of which the court establishes the presence or absence of circumstances justifying the demands and objections of the parties, as well as other circumstances that are important for the correct consideration and resolution of the case. Each party must prove the circumstances to which it refers as the basis for its claims and objections.
