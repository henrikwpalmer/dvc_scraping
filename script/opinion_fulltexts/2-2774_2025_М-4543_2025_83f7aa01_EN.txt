Chapter 33 of the Civil Procedure Code of the Russian Federation regulates the procedure for considering cases of recognizing a movable thing as ownerless or recognizing ownership of an ownerless immovable thing.
According to Art. 12 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation (hereinafter referred to as the Civil Code of the Russian Federation), the protection of civil rights is carried out through the recognition of rights.
In accordance with Art. 210 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, the owner bears the burden of maintaining the property belonging to him, unless otherwise provided by law or contract.
In accordance with paragraph 3 of Art. 218 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, in cases and in the manner provided for by the Code, a person may acquire ownership of property that does not have an owner, property whose owner is unknown, or property that the owner has abandoned or to which he has lost ownership on other grounds provided by law.
According to paragraph 1 of Art. 225 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, ownerless is a thing that does not have an owner or the owner of which is unknown or, unless otherwise provided by law, a property that the owner has refused.
Based on the provisions of paragraph 3 of Art. 225 of the said Code, ownerless immovable things are accepted for registration by the body carrying out state registration of rights to real estate, upon application from the local government body in whose territory they are located. After a year has passed from the date of registration of an ownerless immovable property, the body authorized to manage municipal property may apply to the court with a request to recognize the right of municipal ownership of this thing. An ownerless immovable property, not recognized by a court decision as having come into municipal ownership, may be again taken into possession, use and disposal by the owner who abandoned it, or acquired into ownership by virtue of acquisitive prescription.
According to paragraphs 1 and 2 of Art. 130 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, immovable things (real estate, real estate) include land plots, subsoil plots and everything that is firmly connected to the land, that is, objects whose movement without disproportionate damage to their purpose is impossible, including buildings, structures, unfinished construction objects. Items not related to real estate, including money and securities, are recognized as movable property.
Recognition of ownership of an ownerless immovable property is carried out in a special proceeding, upon application of the body authorized to manage municipal property, in accordance with Art. Art. 290-293 Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation.
By virtue of Part 3 of Art. 263 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation, if, when filing an application or considering a case in a special proceeding, it is established that there is a dispute about the right within the jurisdiction of the court, the court issues a ruling to leave the application without consideration, in which it explains to the applicant and other interested parties their right to resolve the dispute in the manner of claim proceedings.
According to the meaning of these norms, the specified application is considered in special proceedings, when there is no need to resolve the dispute about the subjective right itself.
In accordance with Part 1 of Art. 4 of the Federal Constitutional Law of October 4, 2022 No. 5-FKZ “On the admission of the Donetsk People's Republic into the Russian Federation and the formation of a new subject within the Russian Federation - the Donetsk People's Republic” (hereinafter referred to as Law No. 5-FKZ) legislative and other regulatory legal acts of the Russian Federation are valid on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic from the date of admission to the Russian Federation of the Donetsk People's Republic and the formation of of the Russian Federation of a new subject, unless otherwise provided by Law No. 5 of the Federal Law on Laws.
According to Part 2 of Art. 4 of Law No. 5-FKZ, the regulatory legal acts of the Donetsk People's Republic are valid on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic until the end of the transition period or until the adoption of the relevant regulatory legal act of the Russian Federation and (or) the regulatory legal act of the Donetsk People's Republic.
At the same time, Art. 21 of Law No. 5-FKZ establishes the specifics of regulating relations in the sphere of real estate turnover on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic.
In accordance with and. 2 hours 1 tbsp. 21 of Law No. 5-FKZ, until January 01, 2028, on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic, regulatory legal acts of the Donetsk People's Republic may establish features of the regulation of property relations, including the circulation of land, in agreement with the federal executive body performing the functions of managing state property.
By virtue of Part 7 of Art. 12 of Law No. 5-FKZ, the procedure for recognizing property that is not classified as federal property, the property of a constituent entity of the Russian Federation or municipal property as ownerless, established by the regulatory legal acts of the Donetsk People's Republic, is applied until January 1, 2028 in agreement with the federal executive body performing the functions of managing state property.
In pursuance of the above-mentioned federal constitutional law, on May 10, 2023, the Acting Head of the Donetsk People’s Republic signed Decree No. 140 “On the specifics of acquiring the right of state and municipal property to ownerless real estate on the territory of the Donetsk People’s Republic,” according to which the body and officials of the Donetsk People’s Republic exercising the powers classified by the legislation of the Russian Federation as the powers of local self-government bodies, until the completion of the formation of local authorities self-government, a local government body of a municipal formation in the Donetsk People's Republic, authorized to manage municipal property, has the right to go to court with a demand for recognition of the right of municipal ownership of ownerless buildings, structures (except for linear objects), premises, objects of unfinished construction that do not have an owner or the owner of which is unknown or the owner of which has refused ownership, located on the territory of such a municipal formation, after three months from the date their registration as ownerless real estate by the body carrying out state registration of rights to real estate, unless otherwise provided by this Decree /clause 1.1/.

According to paragraphs 1 and 3 of Art. 225 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, ownerless is a thing that does not have an owner or the owner of which is unknown or, unless otherwise provided by law, the right of ownership to which the owner has refused. If this is not excluded by the rules of this Code on the acquisition of ownership of things that the owner has abandoned (Article 226), on finds (Articles 227 and 228), on stray animals (Articles 230 and 231) and treasure (Article 233), the right of ownership of ownerless movable things can be acquired by virtue of acquisitive prescription.
By virtue of the provisions of Art. 236 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, a citizen or legal entity may renounce the right of ownership of property belonging to him by declaring this or taking other actions that definitely indicate his removal from ownership, use and disposal of property without the intention of retaining any rights to this property.
From the content of these norms it follows that the current legislation provides for the possibility of terminating the right of ownership of a particular property by the owner performing actions indicating his renunciation of his property right.
At the same time, actions indicating the owner’s renunciation of the right of ownership may include, among other things, the removal of the owner from the possession and use of property belonging to him, and failure to take measures to maintain this property.
Clause 1.3.3 of the standard procedure for identifying, recording and accepting into municipal ownership ownerless real estate and escheat property, approved by Decree of the Head of the Donetsk People's Republic No. 116 of April 28, 2021, establishes that an individual or legal entity can renounce the right of ownership of property belonging to him by declaring this or taking other actions that definitely indicate his removal from ownership and use and disposal of property without the intention of retaining any rights to this property, including by ceasing to bear the burden of maintaining such property (in particular through non-payment of utilities for the last three years), provided that the owner has been outside the territory of the Russian Federation for the last six months.
By virtue of clause 3.2 of this Procedure, persons who consider themselves the owners of real estate must prove ownership of such a thing in court.
From the cumulative analysis of the above legal norms, it follows that a person who was the owner of real estate, in the event of failure to provide documents confirming his ownership of the said property in the manner and within the time limits established by law, loses such right to the disputed property, and the existence of this right must be established by a court decision.
