Thus, the court considers it possible to consider the case in the absence of these persons, in accordance with the provisions of Art. 167 Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation.
Having examined the case materials, the court found the following.
In accordance with Art. 55 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation, evidence in the case is information obtained in the manner prescribed by law about the facts, on the basis of which the court establishes the presence or absence of circumstances justifying the demands and objections of the parties, as well as other circumstances that are important for the correct consideration and resolution of the case. Evidence obtained in violation of the law has no legal force and cannot be used as the basis for a court decision.
Article 56 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation determines that each party must prove the circumstances to which it refers as the basis for its claims and objections, unless otherwise provided by federal law. Each person participating in the case must disclose the evidence to which he refers as the basis for his claims and objections to other persons participating in the case, within the period established by the court, unless otherwise established by this Code.
In accordance with Article 225 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation (hereinafter referred to as the Civil Code of the Russian Federation), an ownerless thing is a thing that does not have an owner or the owner of which is unknown or, unless otherwise provided by law, the right of ownership to which the owner has refused.
Paragraph 3 of Article 218 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation establishes that in cases and in the manner provided for by this Code, a person may acquire the right of ownership to property that does not have an owner, to property whose owner is unknown, or to property that the owner has abandoned or to which he has lost the right of ownership on other grounds provided by law.
In accordance with Part 2 of Article 4, Part 7 of Article 12 of the Federal Constitutional Law of October 4, 2022 No. 5-FKZ “On the admission of the Donetsk People's Republic to the Russian Federation and the formation of a new subject within the Russian Federation - the Donetsk People's Republic”, regulatory legal acts of the Donetsk People's Republic are valid on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic until the end of the transition period or until the adoption of the corresponding regulatory legal act of the Russian Federation and (or) normative legal act of the Donetsk People's Republic.
Management of property located on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic, which has signs of ownerlessness and is not classified as federal property, the property of a constituent entity of the Russian Federation or municipal property, is carried out by the authorities of the Donetsk People's Republic in the manner established by the Government of the Russian Federation, unless otherwise provided by this Federal Constitutional Law.
According to the Charter of the municipal formation of the Donetsk city district of the Donetsk People's Republic, adopted by the decision of the Donetsk city council of the Donetsk People's Republic dated November 09, 2023 No. I/5-1, issues of local importance of the urban district include, among other things, the ownership, use and disposal of property in municipal ownership, the implementation of municipal housing control.
The procedure for recognizing such property as ownerless, established by the regulatory legal acts of the Donetsk People's Republic, applies until 01/01/2028, in agreement with the federal executive body performing the functions of managing state property.
In accordance with subparagraph 3.1 of paragraph 3 of the Decree of the Acting Head of the Donetsk People's Republic dated 02.27.2023 No. 64 “On some issues related to the management of property that has signs of ownerlessness, located on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic, and the recognition of such property as ownerless” (hereinafter referred to as Decree No. 64), administrations of cities and districts are bodies for managing property that has signs of ownerlessness ownerless and not classified as federal property, located on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic, in accordance with the Rules approved by the Decree of the Government of the Russian Federation of December 28, 2022 No. 2474 “On approval of the Rules for the management of bodies of the Donetsk People's Republic, Lugansk People's Republic, Zaporozhye region and Kherson region having signs of ownerlessness and not classified as federal property, property subject of the Russian Federation or municipal property with property located on the territories of the specified subjects of the Russian Federation.”
Subclause 3.2 of clause 3 of Decree No. 64 determines that until 01/01/2028, in relation to the property specified in subclause 3.1 of clause 3, the procedure for recognizing it as ownerless is applied, established by Decree of the Head of the Donetsk People's Republic of 04/28/2021 No. 116 “On identifying, recording and accepting ownerless property into municipal ownership.” real estate and escheat property" (hereinafter referred to as Standard Procedure No. 116), taking into account the changes introduced by Decree No. 64.
In accordance with the decision of the Donetsk City Council of the Donetsk People's Republic dated November 30, 2023 No. I/8-3 “On issues of legal succession in the municipal formation of the Donetsk City District of the Donetsk People's Republic”, when performing the functions of managing property that has signs of ownerlessness, the administration of the Donetsk City District performs the functions of identifying, recording and accepting ownerless things into municipal ownership in accordance with the requirements of the Standard Procedure No. 116.
The procedure for identifying, registering, storing and accepting into municipal ownership of the territorial community of the city of Donetsk on the territory of the city of Donetsk is determined by the Procedure for identifying, registering and accepting ownerless real estate and escheat into municipal ownership of the territorial community of the city of Donetsk, approved by the Order of the head of the administration of Donetsk dated June 22, 2021 No. 1370 (hereinafter referred to as Procedure No. 1370).
Clause 1.1 of Procedure No. 1370, which was developed in accordance with the Civil Code of the Russian Federation and in pursuance of paragraph 2 of the Decree of the Head of the Donetsk People's Republic of April 28, 2021 No. 116 “On the identification, registration and acceptance into municipal ownership of ownerless real estate and escheat property”, defines the procedure for identifying, registering, storing and accepting into municipal ownership the territorial community of the city of Donetsk ownerless real estate and escheat property identified on the territory of the city of Donetsk.
According to clause 2.1 of section II of the Procedure for identifying, recording and accepting ownerless real estate residential property into municipal ownership of the municipality of the Donetsk city district of the Donetsk People's Republic, approved by the decision of the Donetsk city council of the Donetsk People's Republic dated 07/04/24 No. I/32-5 (hereinafter referred to as Procedure No. I/32-5), the administration of the Donetsk city district of the Donetsk People's Republic represented by the department municipal property administration of the Donetsk urban district (hereinafter referred to as the municipal property department) independently carries out actions to identify, record and accept ownerless real estate located on the territory of the Donetsk urban district into the municipal property of the Donetsk urban district. Information about residential premises that have signs of ownerlessness may be received by the municipal property department from individuals and legal entities, including public authorities, who have become aware of such property.
According to clause 2.2 of Procedure No. I/32-5, the signs of ownerless real estate in relation to residential premises are: failure to pay for residential premises and utilities within one year before the date of identification of residential premises that have signs of ownerlessness; lack of information about the registered ownership of residential premises in the Unified State Register of Real Estate; non-use of residential premises, which, among other things, creates a threat to its safety (or an apartment building if the premises is an apartment, a room in an apartment building), safety for the life and health of other persons (including the rights holders of other premises in an apartment building if the premises is an apartment, a room in an apartment building).
