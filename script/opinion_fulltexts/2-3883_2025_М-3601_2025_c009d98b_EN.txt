Having examined the case materials and assessed the evidence presented in its entirety, the court comes to the following conclusion.
By virtue of the provisions of Art. 123 of the Constitution of the Russian Federation and Art. 12, 56 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation, justice in civil cases is carried out on the basis of adversarial and equal rights of the parties, each party must prove the circumstances to which it refers as the basis for its claims and objections, unless otherwise provided by federal law.
Evidence is presented by the parties and other persons participating in the case, based on the provisions of Art. 57 Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation.
According to paragraph 1 of Art. 225 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, ownerless is a thing that does not have an owner or an owner who is unknown or, unless otherwise provided by law, a property right to which the owner has refused.
Ownerless real estate is accepted for registration by the body that carries out state registration of rights to real estate, upon application from the local government body in whose territory they are located.
After a year has passed from the date of registration of an ownerless immovable property, the body authorized to manage municipal property may apply to the court with a request to recognize the right of municipal ownership of this thing (clause 3 of Article 225 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation).
According to Art. 236 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, a citizen or legal entity may renounce the right of ownership of property belonging to him by declaring this or taking other actions that definitely indicate his removal from ownership, use and disposal of property without the intention of retaining any rights to this property.
In accordance with clause 6, part 1, art. 262 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation, cases of recognizing a movable thing as ownerless and recognizing the right of municipal ownership of an ownerless thing are considered by a court of general jurisdiction in accordance with special proceedings.
In accordance with Part 3 of Art. 263 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation, if, when filing an application or considering a case in a special proceeding, it is established that there is a dispute about the right within the jurisdiction of the court, the court issues a ruling to leave the application without consideration, in which it explains to the applicant and other interested parties their right to resolve the dispute in the manner of claim proceedings.
From the above norms in their totality it follows that ownerless is an abandoned, abandoned thing that no one actually owns and which no one claims. The lack of ownership rights in itself does not mean that the thing is ownerless. Recognition of ownership of an ownerless thing in a special proceeding is impossible if other persons have legal claims to this thing, since in this case a dispute inevitably arises about the right of these persons to this thing.
However, the Regional Public Organization "Voluntary Society for Assistance to the Army, Aviation and Navy" of the Donetsk People's Republic considers itself the legal owner of the disputed property, citing the decision of the Arbitration Court of the Donetsk Region dated 04/08/2011 in case No. 12/252 mon.
Thus, in the case there is a dispute about the right in relation to the disputed property, therefore, there are no grounds for considering the application under the rules of special proceedings.
In relation to an application for recognition of the right of municipal ownership of an ownerless immovable thing (Part 1 of Article 290 of the Civil Procedure Code of the Russian Federation), the absence of a dispute about the right presupposes the absence of claims of other persons to the same immovable thing.
Under such circumstances, the statement of the Administration of G.O. Makeyevka should be left without consideration.
The applicant is not deprived of the right to apply to court with these demands and resolve the dispute through litigation.
