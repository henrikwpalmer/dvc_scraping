The court, having examined the evidence provided, found the following.
According to clause 6, part 1, art. 262 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation, by way of special proceedings, the court considers cases on recognition of the right of municipal ownership of an ownerless immovable property.
According to Part 1 of Art. 225 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, ownerless is a thing that does not have an owner or the owner of which is unknown or, unless otherwise provided by law, the owner of which has renounced the right of ownership.
Clause 3 of Art. 218 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation provides that a person can acquire ownership of property that does not have an owner, property whose owner is unknown, or property that the owner has abandoned or for which he has lost ownership on other grounds provided by law.
According to Part 3 of Art. 225 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, ownerless real estate is accepted for registration by the body that carries out state registration of rights to real estate, upon application from the local government body in whose territory they are located.
After a year has passed from the date of registration of an ownerless immovable property, the body authorized to manage municipal property may apply to the court with a request to recognize the right of municipal ownership of this thing.
An ownerless immovable property, not recognized by a court decision as having come into municipal ownership, may be again taken into possession, use and disposal by the owner who abandoned it, or acquired into ownership by virtue of acquisitive prescription.
Meanwhile, in accordance with Part 1 of Art. 4 of the Federal Constitutional Law of October 4, 2022 No. 5-FKZ “On the admission of the Donetsk People's Republic into the Russian Federation and the formation of a new subject within the Russian Federation - the Donetsk People's Republic” (hereinafter referred to as Law No. 5-FKZ), legislative and other regulatory legal acts of the Russian Federation are valid on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic from the date of admission of the Donetsk People's Republic into the Russian Federation and the formation of of the Russian Federation of a new subject, unless otherwise provided by Law No. 5-FKZ.
According to Part 2 of Art. 4 of Law No. 5-FKZ, the regulatory legal acts of the Donetsk People's Republic are valid on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic until the end of the transition period or until the adoption of the relevant regulatory legal act of the Russian Federation and (or) the regulatory legal act of the Donetsk People's Republic.
Article 21 of Law No. 5-FKZ establishes the specifics of regulating relations in the sphere of real estate turnover on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic.

A power of attorney is a written authority issued by one person to another person or other persons for representation before third parties. As a general rule, it must contain: the name of the document (power of attorney), the place and date of its execution, information about the represented and the representative, the powers of the latter and the signature of the represented.
A power of attorney executed abroad of the Russian Federation and certified by an authorized person of a foreign state must be legalized by a consular officer or by affixing an apostille. A power of attorney and apostille drawn up in a foreign language must be accompanied by a translation into Russian, the accuracy of which is certified by a notary who speaks the appropriate language. If the notary does not speak the appropriate language, the translation can be made by a translator, whose authenticity of signature is certified by the notary.
The submitted power of attorney does not comply with the requirements of the current legislation on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic and, accordingly, on the territory of the Russian Federation, and therefore cannot be taken into account by the court.
Thus, the court found that no one owns the specified real estate property, no one declared rights to this real estate property within three months from the date of registration as ownerless property, from October 2, 2024, there are no owners of this real estate property.
Having analyzed the legally significant circumstances in this case and examined the evidence presented in its entirety, the court comes to a reliable conclusion that in relation to the declared property at the court hearing there are no contradictions between the declared rights and already registered rights.
From the written evidence available in the case, it follows that within three months from the date of registration of this property as ownerless, its copyright holder has not been established, restrictions (encumbrances) of the right, legal claims declared in court have not been established.
Based on the established circumstances in this case and, guided by the requirements of the above legal norms of legislation regulating the issues of recognition of ownership of a real estate property registered as ownerless, the court found that the property indicated by the applicant is located within the boundaries of the administration of the Amvrosievsky Municipal District, the owner of this property is unknown, the established period from the date of registration of this property by the body carrying out state registration has expired, the legal status of the property at the time of consideration of the case has not changed.
Considering the lack of registered ownership of the specified real estate object, the absence of claims of individuals or legal entities regarding the property that is the subject of the application, the court comes to the conclusion that the stated claims are justified and the need to satisfy them.
