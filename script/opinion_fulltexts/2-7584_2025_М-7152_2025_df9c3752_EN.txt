According to clause 6, part 1, art. 262 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation, in a special proceeding, the court considers cases of recognizing a movable thing as ownerless and recognizing the right of municipal ownership of an ownerless immovable thing.
According to Part 1 of Art. 263 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation, cases of special proceedings are considered and resolved by the court according to the general rules of claim proceedings with the features established by this chapter and Chapters 28 - 38 of this Code.
In accordance with Part 1 of Art. 225 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, ownerless is a thing that does not have an owner or the owner of which is unknown, or unless otherwise provided by law, the right of ownership to which the owner has refused.
In accordance with Part 3 of Art. 263 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation, if, when filing an application or considering a case in a special proceeding, it is established that there is a dispute about the right, the court issues a ruling to leave the application without consideration, in which it explains to the applicant and other interested parties their right to resolve the dispute in the manner of claim proceedings.

On September 30, 2022, an Agreement was concluded between the Russian Federation and the Donetsk People's Republic on the admission of the Donetsk People's Republic to the Russian Federation and the formation of a new entity within the Russian Federation.
According to paragraph 3 of Art. 1 of the Federal Constitutional Law of October 4, 2022 No. 5-FKZ “On the admission of the Donetsk People's Republic into the Russian Federation and the formation of a new subject within the Russian Federation - the Donetsk People's Republic”, the Donetsk People's Republic is considered accepted into the Russian Federation from the date of signing of the Agreement between the Russian Federation and the Donetsk People's Republic on the admission of the Donetsk People's Republic to the Russian Federation and the formation within the Russian Federation Federation of a new entity, the Donetsk People's Republic is considered accepted into the Russian Federation from the date of signing the Agreement between the Russian Federation and the Donetsk People's Republic on the admission of the Donetsk People's Republic to the Russian Federation and the formation of a new entity within the Russian Federation.
According to Part. 1, 2 tbsp. 4 of the Federal Constitutional Law of October 4, 2022 No. 5-FKZ “On the admission of the Donetsk People's Republic into the Russian Federation and the formation of a new subject within the Russian Federation - the Donetsk People's Republic”, legislative and other regulatory legal acts of the Russian Federation are valid on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic from the date of admission of the Donetsk People's Republic into the Russian Federation and the formation of a new entity within the Russian Federation, unless otherwise provided this Federal Constitutional Law. Regulatory legal acts of the Donetsk People's Republic are valid on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic until the end of the transition period or until the adoption of the relevant regulatory legal act of the Russian Federation and (or) the regulatory legal act of the Donetsk People's Republic.
Parts 1, 2 of Article 1 of the Law of the Donetsk People's Republic No. 270-IINS dated April 30, 2021, adopted by the Resolution of the People's Council on April 30, 2021, “On the conversion of property owned by banks of Ukraine into the ownership of the Donetsk People's Republic” (hereinafter referred to as DPR Law No. 270-IINS dated April 30, 2021) it is determined that this Law, in accordance with the Constitution of the Donetsk People's Republic, the Civil Code of the Donetsk People's Republic, determines the procedure and consequences of converting property owned by banks of Ukraine into the ownership of the Donetsk People's Republic in order to ensure state security of the Donetsk People's Republic, protect the rights and legitimate interests of citizens. For the purposes of this Law, banks of Ukraine mean banks that have received a license from the National Bank of Ukraine and carried out their activities in the Donetsk region of Ukraine as of May 14, 2014.
According to Part 2 of Art. 2 Law of the DPR No. 270-IINS dated April 30, 2021, the property of Ukrainian banks, including movable and immovable things, becomes the property of the Donetsk People's Republic.
At the court hearing, it was established that the specified real estate belonged to state property, the copyright holder was PJSC State Savings Bank of Ukraine, and taking into account the Law of the DPR No. 270-IINS dated April 30, 2021. became the property of the Donetsk People's Republic.
Thus, from the facts established during the court hearing, a dispute about the law is discerned.
