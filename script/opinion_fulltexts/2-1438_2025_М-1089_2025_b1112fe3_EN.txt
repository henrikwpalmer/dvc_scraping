Having examined and assessed the evidence collected in the case, the court considers that the stated requirements must be satisfied on the following grounds.
Article 2 of the Civil Procedure Code of the Russian Federation (hereinafter referred to as the Civil Procedure Code of the Russian Federation) determines that the objectives of civil proceedings are the correct and timely consideration and resolution of civil cases in order to protect the violated or disputed rights, freedoms and legitimate interests of citizens, organizations, rights and interests of the Russian Federation, constituent entities of the Russian Federation, municipalities, and other persons who are subjects of civil, labor or other legal relations. Civil proceedings should help strengthen law and order, prevent crime, and foster respect for the law and the court. In accordance with paragraph 6 of part 1 of Article 262 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation, in a special proceeding, the court considers cases of recognizing a movable thing as ownerless and recognizing the right of municipal ownership of an ownerless immovable thing. Article 292 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation determines that when preparing a case for trial, a judge finds out which persons (owners, actual owners and others) can provide information about the ownership of property, and also requests relevant organizations about the information available about it. An application to recognize a thing as ownerless or to recognize the right of ownership to an ownerless immovable thing is considered by the court with the participation of interested persons. By virtue of paragraph 1 of Article 225 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation (hereinafter referred to as the Civil Code of the Russian Federation), an ownerless thing is a thing that does not have an owner or the owner of which is unknown or, unless otherwise provided by law, the right of ownership to which the owner has refused. In accordance with paragraph 3 of Article 225 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, ownerless real estate is accepted for registration by the body that carries out state registration of rights to real estate, upon application from the local government body in whose territory they are located. After a year has passed from the date of registration of an ownerless immovable property, and in the case of registration of a linear object, after three months from the date of registration, the body authorized to manage municipal property may apply to the court with a request to recognize the right of municipal ownership of this thing.
An ownerless immovable property, not recognized by a court decision as having come into municipal ownership, may be again taken into possession, use and disposal by the owner who abandoned it, or acquired into ownership by virtue of acquisitive prescription.
Based on the content of Article 225 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, ownerlessness of real estate presupposes the absence or unknownness of its owner, and the condition for considering an application to recognize property as ownerless is the absence of a dispute about the right. It is these facts that are included in the subject of proof in this category of cases and must be established in court.
