Having heard the representative of the interested party of the administration of the city district of Mariupol of the Donetsk People's Republic, having examined the materials of the case, having assessed the evidence collected in the case in its entirety, the court comes to the following.
In accordance with Part 1 of Article 1 of the Federal Constitutional Law of October 4, 2022 No. 5-FKZ “On the admission of the Donetsk People's Republic to the Russian Federation and the formation of a new subject within the Russian Federation - the Donetsk People's Republic,” the Donetsk People's Republic is admitted to the Russian Federation.
By virtue of Part 7 of Article 12 of the Federal Constitutional Law of October 4, 2022 No. 5-FKZ “On the admission of the Donetsk People’s Republic into the Russian Federation and the formation of a new subject within the Russian Federation - the Donetsk People’s Republic,” management of property located on the territory of the Donetsk People’s Republic, which has signs of ownerlessness and is not classified as federal property, the property of a constituent entity of the Russian Federation or municipal property, is carried out by the authorities Donetsk People's Republic in the manner established by the Government of the Russian Federation, unless otherwise provided by this Federal Constitutional Law. The procedure for recognizing such property as ownerless, established by the regulatory legal acts of the Donetsk People's Republic, is applied until January 1, 2028, in agreement with the federal executive body exercising the functions of managing state property.
By Decree of the Acting Head of the Donetsk People's Republic of May 10, 2023. No. 140 establishes the specifics of acquiring the right of state municipal property to ownerless real estate on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic.
According to clause 1.2 of the Decree of May 10, 2023. No. 140, the executive body of the DPR, authorized to manage state property, has the right to apply to the court with a demand for recognition of the right of state ownership of the objects specified in subclause 1.1 of this clause (with the exception of residential premises, outbuildings intended to satisfy citizens' household and other needs), after three months from the date of their registration as ownerless real estate by the body carrying out state registration of the right to real estate.
According to the Regulations on the Ministry of Property and Land Relations of the DPR dated December 13, 2024 No. 695, the MIZO DPR is the executive body of the Donetsk People's Republic, developing and implementing the state policy of the Donetsk People's Republic in the field of property and land relations.
From the case materials it follows that on October 10, 2024, the Ministry of Youth, Sports and Tourism of the Donetsk People's Republic submitted an application to the State Property Fund of the Donetsk People's Republic to identify real estate that had signs of an ownerless thing, located at the address: Russian Federation, Donetsk People's Republic, Mariupol urban district, Mariupol city, Chernomorskaya street, house 6A.

The main activity is: the activities of government bodies of the constituent entities of the Russian Federation (republics, territories, regions), except for the judiciary, representative offices of executive bodies of the state authorities of the constituent entities of the Russian Federation under the President of the Russian Federation.

According to clause 12 of the Procedure, after three months from the date of registration with the authorized territorial body of the federal executive body carrying out state cadastral registration, state registration of rights, maintenance of the Unified State Register of Real Estate, as an ownerless real estate, with the exception of linear objects (including their integral technological parts), the DPR SPFU applies to the judicial authorities with an application for recognition of the right of state ownership of the ownerless property. immovable thing.
In accordance with Article 12 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation (hereinafter referred to as the Civil Code of the Russian Federation), the protection of civil rights is carried out through the recognition of rights.
In paragraph 59 of the Resolution of the Plenum of the Supreme Court of the Russian Federation No. 10, Plenum of the Supreme Arbitration Court of the Russian Federation No. 22 of April 29, 2010 “On some issues arising in judicial practice when resolving disputes related to the protection of property rights and other property rights” it is explained that, unless otherwise provided by law, a claim for recognition of a right is subject to satisfaction if the plaintiff presents evidence of the emergence of the corresponding right. A claim for recognition of rights filed by persons whose rights and transactions in relation to the disputed property have never been registered can be satisfied in cases where the rights to the disputed property arose before the entry into force of the Registration Law and were not registered in accordance with paragraphs 1 and 2 of Article 6 of the said Law, or arose regardless of their registration in accordance with paragraph 2 of Article 8 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation.
According to paragraph 3 of Article 218 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, in cases and in the manner provided for by this Code, a person may acquire ownership of property that does not have an owner, property whose owner is unknown, or property that the owner has abandoned or to which he has lost ownership on other grounds provided by law.
In accordance with Article 219 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, ownership of buildings, structures and other newly created real estate, subject to state registration, arises from the moment of such registration.
Paragraph 1 of Article 225 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation states that an ownerless thing is a thing that does not have an owner or the owner of which is unknown or, unless otherwise provided by law, the right of ownership to which the owner has renounced.
The procedure for recognizing the ownership of an ownerless immovable property by the body authorized to manage municipal property is defined in Chapter 33 of the Civil Procedure Code of the Russian Federation (hereinafter referred to as the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation).
Based on Part 2 of Article 293 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation, the court, having recognized that the immovable thing does not have an owner or the owner of the immovable thing is unknown, and it was registered in the prescribed manner, makes a decision to recognize the right of municipal property or the property of the federal city of Moscow or St. Petersburg to this thing.
Thus, based on the specified provisions of the regulatory legal acts, taking into account that the disputed object relates to real estate, the applicant complied with the pre-trial procedure, which consists of taking certain actions, namely, the ownerless real estate was registered with the body that carries out state registration of rights to real estate, and was registered for more than three months, the court comes to the conclusion that the application is satisfied,
