Clause 1.1. Decree of the Head of the Donetsk People's Republic No. 140 of May 10, 2023 “On the specifics of acquiring the right of state and municipal property to ownerless real estate on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic” (hereinafter referred to as Decree No. 140 of May 10, 2023), it is established that the body and officials of the Donetsk People's Republic exercising the powers assigned by the legislation of the Russian Federation to the powers bodies of local self-government, before the completion of the formation of bodies of local self-government, the local government body of a municipal formation in the Donetsk People's Republic, authorized to manage municipal property, has the right to apply to the court with a demand for recognition of the right of municipal ownership of ownerless buildings, structures (except for linear objects), premises, objects of unfinished construction that do not have an owner or the owner of which is unknown or the ownership of which the owner has refused, located on the territory of such municipal formation, after three months from the date of their registration as ownerless real estate by the body carrying out state registration of rights to real estate, unless otherwise provided by Decree No. 140 of May 10, 2023.
Due to the fact that the administration of the Donetsk urban district is vested with the authority to manage municipal property, in connection with which, the court comes to the conclusion that the authorized body applied to the court to recognize the residential premises as ownerless and to recognize the right of municipal property of the municipal entity to this residential premises.
Paragraph one of 225 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation defines that an ownerless thing is a thing that does not have an owner or the owner of which is unknown or, unless otherwise provided by law, the right of ownership to which the owner has refused.
Paragraph 3 of Article 218 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation states that in cases and in the manner provided for by the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, a person can acquire ownership of property that does not have an owner, property whose owner is unknown, or property that the owner has refused or to which he has lost ownership on other grounds provided by law.
Article 2 of the Law of the Donetsk People's Republic of March 21, 2024 No. 66-RZ, establishes that the signs of ownerless property in relation to residential premises are: failure to pay for residential premises and utilities within one year before the day of identification of residential premises that have signs of ownerlessness; lack of information about the registered ownership of residential premises in the Unified State Register of Real Estate; non-use of residential premises, which, among other things, creates a threat to its safety (or an apartment building if the premises is an apartment, a room in an apartment building), safety for the life and health of other persons (including the rights holders of other premises in an apartment building if the premises is an apartment, a room in an apartment building).
