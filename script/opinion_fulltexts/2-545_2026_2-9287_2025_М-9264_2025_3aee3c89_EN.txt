In this regard, taking into account the provisions of Article 167 of the Civil Procedure Code of the Russian Federation (hereinafter referred to as the Civil Procedure Code of the Russian Federation), the court considers it possible to consider the case in the absence of persons who did not appear.
Having examined the case materials, the court comes to the conclusion that the application must be granted for the following reasons.
According to the provisions of Article 3 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation, an interested person has the right, in the manner established by the legislation on civil proceedings, to apply to the court for the protection of violated or disputed rights, freedoms or legitimate interests.
In accordance with Part 2 of Article 290 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation, an application for recognition of ownership of an ownerless immovable property is submitted to the court at its location by the body authorized to manage municipal property or property owned by the federal city of Moscow, St. Petersburg or Sevastopol.
Part 2 of Article 293 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation provides that the court, having recognized that the immovable thing does not have an owner or the owner of the immovable thing is unknown and it has been registered in the prescribed manner, makes a decision to recognize the right of municipal property or the property of a federal city of Moscow, St. Petersburg or Sevastopol to this thing.
By virtue of paragraph 1 of Article 225 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation (hereinafter referred to as the Civil Code of the Russian Federation), an ownerless thing is a thing that does not have an owner or the owner of which is unknown or, unless otherwise provided by law, the right of ownership to which the owner has refused.
Paragraph 3 of Article 218 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation establishes that in cases and in the manner provided for by this Code, a person may acquire the right of ownership to property that does not have an owner, to property whose owner is unknown, or to property that the owner has abandoned or to which he has lost the right of ownership on other grounds provided by law.
According to Article 4 of the Federal Constitutional Law of the Russian Federation of October 4, 2022 No. 5-FKZ “On the admission into the Russian Federation of the Donetsk People's Republic and the formation within the Russian Federation of a new subject of the Donetsk People's Republic” (hereinafter referred to as the Law of October 4, 2022 No. 5-FKZ), legislative and other regulatory legal acts of the Russian Federation are valid on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic from the date of adoption in the Russian Federation of the Donetsk People's Republic and the formation of a new subject within the Russian Federation, unless otherwise provided by this Federal Constitutional Law.
Regulatory legal acts of the Donetsk People's Republic are valid on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic until the end of the transition period or until the adoption of the corresponding regulatory legal act of the Russian Federation and (or) the regulatory legal act of the Donetsk People's Republic.
According to Part 7 of Article 12 of the Federal Constitutional Law of the Russian Federation of October 4, 2022 No. 5-FKZ, the management of property located on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic, which has signs of ownerlessness and is not classified as federal property, the property of a constituent entity of the Russian Federation or municipal property, is carried out by the authorities of the Donetsk People's Republic in the manner established by the Government of the Russian Federation, unless otherwise provided by this Federal Constitutional Law. The procedure for recognizing such property as ownerless, established by the regulatory legal acts of the Donetsk People's Republic, is applied until January 1, 2028, in agreement with the federal executive body exercising the functions of managing state property.
In accordance with subparagraph 3.1 of paragraph 3 of the Decree of the Acting Head of the Donetsk People's Republic dated February 27, 2023 No. 64 “On some issues related to the management of property that has signs of being ownerless, located on the territory of the Donetsk People’s Republic, and the recognition of such property as ownerless” (hereinafter referred to as Decree No. 64), administrations of cities and districts are bodies for managing property that has signs of being ownerless. ownerless and not classified as federal property, located on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic, in accordance with the Rules approved by Decree of the Government of the Russian Federation of December 28, 2022 No. 2474 “On approval of the Rules for the management of bodies of the Donetsk People's Republic, Lugansk People's Republic, Zaporozhye region and Kherson region, having signs of ownerlessness and not classified as federal property, property subject of the Russian Federation or municipal property with property located on the territories of the specified subjects of the Russian Federation.”
Subclause 3.2 of clause 3 of Decree No. 64 determines that until January 1, 2028, in relation to the property specified in subclause 3.1 of clause 3, the procedure for recognizing it as ownerless is applied, established by Decree of the Head of the Donetsk People's Republic of April 28, 2021 No. 116 “On the identification, recording and acceptance into municipal ownership of ownerless real estate and escheated property" (hereinafter referred to as Standard Procedure No. 116), taking into account the changes introduced by Decree No. 64.
In accordance with the decision of the Donetsk City Council of the Donetsk People's Republic dated November 30, 2023 No. 1/8-3 “On issues of legal succession in the municipal formation of the Donetsk City District of the Donetsk People's Republic”, when performing the functions of managing property that has signs of ownerlessness, the administration of the Donetsk City District performs the functions of identifying, recording and accepting ownerless things into municipal ownership.
The procedure for identifying, registering, storing and accepting into municipal ownership of the territorial community of the city of Donetsk on the territory of the city of Donetsk is determined by the Procedure for identifying, recording and accepting ownerless real estate and escheat into municipal ownership of the territorial community of the city of Donetsk, approved by the order of the head of the administration of the city of Donetsk dated June 22, 2021 No. 1370 (hereinafter referred to as Procedure No. 1370).
Clause 1.1 of Procedure No. 1370, which was developed in accordance with the Civil Code of the Russian Federation and in pursuance of paragraph 2 of the Decree of the Head of the Donetsk People's Republic of April 28, 2021 No. 116 “On the identification, registration and acceptance into municipal ownership of ownerless real estate and escheat property”, defines the procedure for identifying, registering, storing and accepting into municipal ownership the territorial community of the city of Donetsk ownerless real estate and escheat property identified on the territory of the city of Donetsk.
According to clause 2.1 of section II of the Procedure for identifying, recording and accepting ownerless real estate residential property into municipal ownership of the municipality of the Donetsk city district of the Donetsk People's Republic, approved by the decision of the Donetsk city council of the Donetsk People's Republic dated July 4, 2024 No. 1/32-5 (hereinafter referred to as Procedure No. 1/32-5), the administration of the Donetsk city district of the Donetsk People's Republic represented by the department municipal property, the administration of the Donetsk urban district independently carries out actions to identify, record and accept ownerless real estate located on the territory of the Donetsk urban district into the municipal property of the Donetsk urban district. Information about residential premises that have signs of ownerlessness may be received by the municipal property department from individuals and legal entities, including public authorities, who have become aware of such property.
According to clause 2.2 of Procedure No. 1/32-5, the signs of ownerless real estate in relation to residential premises are: failure to pay for residential premises and utilities within one year before the day of identification of residential premises that have signs of ownerlessness; lack of information about the registered ownership of residential premises in the Unified State Register of Real Estate; non-use of residential premises, which, among other things, creates a threat to its safety (or an apartment building if the premises is an apartment, a room in an apartment building), safety for the life and health of other persons (including the rights holders of other premises in an apartment building if the premises is an apartment, a room in an apartment building).
