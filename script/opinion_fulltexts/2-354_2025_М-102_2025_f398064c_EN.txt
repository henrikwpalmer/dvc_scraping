Having studied the materials of the civil case, I consider it necessary to correct the mistake made in the court decision for the following reasons.
Mistakes are subject to correction in accordance with Art. 200, 203.1 Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation.
According to Art. 200 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation, the court may, on its own initiative or at the request of persons participating in the case, correct clerical errors in the court decision.
Issues of correcting clerical errors are considered by the court within ten days from the date of receipt of the application to the court, without holding a court hearing and without notifying the persons participating in the case. If necessary, the court may summon persons participating in the case to a court hearing, notifying them of the time and place of its holding (Article 203.1 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation).
Analyzing the requirements of these norms, the court believes that the judicial decision must be accurate. Errors in the text of a court decision, caused by arithmetic errors or grammatical errors (slips), relating to significant circumstances or complicating the execution of the decision, can be eliminated by the court that made the decision. The court can only correct those arithmetic errors that it itself has made. Typos are errors caused by incorrect spelling of words. Only those errors that are significant are subject to correction. These include writing surnames and names, addresses, names of the disputed property, indicating dates and deadlines. This is especially true for the operative part of the decision. In the operative part, any clerical error is of significant importance, since it can complicate the execution of the decision. The initiative to correct errors can come from any of the persons participating in the case or the court. Errors can be corrected in a court decision regardless of whether it has entered into legal force or been executed.
