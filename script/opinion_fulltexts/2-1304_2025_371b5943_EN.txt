By virtue of Art. 209 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, the owner has the rights to own, use and dispose of his property.
The subject of a claim for recognition of a right is a statement of the fact that the subject has a proprietary right to property, and the basis for the claim is the circumstances confirming that the plaintiff has such a right. The use of this method of protection requires proof of the legal basis for the emergence of the right of ownership of the specified property by the person applying to the court.
According to Part 3 of Article 218 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, in the cases and in the manner provided for by this Code, a person may acquire ownership of property that does not have an owner, property whose owner is unknown, or property that the owner has abandoned or to which he has lost ownership on other grounds provided by law.
In accordance with Part 1, Part 3 of Article 225 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, an ownerless thing is one that does not have an owner or the owner of which is unknown, or, unless otherwise provided by law, the owner of which has renounced the right of ownership. Ownerless real estate is accepted for registration by the body that carries out state registration of rights to real estate, upon application from the local government body in whose territory they are located.
The provisions of Art. 236 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation establishes that a citizen or legal entity may renounce the right of ownership of property belonging to him by declaring this, or by performing other actions that definitely indicate his removal from ownership, use and disposal of property without the intention of retaining any rights to this property. Relinquishment of the right of ownership does not entail the termination of the rights and obligations of the owner in relation to the relevant property until the acquisition of ownership of it by another person.
In accordance with Part 1 of Article 4 of the Federal Constitutional Law No. 5-FKZ of October 4, 2022 “On the admission of the Donetsk People’s Republic into the Russian Federation and the formation of a new subject within the Russian Federation - the Donetsk People’s Republic” (hereinafter referred to as Law No. 5-FKZ), legislative and other regulatory legal acts of the Russian Federation are valid on the territory of the Donetsk People’s Republic from the date of admission of the Donetsk People’s Republic to the Russian Federation of the People's Republic and the formation of a new subject within the Russian Federation, unless otherwise provided by this Federal Constitutional Law.
Regulatory legal acts of the Donetsk People's Republic are valid until the end of the transition period or until the adoption of the corresponding regulatory legal act of the Russian Federation and (or) the regulatory legal act of the Donetsk People's Republic.
In accordance with clause 2, part 1, art. 21 of Law No. 5-FKZ, until 01.01.2028, on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic, regulatory legal acts of the Donetsk People's Republic may establish features of regulation of property relations, including land turnover, in agreement with the federal executive body performing the functions of managing state property.
According to Part 7 of Article 12 of the Federal Constitutional Law of October 4, 2022 No. 5-FKZ “On the admission of the Donetsk People’s Republic to the Russian Federation and the formation of a new subject within the Russian Federation - the Donetsk People’s Republic”, management of property located on the territory of the Donetsk People’s Republic, which has signs of ownerlessness and is not classified as federal property, the property of a constituent entity of the Russian Federation or municipal property, is carried out by the authorities of the Donetsk of the People's Republic in the manner established by the Government of the Russian Federation, unless otherwise provided by this Federal Constitutional Law. The procedure for recognizing such property as ownerless, established by the regulatory legal acts of the Donetsk People's Republic, is applied until January 1, 2028, in agreement with the federal executive body performing the functions of managing state property.
In pursuance of the above federal constitutional law, on May 10, 2023, the Acting Head of the Donetsk People's Republic signed Decree No. 140 “On the peculiarities of acquiring state and municipal ownership of ownerless real estate on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic,” according to which the executive body of the Donetsk People's Republic authorized to manage state property has the right to go to court with a demand for recognition of state ownership of ownerless buildings after three months from the date of their registration as ownerless real estate by the body carrying out state registration of title to real estate. The executive body of the Donetsk People's Republic authorized to manage state property is the State Property Fund of the DPR (clause 1.2 of the Decree).
By Decree of the Acting Head of the Donetsk People's Republic of February 27, 2023 No. 64 “On some issues related to the management of property that has signs of ownerlessness, located on the territory of the Donetsk People’s Republic, and the recognition of such property as ownerless” (hereinafter referred to as Decree No. 64), the Government of the Donetsk People’s Republic is vested with the authority to establish (adopt) the procedure for recognition as ownerless property that has signs of being ownerless and not classified as federal property, property of a constituent entity of the Russian Federation or municipal property located on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic, with the exception of real estate in the form of residential premises, residential buildings, and outbuildings.
As part of the implementation of Decree No. 140, as well as Decree No. 64, by Decree of the Government of the Donetsk People's Republic dated June 22, 2023 No. 45-5, the executive body of the Donetsk People's Republic authorized to manage state real estate established the State Property Fund of the Donetsk People's Republic and approved the Procedure for recognizing real estate located on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic as ownerless, with the exception of residential houses, residential premises, garden houses and outbuildings intended to satisfy citizens' household and other needs (hereinafter referred to as the Procedure), which regulates legal relations and establishes the procedure for recognizing property on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic as ownerless, as well as recognizing the right of state ownership of the Donetsk People's Republic to ownerless real estate.
Clause 7 of the Procedure establishes that no later than the next working day after receiving an application for the identification of an ownerless immovable property from the body that identified the object, the State Property Fund of the Donetsk People's Republic sends information about the identified ownerless immovable property to the executive body of the Donetsk People's Republic that implements state policy in the field of mass communications, with the aim of posting this information on the official website of the Donetsk People's Republic in the information and telecommunications network "Internet" to identify and inform property owners.
According to clause 12 of the Procedure, after three months from the date of registration with the authorized territorial body of the federal executive body carrying out state cadastral registration, state registration of rights, maintenance of the Unified State Register of Real Estate as an ownerless immovable property, with the exception of linear objects (including integral technological parts), the FGI DPR has the right to apply to the judicial authorities with an application for recognition of the right of state ownership of the ownerless immovable thing.
According to Part 2 of Art. 290 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation, an application for recognition of ownership of an ownerless immovable property is submitted to the court at its location by the body authorized to manage municipal property or property owned by the federal city of Moscow, St. Petersburg or Sevastopol.
According to Part 2 of Article 291 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation, the application of the body authorized to manage municipal property or property owned by the federal city of Moscow, St. Petersburg or Sevastopol for recognition of ownership of an ownerless immovable thing must indicate by whom, when the immovable thing was registered, and must also provide evidence indicating the absence of its owner.
Taking into account the above, as well as the above rules of law in their totality, the court comes to the conclusion that the ownerlessness of a thing should be understood as the actual state of the thing, regardless of the right of ownership to it, in which the thing is not in anyone’s possession and (or) there is no interest in it on the part of a specific person.
The absence of state registration of the ownership rights of a company or another person to a disputed object cannot in itself be a basis for declaring it ownerless.

At the same time, in accordance with clause 5, part 1, part 2, article 12 of the Federal Constitutional Law of October 4, 2022 No. 5-FKZ “On the admission of the Donetsk People's Republic into the Russian Federation and the formation of a new subject within the Russian Federation - the Donetsk People's Republic”, are recognized and valid on the territory of the Russian Federation without limitation of validity and any confirmation from the state bodies of the Russian Federation, state bodies of the Donetsk People's Republic, if otherwise not provided for by this Federal Constitutional Law or does not follow from the documents themselves or the essence of the relationship, documents that were in force on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic on the day of the admission of the Donetsk People's Republic to the Russian Federation and the formation of a new subject within the Russian Federation, which are the basis for state registration of rights to real estate located on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic, confirming the emergence, change, limitation, transfer or termination of rights to such property, encumbrance of such property, issued by state authorities of Ukraine, local government bodies of Ukraine and (or) certified by notaries of Ukraine (if documents for such property are not issued by state and (or) other official bodies of the Donetsk People's Republic and (or) not certified by notaries of the Donetsk People's Republic or not issued by bodies included in the unified system of public authority in the Russian Federation). The documents specified in paragraph 5 of part 1 of this article, issued before the day of admission to the Russian Federation of the Donetsk People's Republic and the formation of a new subject within the Russian Federation, are submitted before January 1, 2028 for state registration of the relevant rights, restrictions and encumbrances by the bodies carrying out state registration of rights to real estate and transactions with it, in the Unified State Register of Real Estate in the manner established by the legislation of the Russian Federation.
In addition, the absence of state registration of the right to disputed real estate cannot affect the court’s conclusions about the presence of an owner (owners) of the disputed real estate, since in accordance with the Determination of the Constitutional Court of the Russian Federation of July 5, 2001 No. 154-O, state registration, as a formal condition for ensuring state, including judicial, protection of the rights of a person arising from contractual relations, the object of which is real estate, is intended only to certify on the part of the state the legal force of the relevant title documents, it does not affect the very content of civil law and does not limit freedom of contract, legal equality, autonomy of will and property independence of the parties.
The court was not presented with any evidence indicating that the owner abandoned his property, as well as that the applicant took all necessary measures to establish the owner of the property and that the disputed property is ownerless property.
Registration by the applicant of the disputed property as ownerless under the specified circumstances is not evidence of its ownerlessness. The absence of information about the owner in the registers of federal property, subject property and municipal property is also not evidence confirming the fact indicated by the applicant.
Since the applicant has not proven the existence of grounds for classifying the disputed object as ownerless, the specified object has an owner, accordingly, recognition by the court of ownership of the object specified in the application on the grounds presented by the applicant would contradict Part 2 of Art. 218 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, according to which the right of ownership of property that has an owner can be acquired by another person on the basis of a contract of sale, exchange, donation or other transaction on the alienation of this property.
In such circumstances, since the owner of the disputed property was reliably identified at the court hearing, the applicant did not present evidence indicating that the owner renounced the right of ownership of the immovable property, taking into account the above provisions of the law and the circumstances of the case, the court comes to the conclusion that there are no legal grounds for recognizing the property as ownerless property and recognizing the right of state ownership of the disputed property, and therefore the stated demands of the State Property Fund of the Donetsk People's Republic cannot be satisfied.
