In accordance with Part 3 of Art. 196 of the Civil Procedure Code of the Russian Federation, the court makes a decision on the claims stated by the plaintiff.
According to Part 2 of Art. 56 of this code, the court determines what circumstances are important for the case, which party must prove them, and brings the circumstances up for discussion, even if the parties did not refer to any of them.
The relevant circumstances are determined by the court based on the grounds of claim indicated by the plaintiff, the arguments and objections of the parties and the rules of substantive law to be applied in resolving the dispute.
Part 1 art. 196 of the said code also establishes that when making a decision, the court evaluates the evidence, determines which circumstances relevant to the consideration of the case have been established and which circumstances have not been established, what are the legal relations of the parties, what law should be applied in this case and whether the claim is subject to satisfaction.
Art. 198 of the same code stipulates that the reasons for the court decision must indicate the circumstances of the case established by the court; evidence on which the court's conclusions about these circumstances are based; reasons why the court rejects certain evidence; laws that guided the court.
According to Art. 56 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation, each party must prove the circumstances to which it refers as the basis for its claims and objections.
By virtue of Art. 67 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation, the court evaluates evidence according to its internal conviction, based on a comprehensive, complete, objective and direct examination of the evidence available in the case.

Thus, Chapter 33 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation regulates the procedure for considering cases of recognizing a movable thing as ownerless or recognizing ownership of an ownerless immovable thing.
According to paragraph 3 of Art. 218 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, in cases and in the manner provided for by this Code, a person may acquire the right of ownership to property that does not have an owner, to property whose owner is unknown, or to property that the owner has abandoned or to which he has lost the right of ownership on other grounds provided by law.
In accordance with paragraph 1 of Art. 225 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, ownerless is a thing that does not have an owner, the owner of which is unknown, or the right of ownership to which the owner has refused.
Based on the provisions of paragraph 3 of Art. 225 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, ownerless real estate is accepted for registration by the body that carries out state registration of rights to real estate, upon application from the local government body in whose territory they are located. After a year has passed from the date of registration of an ownerless immovable property, the body authorized to manage municipal property may apply to the court with a request to recognize the right of municipal ownership of this thing. An ownerless immovable property, not recognized by a court decision as having come into municipal ownership, may be again taken into possession, use and disposal by the owner who abandoned it, or acquired into ownership by virtue of acquisitive prescription.
According to paragraphs 1 and 2 of Art. 130 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, immovable things (real estate, real estate) include land plots, subsoil plots and everything that is firmly connected to the land, that is, objects whose movement without disproportionate damage to their purpose is impossible, including buildings, structures, unfinished construction projects. Items not related to real estate, including money and securities, are recognized as movable property.
Recognition of ownership of an ownerless immovable property is carried out in a special proceeding upon the application of the body authorized to manage municipal property, in accordance with Art. Art. 290-293 Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation.
By virtue of Part 3 of Art. 263 of the Civil Procedure Code of the Russian Federation, if, when filing an application or considering a case in a special proceeding, it is established that there is a dispute about the right within the jurisdiction of the court, the court issues a ruling to leave the application without consideration, in which it explains to the applicant and other interested parties their right to resolve the dispute in the manner of litigation.
According to the meaning of these norms, the specified application is considered in special proceedings, when there is no need to resolve the dispute about the subjective right itself.
In accordance with Part 1 of Art. 4 of the Federal Constitutional Law of October 4, 2022 No. 5-FKZ “On the admission of the Donetsk People's Republic into the Russian Federation and the formation of a new subject within the Russian Federation - the Donetsk People's Republic” (hereinafter referred to as Law No. 5-FKZ) legislative and other regulatory legal acts of the Russian Federation are valid on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic from the date of admission to the Russian Federation of the Donetsk People's Republic and the formation of of the Russian Federation of a new subject, unless otherwise provided by Law No. 5-FKZ.
According to Part 2 of Art. 4 of Law No. 5-FKZ, the regulatory legal acts of the Donetsk People's Republic are valid on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic until the end of the transition period or until the adoption of the relevant regulatory legal act of the Russian Federation and (or) the regulatory legal act of the Donetsk People's Republic.
At the same time, Art. 21 of Law No. 5-FKZ establishes the specifics of regulating relations in the sphere of real estate turnover on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic.
In accordance with clause 2, part 1, art. 21 of Law No. 5-FKZ, until January 01, 2028, on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic, regulatory legal acts of the Donetsk People's Republic may establish features of the regulation of property relations, including the circulation of land, in agreement with the federal executive body performing the functions of managing state property.
By virtue of Part 7 of Art. 12 of Law No. 5-FKZ, the procedure for recognizing property that is not classified as federal property, the property of a constituent entity of the Russian Federation or municipal property as ownerless, established by the regulatory legal acts of the Donetsk People's Republic, is applied until January 1, 2028 in agreement with the federal executive body performing the functions of managing state property.
In pursuance of the above federal constitutional law, on May 10, 2023, the Acting Head of the Donetsk People's Republic signed Decree No. 140 “On the specifics of acquiring the right of state and municipal property to ownerless real estate on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic” (hereinafter referred to as Decree No. 140), according to which the executive body of the Donetsk People's Republic authorized to manage state property has the right to go to court with a demand for recognition of the right state ownership of ownerless buildings after three months from the date of their registration as ownerless real estate by the body carrying out state registration of rights to real estate.
The executive body of the Donetsk People's Republic authorized to manage state property is the Ministry of Property and Land Relations of the Donetsk People's Republic.
By decree of the Acting Head of the Donetsk People's Republic of February 27. 2023 No. 64 “On some issues related to the management of property that has signs of ownerlessness, located on the territory of the Donetsk People’s Republic, and the recognition of such property as ownerless” (hereinafter referred to as Decree No. 64), the Government of the Donetsk People’s Republic is empowered to establish (adopt) a procedure for recognizing as ownerless property that has signs of ownerlessness and is not classified as federal property, the property of a subject Russian Federation or municipal property located on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic, with the exception of real estate in the form of residential premises, residential buildings, and outbuildings.
As part of the implementation of Decree No. 140, as well as Decree No. 64, by Decree of the Government of the Donetsk People's Republic of June 22, 2023 No. 45-5, the executive body of the Donetsk People's Republic authorized to manage state real estate established the State Property Fund of the Donetsk People's Republic and approved the Procedure for recognizing real estate located on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic ownerless, with the exception of residential buildings, residential premises, garden houses and outbuildings intended to satisfy citizens' household and other needs (hereinafter referred to as the Procedure), which regulates legal relations and establishes the procedure for recognizing property on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic as ownerless, as well as recognizing the right of state ownership of the Donetsk People's Republic to an ownerless immovable property.

If this is not excluded by the rules of this Code on the acquisition of ownership of things. which the owner abandoned (Article 226), about a find (Articles 227 and 228), about stray animals (Articles 230 and 231) and treasure (Article 233), the right of ownership of ownerless movable things can be acquired by virtue of acquisitive prescription.
By virtue of the provisions of Art. 236 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, a citizen or legal entity may renounce the right of ownership of property belonging to him by declaring this or taking other actions that definitely indicate his removal from ownership, use and disposal of property without the intention of retaining any rights to this property.
From the content of these norms it follows that the current legislation provides for the possibility of terminating the right of ownership of a particular property by the owner performing actions indicating his renunciation of his property right.
At the same time, actions indicating the owner’s renunciation of the right of ownership may include, among other things, the removal of the owner from the possession and use of property belonging to him, and failure to take measures to maintain this property.
