The ownerless status of a thing is undesirable for civil circulation; it should be considered as a temporary phenomenon, since it not only makes it difficult to extract its useful properties from such a thing, but creates uncertainty in matters of content, liability due to harm caused during its use, and also entails other negative consequences. Recognition of the right of municipal ownership of ownerless property is closely related to issues of management and disposal of municipal property, which in many cases makes it possible to preserve it from destruction and looting, and makes it possible to use this property in accordance with current legislation. Since within three months from the moment the real estate property was registered as ownerless, no one declared their rights to this property, the administration had the right to apply to the court to recognize the right of municipal ownership to it.

By virtue of Art. 209 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, the owner has the rights to own, use and dispose of his property.
The subject of a claim for recognition of a right is a statement of the fact that the subject has a proprietary right to property, and the basis for the claim is the circumstances confirming that the plaintiff has such a right. The use of this method of protection requires proof of the legal basis for the emergence of the right of ownership of the specified property by the person applying to the court.
According to Part 3 of Art. 218 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, in cases and in the manner provided for by this Code, a person may acquire the right of ownership to property that does not have an owner, to property whose owner is unknown, or to property that the owner has abandoned or to which he has lost the right of ownership on other grounds provided by law.
In accordance with Part 1, Part 3 of Art. 225 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, ownerless is a thing that does not have an owner or the owner of which is unknown, or, unless otherwise provided by law, the right of ownership to which the owner has refused. Ownerless real estate is accepted for registration by the body that carries out state registration of rights to real estate, upon application from the local government body in whose territory they are located.
The provisions of Art. 236 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation establishes that a citizen or legal entity may renounce the right of ownership of property belonging to him by declaring this, or by performing other actions that definitely indicate his removal from ownership, use and disposal of property without the intention of retaining any rights to this property. Relinquishment of the right of ownership does not entail the termination of the rights and obligations of the owner in relation to the relevant property until the acquisition of ownership of it by another person.
In accordance with Part 1 of Art. 4 of the Federal Constitutional Law of October 4, 2022 No. 5-FKZ “On the admission of the Donetsk People's Republic into the Russian Federation and the formation of a new subject within the Russian Federation - the Donetsk People's Republic”, legislative and other regulatory legal acts of the Russian Federation are valid on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic from the date of admission of the Donetsk People's Republic into the Russian Federation and the formation of a new entity within the Russian Federation, unless otherwise provided this Federal Constitutional Law.
Regulatory legal acts of the Donetsk People's Republic are valid until the end of the transition period or until the adoption of the corresponding regulatory legal act of the Russian Federation and (or) the regulatory legal act of the Donetsk People's Republic.
According to Part 7 of Art. 12 of the Federal Constitutional Law of October 4, 2022 No. 5-FKZ “On the admission of the Donetsk People's Republic to the Russian Federation and the formation of a new subject within the Russian Federation - the Donetsk People's Republic”, management of property located on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic, which has signs of ownerlessness and is not classified as federal property, the property of a subject of the Russian Federation or municipal property, is carried out by the authorities of the Donetsk People's Republic in the manner established by the Government of the Russian Federation, unless otherwise provided by this Federal Constitutional Law. The procedure for recognizing such property as ownerless, established by the regulatory legal acts of the Donetsk People's Republic, is applied until January 1, 2028, in agreement with the federal executive body performing the functions of managing state property.
Decree of the Head of the Donetsk People's Republic dated April 28, 2021 No. 116 established a standard procedure for identifying, recording and accepting ownerless real estate and escheatable property into municipal ownership (hereinafter referred to as the Standard Procedure).
According to clause 2.2. According to the standard procedure, the administrations of districts and cities of the Donetsk People's Republic independently carry out actions to identify, record and accept ownerless real estate into the municipal ownership of the administrative-territorial units of the Donetsk People's Republic on the territory of which they are located.
According to clause 2.11 of the Standard Procedure, after 30 calendar days from the date of publication of the announcement, in the event of the absence of a person (persons) who considers themselves the owner of an immovable thing, and after receiving from the authorities specified in subclause 2.9.3 of clause 2.9 of this Procedure, responses indicating the absence of the owner of an immovable thing that has signs of being ownerless, the administration: - within 5 working days, an act on the impossibility of identifying the owner of this property; - organizes, if necessary, in the prescribed manner, work on conducting a technical inventory of immovable property that has signs of being ownerless, preparing technical documentation for this object, as well as assigning a postal address to it; - carries out, if necessary, the collection of other documents confirming the presence of signs of ownerless things, including documents indicating the absence of residents in ownerless residential premises (inspection reports, extracts from house books, personal accounts, etc.).
Clause 2.12. The standard procedure provides that in order to register an immovable thing as ownerless, he issues an appropriate order and applies to the body that carries out state registration of real rights to real estate and their restrictions (encumbrances) with an application to register the immovable thing as ownerless.
According to paragraphs. 2, 3, 12, 13 of the Procedure for registering ownerless real estate, established by Order of Rosreestr No. P/0086 dated March 15, 2023, registration of ownerless real estate is carried out by the federal executive body, which carries out the functions of developing state policy and legal regulation in the field of state cadastral registration of real estate and state registration of rights to real estate and transactions with it, its territorial bodies, executive authorities of the Republic of Crimea and the federal city of Sevastopol upon transferring to them the powers of the rights registration body by resolutions of the Government of the Russian Federation in accordance with Part 8 of Article 72 of Federal Law No. 218-FZ (hereinafter referred to as the rights registration body). Buildings (structures), structures, premises, parking spaces, objects of unfinished construction (hereinafter referred to as real estate objects), information about which is included in the Unified State Register of Real Estate (hereinafter referred to as the Unified State Register of Real Estate) and which do not have owners or whose owners are unknown, or, unless otherwise provided by law, the owners of which have renounced their ownership rights, are accepted for registration. The decision to register a real estate property as ownerless is made no later than fifteen working days from the date of receipt of the application and necessary documents by the rights registration authority. Registration of a real estate property as ownerless is carried out by entering the relevant information into the Unified State Register of Real Estate in accordance with the procedure for maintaining the Unified State Register of Real Estate, approved in accordance with Part 7 of Article 7 of Federal Law No. 218-FZ.
According to Art. 2 of the DPR Law “On the specifics of identifying, using and recognizing the right of municipal property of municipalities of the Donetsk People’s Republic to residential premises with signs of ownerless property located on the territory of the Donetsk People’s Republic”, adopted by the Resolution of the People’s Council on March 21, 2024 (hereinafter referred to as Law No. 66-RZ of March 21, 2024), signs of ownerless property in relation to residential premises are: failure to pay for residential premises and utilities within one year before the day of identification of residential premises that have signs of ownerlessness; lack of information about the registered ownership of residential premises in the Unified State Register of Real Estate; non-use of residential premises, which, among other things, creates a threat to its safety (or an apartment building if the premises is an apartment, a room in an apartment building), safety for the life and health of other persons (including the rights holders of other premises in an apartment building if the premises is an apartment, a room in an apartment building).
According to Art. 10 of Law No. 66-RZ of March 21, 2024, after three months from the date of registration of the residential premises as ownerless by the territorial body of the federal executive body carrying out state cadastral registration, state registration of rights, maintaining the Unified State Register of Real Estate, the authorized body applies to the court with an application for recognition of the right of municipal ownership of the municipality to this residential premises in the manner prescribed by the legislation of the Russian Federation Federation.
According to Part 2 of Art. 290 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation, an application for recognition of ownership of an ownerless immovable property is submitted to the court at its location by the body authorized to manage municipal property or property owned by the federal city of Moscow, St. Petersburg or Sevastopol.
In accordance with Part 2 of Art. 291 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation, in the application of the body authorized to manage municipal property or property owned by the federal city of Moscow, St. Petersburg or Sevastopol, on the recognition of ownership of an ownerless immovable thing, it must be indicated by whom, when the immovable thing was registered, and evidence indicating the absence of its owner must also be provided.
By virtue of Art. 293 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation, the court, having recognized that the immovable thing does not have an owner or the owner of the thing is unknown and it was registered in the prescribed manner, makes a decision to recognize the right of municipal ownership of this thing.
Thus, by virtue of the above rules of law in their totality, the ownerlessness of a thing should be understood as the actual state of the thing, regardless of the right of ownership to it, in which the thing is not in anyone’s possession and (or) there is no interest in it on the part of a specific person.
Considering the stated requirements, taking into account the provisions of the current legislation and the established circumstances of the case, the court considers that in relation to the disputed real estate property there are no defining signs of ownerless property established by Article 2 of Law No. 66-RZ of March 21, 2024, in particular, such a sign as “failure to pay for residential premises and utilities within one year before the day of identification of residential premises that have signs of ownerlessness”, since after the applicant identified a disputed piece of real estate in June 2024 as having signs of an ownerless item, and registered it on September 20, 2024, it was established that utility bills (services for the maintenance of a residential building and local area) were paid for this residential premises in the period from September 2024 to January 2025, that is, during the year, monthly, for the total amount 7490 rubles.
Regarding the lack of information about the registered ownership of residential premises at the specified address in the Unified State Register of Real Estate, the court notes the following.
In accordance with clause 5, part 1, part 2 of article 12 of the Federal Constitutional Law of October 4, 2022 No. 5-FKZ “On the admission of the Donetsk People's Republic into the Russian Federation and the formation of a new subject within the Russian Federation - the Donetsk People's Republic”, are recognized and valid on the territory of the Russian Federation without limitation of validity and any confirmation from the state bodies of the Russian Federation, state bodies of the Donetsk People's Republic, unless otherwise provided for by this Federal Constitutional Law or does not follow from the documents themselves or the essence of the relationship, documents that were in force on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic on the day of the admission of the Donetsk People's Republic to the Russian Federation and the formation of a new subject within the Russian Federation, which are the basis for state registration of rights to real estate located on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic, confirming the emergence, change, limitation, transfer or termination of rights to such property, encumbrance of such property, issued by authorities state authorities of Ukraine, local government bodies of Ukraine and (or) certified by notaries of Ukraine (unless documents for such property were issued by state and (or) other official bodies of the Donetsk People's Republic and (or) not certified by notaries of the Donetsk People's Republic or were not issued by bodies included in the unified system of public authority in the Russian Federation). The documents specified in paragraph 5 of part 1 of this article, issued before the day of admission to the Russian Federation of the Donetsk People's Republic and the formation of a new subject within the Russian Federation, are submitted before January 1, 2028 for state registration of the relevant rights, restrictions and encumbrances by the bodies carrying out state registration of rights to real estate and transactions with it, in the Unified State Register of Real Estate in the manner established by the legislation of the Russian Federation.
In addition, the absence of state registration of the right to disputed real estate cannot affect the court’s conclusions about the presence of an owner (owners) of the disputed real estate, since in accordance with the Determination of the Constitutional Court of the Russian Federation of July 5, 2001 No. 154-O, state registration, as a formal condition for ensuring state, including judicial, protection of the rights of a person arising from contractual relations, the object of which is real estate, is intended only to certify on the part of the state the legal force of the relevant title documents, it does not affect the very content of civil law and does not limit freedom of contract, legal equality, autonomy of will and property independence of the parties.
The court was not presented with any evidence indicating that the owner (or the owner’s heir) abandoned his property, or that the applicant took all necessary measures to identify the owner of the property.
At the same time, according to the provisions of Art. Art. 55, 56, 67 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation, each party must prove the circumstances to which it refers as the basis for its claims and objections. Evidence in the case is information obtained in the manner prescribed by law about the facts, on the basis of which the court establishes the presence or absence of circumstances justifying the demands and objections of the parties, as well as other circumstances that are important for the proper consideration and resolution of the case.
The owner's inaction in relation to his property, namely: lack of control over the property, failure to take measures to properly maintain it, and the presence of arrears in paying for utilities cannot be considered as his refusal of ownership of this property.
Registration by the applicant of the disputed property as ownerless under the specified circumstances is not evidence of its ownerlessness. The absence of information about the owner in the registers of federal property, subject property and municipal property is also not evidence confirming the fact indicated by the applicant.
Under these circumstances, due to the absence of the necessary signs of ownerless property in relation to residential premises established by the current legislation, including special rules governing the institution of identifying ownerless real estate in the Donetsk People's Republic, the court does not see legal grounds for recognizing the applicant's right of municipal ownership of a real estate property located at the address: Russian Federation, Donetsk People's Republic, Gorlovka urban district,
<address>
(cadastral
№
), and comes to the conclusion that the stated demands of the Administration of the Gorlovka urban district of the Donetsk People's Republic are refused.
