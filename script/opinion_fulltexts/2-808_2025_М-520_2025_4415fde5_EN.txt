Guided by Art. 167 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation, the court decided to consider the case in the absence of the parties who did not appear.
The court, having examined the case materials, assessing the evidence presented in the case in its totality and interrelation, the court came to the conclusion that the requirements for the application are not legal and justified and cannot be satisfied.
According to paragraph 1 of Article 225 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, ownerless is a thing that does not have an owner or the owner of which is unknown or, unless otherwise provided by law, the right of ownership to which the owner has refused.
According to paragraph 3 of this article, ownerless immovable things are accepted for registration by the body carrying out state registration of rights to real estate, upon application from the local government body in whose territory they are located. After a year has passed from the date of registration of an ownerless immovable property, the body authorized to manage municipal property may apply to the court with a request to recognize the right of municipal ownership of this thing. An ownerless immovable property, not recognized by a court decision as having come into municipal ownership, may be again taken into possession, use and disposal by the owner who abandoned it, or acquired into ownership by virtue of acquisitive prescription.
During the investigation of the case, it was established that the premises in question with an area of 25.1 sq.m. are an integral part of the building located at
<address>
according to the technical passport, which is the property of the Limited Liability Company "Contact".
Within the meaning of the above-mentioned Article 225 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, a thing that has an owner who has not renounced the right of ownership to it cannot be recognized as ownerless.
There is also no information that the owner would refuse this premises - the entire building belongs on the basis of a purchase and sale agreement and a certificate of ownership to the Limited Liability Company "Contact".
In view of the above, the applicant's request to the Administration
<address>
is unfounded and cannot be satisfied. No evidence was presented to support a different conclusion in the case.
