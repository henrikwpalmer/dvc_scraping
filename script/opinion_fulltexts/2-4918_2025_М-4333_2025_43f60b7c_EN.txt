Taking into account the above, guided by Article 167 of the Civil Procedure Code of the Russian Federation, due to the fact that the persons participating in the case have been properly notified, the court considers it possible to consider the case in the absence of the above-mentioned persons.
According to the provisions of Art. 290 of the Civil Procedure Code of the Russian Federation, an application to recognize a movable thing as ownerless is submitted to the court by the person who took possession of it at the place of residence or location of the applicant.
The application for recognition of a movable thing as ownerless must indicate which movable thing is subject to recognition as ownerless, its main features must be described, as well as evidence indicating the owner’s renunciation of ownership of it, and evidence indicating that the applicant has taken possession of this thing (Article 291 of the Civil Procedure Code of the Russian Federation). The application of the body authorized to manage municipal property or property owned by the federal city of Moscow, St. Petersburg or Sevastopol for recognition of ownership of an ownerless immovable thing must indicate by whom, when the immovable thing was registered, and must also provide evidence indicating the absence of its owner.
According to the provisions of Art. 292 of the Civil Procedure Code of the Russian Federation, when preparing a case for trial, a judge finds out which persons (owners, actual owners and others) can provide information about the ownership of property, and also requests relevant organizations about the information available about it. An application to recognize a thing as ownerless or to recognize the right of ownership to an ownerless immovable thing is considered by the court with the participation of interested persons.

By virtue of clause 3 of Art. 263 of the Civil Procedure Code of the Russian Federation, if, when filing an application or considering a case in a special proceeding, it is established that there is a dispute about the right within the jurisdiction of the court, the court issues a ruling to leave the application without consideration, in which it explains to the applicant and other interested parties their right to resolve the dispute in the manner of litigation.
Within the meaning of this norm, the absence of a dispute about the law is a prerequisite for the application of procedural rules of special proceedings. If, during the consideration of the case, the presence of a substantive legal dispute is revealed, then its consideration should take place according to other procedural rules - the rules of claim proceedings.
Thus, since the resolution of this case revealed the existence of a substantive legal dispute between the applicant and an interested party, the court considers it necessary to submit a statement from the Ministry of Property and Land Relations of the Donetsk People's Republic, the interested party: Department of the Federal Service for State Registration, Cadastre and Cartography for the Donetsk People's Republic, Limited Liability Company "SEVPROM", on recognition of a non-residential building as an ownerless immovable property, recognition of the right of state ownership to ownerless real estate - leave without consideration.
The right to judicial protection in this case can be exercised not through special proceedings, but on a general basis, by filing a claim in the manner provided for in Art. 131-138 of the Civil Procedure Code of the Russian Federation.
