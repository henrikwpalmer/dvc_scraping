In accordance with paragraph 1 of Art. 225 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, ownerless is a thing that does not have an owner or the owner of which is unknown or, unless otherwise provided by law, the right of ownership to which the owner has refused.
According to Part 2 of Art. 290 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation, an application for recognition of the right of municipal ownership of an ownerless immovable property is submitted to the court at its location by the body authorized to manage municipal property.
Based on the legal position set out in paragraph 59 of the resolution of the Plenum of the Supreme Court of the Russian Federation
№
, Plenum of the Supreme Arbitration Court of the Russian Federation
№
from
DD.MM.YYYY
“On some issues arising in judicial practice when resolving disputes related to the protection of property rights and other property rights”, unless otherwise provided by law, a claim for recognition of a right is subject to satisfaction if the plaintiff presents evidence of the emergence of the corresponding right.
The procedure for recognizing the ownership of an ownerless immovable thing by the body authorized to manage municipal property is defined in
Full name4
33 Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation.
Registration of an object of real estate as ownerless, as well as subsequent recognition by the body authorized to manage municipal property of ownership of ownerless real estate in court on the basis of Art. 225 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation is possible only in the absence of legal claims of other persons to such property. At the same time, the absence of state registration of the ownership rights of a company or another person to a disputed object cannot in itself be a basis for declaring it ownerless.
