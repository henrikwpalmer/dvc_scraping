The court, having examined the case materials, comes to the following conclusion.
According to Part 1 of Art. 225 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, ownerless is a thing that does not have an owner or the owner of which is unknown, or a thing to which the owner has renounced the right of ownership.
By virtue of Part 1 of Art. 226 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, things abandoned by the owner or otherwise left by him for the purpose of renouncing the right of ownership of them (abandoned things) may be turned into their property by other persons in the manner provided for in paragraph 2 of this article.
Other abandoned things become the property of the person who took possession of them if, at the request of this person, they are recognized by the court as ownerless.
Depending on the type of ownerless movable thing, the law establishes various grounds and procedures for acquiring ownership rights, in particular a court decision.
Articles 225 - 233 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation regulate the acquisition of ownership rights to such things, including finds. This Code proceeds from the fact that if the owner abandoned the movable thing (Article 226) or did not apply for the found thing or was not identified (Article 228), priority in acquiring ownership of an ownerless thing is given, respectively, to persons interested in acquiring ownership or who found the thing.

By virtue of the above norms of substantive and procedural law, the decision to recognize a movable thing as ownerless and to transfer it into the ownership of the person who took possession is allowed only in relation to abandoned things. If a thing does not have an owner or the owner of the thing is unknown, the procedure for acquiring ownership rights established by Art. 226 Civil Code of the Russian Federation, ch. 33 Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation, not applicable.
According to Art. 236 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, a citizen or legal entity may renounce the right of ownership of property belonging to him by declaring this or taking other actions that definitely indicate his removal from ownership, use and disposal of property without the intention of retaining any rights to this property.
Confirmation that these cars are abandoned is the condition of these cars, reflected in the reports of its inspection and transfer.
In addition, the presence of cars in the parking lot entails an unreasonable borrowing of resources from government bodies and serves as a distraction from the actual functions of the body.
