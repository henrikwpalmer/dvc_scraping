The applicant's representative did not appear at the court hearing and was duly notified. The court received an application for consideration in the absence of a representative; the stated demands were requested to be satisfied.
The representative of the interested party, the Department of the Federal Service for State Registration, Cadastre and Cartography of the DPR, did not appear at the court hearing; the court was duly notified of the time and place of the hearing of the case. The court received an application to consider a civil case in the absence of a representative.
In accordance with Art. 167 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation, the court considers it possible to consider the case in the absence of persons who failed to appear.
Having studied the case materials, the court finds the application to be satisfied for the following reasons.
According to paragraph 3 of Article 218 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, in cases and in the manner provided for by this Code, a person may acquire ownership of property that does not have an owner, property whose owner is unknown, or property that the owner has abandoned or to which he has lost ownership on other grounds provided by law.
Paragraph 1 of Article 225 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation states that an ownerless thing is a thing that does not have an owner or the owner of which is unknown or, unless otherwise provided by law, the right of ownership to which the owner has refused.
Ownerless real estate is accepted for registration by the body that carries out state registration of rights to real estate, upon application from the local government body in whose territory they are located. After a year has passed from the date of registration of an ownerless immovable property, the body authorized to manage municipal property may apply to the court with a request to recognize the right of municipal ownership of this thing (clause 3 of Article 225 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation).
Based on the content of Article 225 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, ownerlessness of real estate presupposes the absence or unknownness of its owner, and the condition for considering an application to recognize property as ownerless is the absence of a dispute about the right. It is these facts that are included in the subject of proof in this category of cases and must be established in court.
According to Article 4 of the Federal Constitutional Law of the Russian Federation of October 4, 2022 No. 5-FKZ, “On the admission into the Russian Federation of the Donetsk People's Republic and the formation within the Russian Federation of a new subject - the Donetsk People's Republic” (hereinafter referred to as Law No. 5-FKZ), legislative and other regulatory legal acts of the Russian Federation are valid on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic from the date of admission of the Donetsk People's Republic to the Russian Federation of the People's Republic and the formation of a new subject within the Russian Federation, unless otherwise provided by this Federal Constitutional Law. Regulatory legal acts of the Donetsk People's Republic are valid on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic until the end of the transition period or until the adoption of the corresponding regulatory legal act of the Russian Federation and (or) the regulatory legal act of the Donetsk People's Republic.
According to Part 7 of Article 12 of Law No. 5-FKZ, management of property located on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic, which has signs of ownerlessness and is not classified as federal property, the property of a constituent entity of the Russian Federation or municipal property, is carried out by the authorities of the Donetsk People's Republic in the manner established by the Government of the Russian Federation, unless otherwise provided by this Federal Constitutional Law.
The procedure for recognizing such property as ownerless, established by the regulatory legal acts of the Donetsk People's Republic, is applied until January 1, 2028, in agreement with the federal executive body exercising the functions of managing state property.
Decree of the Acting Head of the Donetsk People's Republic dated May 10, 2023 No. 140 (hereinafter referred to as Decree No. 140 dated 05.10.2023) established the specifics of acquiring state and municipal property rights to ownerless real estate on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic.
Regulatory legal acts of the Donetsk People's Republic are valid on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic until the end of the transition period or until the adoption of the corresponding regulatory legal act of the Russian Federation and (or) the regulatory legal act of the Donetsk People's Republic.
According to Part 7 of Article 12 of Law No. 5-FKZ, management of property located on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic, which has signs of ownerlessness and is not classified as federal property, the property of a constituent entity of the Russian Federation or municipal property, is carried out by the authorities of the Donetsk People's Republic in the manner established by the Government of the Russian Federation, unless otherwise provided by this Federal Constitutional Law. The procedure for recognizing such property as ownerless, established by the regulatory legal acts of the Donetsk People's Republic, is applied until January 1, 2028, in agreement with the federal executive body exercising the functions of managing state property.
In accordance with subparagraph 3.1 of paragraph 3 of the Decree of the Acting Head of the Donetsk People's Republic dated February 27, 2023 No. 64 “On some issues related to the management of property that has signs of being ownerless, located on the territory of the Donetsk People’s Republic, and the recognition of such property as ownerless” (hereinafter referred to as Decree No. 64), administrations of cities and districts are bodies for managing property that has signs of being ownerless. ownerless and not classified as federal property, located on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic, in accordance with the Rules approved by the Decree of the Government of the Russian Federation of December 28, 2022 No. 2474 “On approval of the Rules for the management of bodies of the Donetsk People's Republic, Lugansk People's Republic, Zaporozhye region and Kherson region, having signs of ownerlessness and not classified as federal property, property subject of the Russian Federation or municipal property with property located on the territories of the specified subjects of the Russian Federation.”
Clause 1.2 of the decision of the Donetsk City Council of the Donetsk People's Republic dated November 30, 2023 No. 1/8-3 “On issues of legal succession in the municipal formation of the Donetsk City District of the Donetsk People's Republic” (hereinafter referred to as decision No. 1/8-3) determined that the legal successor of property rights and obligations of the bodies exercising on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic on the day of the admission of Donetsk to the Russian Federation of the People's Republic and the formation of a new subject within the Russian Federation, the powers assigned by the legislation of the Russian Federation to the powers of local self-government bodies are exercised by the administration of the urban district of Donetsk of the Donetsk People's Republic (hereinafter referred to as the administration of the urban district) - in terms of the property necessary to ensure its activities, as well as other property that is in the municipal ownership of the municipal formation of the urban district of Donetsk of the Donetsk People's Republic (treasury, property transferred to operational management, economic management).
Clause 3.1.11 of the Regulations on the Department of Municipal Property of the Administration of the Donetsk City District (hereinafter referred to as the Department of Municipal Property), approved by the decision of the Donetsk City Council of the Donetsk People's Republic dated 01/06/2024 No. 1/14-5, determines that the Department of Municipal Property carries out work on the transfer of ownerless (escheated) real estate to the municipal property of the Donetsk City District.
The procedure for identifying, recording and accepting into the municipal ownership of the municipality the Donetsk City District of the Donetsk People's Republic ownerless real estate of the housing stock is determined by the procedure for identifying, accounting and accepting into the municipal ownership of the municipality the Donetsk City District of the Donetsk People's Republic ownerless real estate of the housing stock, approved by the decision of the Donetsk City Council of the Donetsk People's Republic dated 04.07.2024 No. 1/32-5 (hereinafter - Procedure No. 1/32-5), which was developed in accordance with the Constitution of the Russian Federation, the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, the Constitution of the Donetsk People's Republic, the Law of the Donetsk People's Republic of March 21, 2024 No. 66-RZ “On the specifics of identifying, using and recognizing the right of municipal property of municipalities of the Donetsk People's Republic to residential premises with signs ownerless property located on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic" (hereinafter referred to as Law No. 66-RZ).
According to clause 2.1 of Section II of Order No. 1/32-5, the administration of the city district, represented by the department of municipal property, independently carries out actions to identify, record and accept into municipal ownership ownerless real estate located on the territory of the urban district of Donetsk. Information about residential premises that have signs of ownerlessness may be received by the municipal property department from individuals and legal entities, including public authorities, who have become aware of such property.
According to clause 2.2 of Procedure No. 1/32-5, the signs of ownerless real estate in relation to residential premises are: failure to pay for residential premises and utilities within one year before the day of identification of residential premises that have signs of ownerlessness; lack of information about the registered ownership of residential premises in the Unified State Register of Real Estate; non-use of residential premises, which, among other things, creates a threat to its safety (or an apartment building if the premises is an apartment, a room in an apartment building), safety for the life and health of other persons (including the rights holders of other premises in an apartment building if the premises is an apartment, a room in an apartment building).
