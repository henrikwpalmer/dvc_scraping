In accordance with the requirements of Part 3 of Article 167 of the Civil Procedure Code of the Russian Federation (hereinafter referred to as the Civil Procedure Code of the Russian Federation), the court considers it possible to consider the case in the absence of interested parties.
The court, having examined the case materials, assessing the totality of the evidence presented, considers that, in accordance with Art. 56 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation, each party must prove the circumstances to which it refers as the basis for its claims and objections.
By virtue of Art. 67 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation, the court evaluates evidence according to its internal conviction, based on a comprehensive, complete, objective and direct examination of the evidence available in the case.
According to Part 3 of Art. 218 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, in cases and in the manner provided for by this Code, a person may acquire the right of ownership to property that does not have an owner, to property whose owner is unknown, or to property that the owner has abandoned or to which he has lost the right of ownership on other grounds provided by law.
In accordance with Part 1 of Art. 225 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, ownerless is a thing that does not have an owner, the owner of which is unknown, or the right of ownership to which the owner has refused.
Based on the provisions of Part 3 of Art. 225 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, ownerless real estate is accepted for registration by the body that carries out state registration of rights to real estate, upon application from the local government body in whose territory they are located.
According to Art. 130 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, immovable things (real estate, real estate) include land plots, subsoil plots and everything that is firmly connected to the land, that is, objects whose movement without disproportionate damage to their purpose is impossible, including buildings, structures, unfinished construction objects. Real estate includes residential and non-residential premises, as well as parts of buildings or structures intended to accommodate vehicles (car spaces), if the boundaries of such premises, parts of buildings or structures are described in the manner established by the legislation on state cadastral registration. Items not related to real estate, including money and securities, are recognized as movable property. Registration of rights to movable things is not required, except in cases specified in the law.
Recognition of ownership of an ownerless immovable property is carried out in a special proceeding upon the application of the body authorized to manage municipal property, in accordance with Art. Art. 290-293 Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation.
According to Art. 2 of the Law of the Donetsk People's Republic of March 21, 2024 No. 66-RZ “On the specifics of identifying, using and recognizing the right of municipal property of municipalities of the Donetsk People's Republic to residential premises that have signs of ownerless property located on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic” (hereinafter referred to as Law No. 66-RZ), the signs of ownerless property in relation to residential premises are:
1) failure to pay for residential premises and utilities within one year from the date of identification of residential premises that have signs of ownerlessness;
2) lack of information about the registered ownership of residential premises in the Unified State Register of Real Estate;
3) non-use of residential premises, which, among other things, creates a threat to its safety (or an apartment building if the premises is an apartment, a room in an apartment building), safety for the life and health of other persons (including the rights holders of other premises in an apartment building if the premises is an apartment, a room in an apartment building).
In accordance with Part 1 of Art. 10 of Law No. 66-RZ, a completely similar norm is enshrined in paragraph 2 of part 1 of Article 7 of the Law of the Donetsk People’s Republic of March 21, 2024 No. 66-RZ “On the peculiarities of identifying, using and recognizing the right of municipal property of municipalities of the Donetsk People’s Republic to residential premises with signs of ownerless property located on the territory of the Donetsk People’s Republic”, after three months from the day the residential premises are designated as ownerless by the territorial body of the federal executive body carrying out state cadastral registration, state registration of rights, maintaining the Unified State Register of Real Estate, the authorized body applies to the court with an application for recognition of the right of municipal ownership of the municipal formation to this residential premises in the manner prescribed by the legislation of the Russian Federation.
