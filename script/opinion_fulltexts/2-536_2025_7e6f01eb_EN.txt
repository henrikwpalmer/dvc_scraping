In accordance with paragraph 1 of Article 225 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, an ownerless thing is a thing that does not have an owner or the owner of which is unknown, or a thing for which the owner has renounced the right of ownership.
According to paragraph 1 of Article 226 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, movable things abandoned by the owner or otherwise abandoned by him for the purpose of renouncing ownership of them (abandoned things) can be turned into their property by other persons.
Thus, for the acceptance of a thing into ownership by other persons as ownerless, a necessary condition is to establish the fact of the absence of the owner as such or the owner’s renunciation of his right.
According to Part 1 of Article 293 of the Civil Procedure Code of the Russian Federation, the court, having recognized that the owner has renounced the right of ownership of the movable thing, makes a decision to recognize the movable thing as ownerless and transfer it to the ownership of the person who has taken possession of it.
