In accordance with the requirements of Part 3 of Article 167 of the Civil Procedure Code of the Russian Federation (hereinafter referred to as the Civil Procedure Code of the Russian Federation), the court considers it possible to consider the case in the absence of persons who failed to appear.
The court, having examined the case materials and assessed the totality of the evidence presented, considers the stated demands to be justified and subject to satisfaction, for the following reasons.
According to Art. 56 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation, each party must prove the circumstances to which it refers as the basis for its claims and objections.
By virtue of Art. 67 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation, the court evaluates evidence according to its internal conviction, based on a comprehensive, complete, objective and direct examination of the evidence available in the case.
According to Part 3 of Art. 218 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, in cases and in the manner provided for by this Code, a person may acquire the right of ownership to property that does not have an owner, to property whose owner is unknown, or to property that the owner has abandoned or to which he has lost the right of ownership on other grounds provided by law.
In accordance with Part 1 of Art. 225 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, ownerless is a thing that does not have an owner, the owner of which is unknown, or the right of ownership to which the owner has refused.
Based on the provisions of Part 3 of Art. 225 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, ownerless real estate is accepted for registration by the body that carries out state registration of rights to real estate, upon application from the local government body in whose territory they are located.
According to Art. 130 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, immovable things (real estate, real estate) include land plots, subsoil plots and everything that is firmly connected to the land, that is, objects whose movement without disproportionate damage to their purpose is impossible, including buildings, structures, unfinished construction objects. Real estate includes residential and non-residential premises, as well as parts of buildings or structures intended to accommodate vehicles (car spaces), if the boundaries of such premises, parts of buildings or structures are described in the manner established by the legislation on state cadastral registration. Items not related to real estate, including money and securities, are recognized as movable property. Registration of rights to movable things is not required, except in cases specified in the law.
Recognition of ownership of an ownerless immovable property is carried out in a special proceeding upon the application of the body authorized to manage municipal property, in accordance with Art. Art. 290-293 Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation.
In accordance with Part 1 of Art. 4 of the Federal Constitutional Law of October 4, 2022 No. 5-FKZ “On the admission of the Donetsk People's Republic into the Russian Federation and the formation of a new subject within the Russian Federation - the Donetsk People's Republic” (hereinafter referred to as Law No. 5-FKZ), legislative and other regulatory legal acts of the Russian Federation are valid on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic from the date of admission of the Donetsk People's Republic into the Russian Federation and the formation of of the Russian Federation of a new subject, unless otherwise provided by Law No. 5-FKZ.
According to Part 2 of Art. 4 of Law No. 5-FKZ, the regulatory legal acts of the Donetsk People's Republic are valid on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic until the end of the transition period or until the adoption of the relevant regulatory legal act of the Russian Federation and (or) the regulatory legal act of the Donetsk People's Republic.
At the same time, Art. 21 of Law No. 5-FKZ establishes the specifics of regulating relations in the sphere of real estate turnover on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic.
In accordance with clause 2, part 1, art. 21 of Law No. 5-FKZ, until January 1, 2028, on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic, regulatory legal acts of the Donetsk People's Republic may establish features of the regulation of property relations, including the circulation of land, in agreement with the federal executive body performing the functions of managing state property.
According to Part 7 of Article 12 of Law No. 5-FKZ, management of property located on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic, which has signs of ownerlessness and is not classified as federal property, the property of a constituent entity of the Russian Federation or municipal property, is carried out by the authorities of the Donetsk People's Republic in the manner established by the Government of the Russian Federation, unless otherwise provided by this Federal Constitutional Law. The procedure for recognizing such property as ownerless, established by the regulatory legal acts of the Donetsk People's Republic, is applied until January 1, 2028, in agreement with the federal executive body exercising the functions of managing state property.
Decree of the Acting Head of the Donetsk People's Republic dated 05/10/2023 No. 140 (hereinafter referred to as Decree No. 140 dated 05/10/2023) established the specifics of acquiring state and municipal property rights to ownerless real estate on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic.
According to clause 1.2 of Decree No. 140 dated 10.05.2023, the executive body of the Donetsk People's Republic authorized to manage state property has the right to apply to the court with a demand for recognition of the right of state ownership of the objects defined in subclause 1.1 of this clause (with the exception of residential premises, outbuildings intended to satisfy citizens' household and other needs), after three months from the date of their registration as ownerless real estate by the body carrying out state registration of rights to real estate.
By Decree of the Acting Head of the Donetsk People's Republic dated 02.27.2023 No. 64 “On some issues related to the management of property that has signs of ownerlessness, located on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic, and the recognition of such property as ownerless” (hereinafter referred to as Decree dated 02.27.2023 No. 64), the Government of the Donetsk People's Republic is vested with the authority to establish (adoption) of the procedure for recognizing as ownerless property that has signs of ownerlessness and is not classified as federal property, the property of a constituent entity of the Russian Federation or municipal property located on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic, with the exception of real estate objects in the form of residential premises, residential buildings, and outbuildings.
In accordance with Decrees dated 10.05.2023 No. 140, dated 27.02.2023 No. 64, Decree of the Government of the Donetsk People's Republic dated 22.06.2023 No. 45-5, the executive body of the Donetsk People's Republic authorized to manage state real estate determined the State Property Fund of the Donetsk People's Republic and approved the Procedure for recognition located on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic of real estate ownerless, with the exception of residential buildings, residential premises, garden houses and outbuildings intended to satisfy citizens' household and other needs (hereinafter referred to as the Procedure), which regulates legal relations and establishes the procedure for recognizing property as ownerless on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic, as well as recognizing the right of state ownership of the Donetsk People's Republic to ownerless real estate.
