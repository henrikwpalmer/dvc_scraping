INSTALLED:
The administration of the Amvrosiyevsky municipal district of the Donetsk People's Republic filed an application for recognition of the right of municipal ownership of ownerless property (the interested party is the Office of the Federal Service for State Registration, Cadastre and Cartography for the Donetsk People's Republic), in which it asks the court to recognize the right of municipal ownership of the municipal formation of the Amvrosiyevsky municipal district of the Donetsk People's Republic for ownerless real estate - an apartment located on address: Russian Federation, Donetsk People's Republic, Amvrosievsky municipal district,
<address>
There is no cadastral number. In support of the stated requirements, the applicant indicated that on April 25, 2024, the specified real estate was accepted by the Rosreestr Office for the Donetsk People's Republic for registration as ownerless. Other owners of this real estate are unknown. Thus, by registering an object as ownerless, the territorial body of the federal executive body, which carries out cadastral registration, state registration of rights, maintaining the Unified State Register of Real Estate, confirms the fact that the state registration of the object as ownerless was carried out lawfully. Within 3 months and after 3 months from the moment the object was registered as ownerless, no one declared their rights to this object, that is, there is no dispute about the right.
The representative of the plaintiff Podlesnaya O.I., having been duly notified at the court hearing, did not appear, in her statement she asked that the case be considered without her participation, she insists on satisfying the claims and asks them to be satisfied.
The interested party - the Office of the Federal Service for State Registration, Cadastre and Cartography for the Donetsk People's Republic, having been duly notified, did not send a representative to the court hearing; in their statement they asked to consider the case without the participation of their representative.
By protocol ruling dated February 18, 2025, V.I. Serdyuk was involved as an interested party, who, having been duly notified at the court hearing, did not appear; no statements were received from him, including about postponing the case to the court.
In accordance with Part 5 of Article 167 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation, the court considers it possible to consider the case in the absence of persons who did not appear.
Having studied the case materials, the court comes to the following conclusions.
By virtue of Article 125 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, on behalf of municipalities, by their actions, local government bodies can acquire and exercise rights and obligations within the framework of their competence, established by acts defining the status of these bodies.
According to Part 3 of Article 218 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, in cases and in the manner provided for by the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, a person can acquire ownership of property that does not have an owner, property whose owner is unknown, or property that the owner has abandoned or to which he has lost ownership on other grounds provided by law.
In accordance with Part 1 of Article 225 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, an ownerless thing is one that does not have an owner or the owner of which is unknown or, unless otherwise provided by law, the owner of which has renounced the right of ownership.
According to Part 3 of Article 225 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, ownerless real estate is accepted for registration by the body that carries out state registration of rights to real estate, upon application from the local government body in whose territory they are located.
Ownerless real estate is accepted for registration by the body that carries out state registration of rights to real estate, upon application from the local government body in whose territory they are located. After a year has passed from the date of registration of an ownerless immovable property, the body authorized to manage municipal property may apply to the court with a request to recognize the right of municipal ownership of this thing.
An ownerless immovable property, not recognized by a court decision as having come into municipal ownership, may be again taken into possession, use and disposal by the owner who abandoned it, or acquired into ownership by virtue of acquisitive prescription.
