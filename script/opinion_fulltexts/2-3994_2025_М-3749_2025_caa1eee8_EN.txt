In accordance with paragraph 6 of part 1 of Article 262 of the Civil Procedure Code of the Russian Federation, in a special proceeding the court considers cases of recognizing a movable thing as ownerless and recognizing the right of municipal ownership of an ownerless immovable thing.
By virtue of Part 1 of Article 292 of the Civil Procedure Code of the Russian Federation, the judge, when preparing the case for trial, finds out which persons (owners, actual owners and others) can provide information about the ownership of the property, and also asks the relevant organizations about the information available about it.
As follows from Article 293 of the Civil Procedure Code of the Russian Federation, the court, having recognized that the immovable thing does not have an owner or the owner of the immovable thing is unknown, and it has been registered in the prescribed manner, makes a decision to recognize the right of municipal property or the property of the corresponding city of federal significance to this thing.
This method of acquiring municipal ownership rights to real estate objects is possible in the absence of legal claims to the objects on the part of other persons and the actual possession of this property by these persons.
Part 3 of Article 263 of the Civil Procedure Code of the Russian Federation establishes that if, when filing an application or considering a case in a special proceeding, the existence of a dispute about the right is established, the court issues a ruling to leave the application without consideration, in which it explains to the applicant and other interested parties their right to resolve the dispute in the manner of litigation.
