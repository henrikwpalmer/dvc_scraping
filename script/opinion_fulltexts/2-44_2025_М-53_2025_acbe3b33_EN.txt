Clause 3 of Art. 218 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation establishes that in cases and in the manner provided for by this Code, a person may acquire the right of ownership to property that does not have an owner, to property whose owner is unknown, or to property that the owner has abandoned or to which he has lost the right of ownership on other grounds provided by law.
According to Art. 225 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, ownerless is a thing that does not have an owner, the owner of which is unknown, or the right of ownership to which the owner has refused.

Based on Part 3 of Article 1 of the Federal Law of the Russian Federation dated July 13, 2015 No. 218-FZ “On State Registration of Real Estate” (hereinafter referred to as Law No. 218-FZ), state registration of rights to real estate is a legal act of recognition and confirmation of the emergence, change, transition, termination of a certain person’s right to real estate or restrictions on such a right and encumbrance of real estate (hereinafter referred to as state registration of rights).
In accordance with Part 3 of Article 3 of Law No. 218-FZ, the competence of the rights registration authority when carrying out state cadastral registration and state registration of rights includes, among other things:
checking the validity of the documents submitted by the applicant and the availability of the corresponding rights of the person or authority who prepared the document;
checking the availability of previously registered and previously declared rights;
registration of ownerless real estate in the manner established by the regulatory body.
Thus, by placing real estate at the address:
Russian Federation, Donetsk People's Republic,
<address>
to be registered as ownerless by the territorial body of the federal executive body carrying out state cadastral registration, state registration of rights, maintenance of the Unified State Register of Real Estate, confirms the fact that state registration as ownerless was carried out lawfully.
Part 1 of Article 10 of the Law of the Donetsk People's Republic of March 21, 2024
No. 66-RZ “On the peculiarities of identifying, using and recognizing the right of municipal property of municipalities of the Donetsk People’s Republic to residential premises with signs of ownerless property located on the territory of the Donetsk People’s Republic” (hereinafter referred to as Law No. 66-RZ) it is determined that after three months from the date of registration of a residential premises as ownerless by the territorial body of the federal executive body carrying out the state cadastral accounting, state registration of rights, maintenance of the Unified State Register of Real Estate, the authorized body applies to the court with an application for recognition of the right of municipal property of a municipal entity to this residential premises in the manner prescribed by the legislation of the Russian Federation.
Guided by Decree No. 140 dated May 10, 2023, in accordance with paragraph 12 of the Procedure, after three months from the date of registration with the authorized territorial body of the federal executive body carrying out state cadastral registration, state registration of rights, maintaining the Unified State Register of Real Estate as an ownerless immovable thing, with the exception of linear objects (including their integral technological parts), MIZO DPR has the right to apply to the judicial authorities with an application to recognize the right of state ownership of an ownerless immovable property.
After the expiration of the above period from the date of registration of the ownerless immovable property, any individuals and legal entities have claims regarding property at the address: Russian Federation, Donetsk People's Republic,
<address>
, which is the subject of this application, was not declared, that is, there is no dispute about the right.
Under such circumstances, taking into account that the applicant carried out all the activities in compliance with the deadlines and norms of the current legislation and as of today the right to this object has not been declared, and it has all the signs of ownerless property, the court comes to the conclusion that the stated requirements have been satisfied in full.
