“On some issues arising in judicial practice when resolving disputes related to the protection of property rights and other property rights” it is stated that a person who considers himself the owner of real estate in his possession, the right to which is registered for another entity, has the right to apply to the court with a claim for recognition of ownership rights. Recognition of ownership of property that is not in the direct possession of the plaintiff is possible only by filing a claim to recover property from someone else’s illegal possession. When considering a vindication claim, it is possible to establish the good faith of the acquisition of property and its proper owner, the connection of right and actual ownership, as well as the protection of the owner by the rules of limitation, which guarantees to all parties to the dispute the protection of their rights, interests, as well as the stability of civil circulation.
According to Part 3 of Art. 218 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, in cases and in the manner provided for by this Code, a person may acquire the right of ownership to property that does not have an owner, to property whose owner is unknown, or to property that the owner has abandoned or to which he has lost the right of ownership on other grounds provided by law.
In accordance with Part 3 of Art. 225 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, ownerless real estate is accepted for registration by the body that carries out state registration of rights to real estate, upon application from the local government body in whose territory they are located.

According to Art. 290 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation, an application to recognize a movable thing as ownerless is submitted to the court by the person who took possession of it at the place of residence or location of the applicant.
