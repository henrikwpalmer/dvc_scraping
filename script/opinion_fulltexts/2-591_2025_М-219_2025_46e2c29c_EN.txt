From the analysis of the provisions of paragraph 1 of part 1 of article 262, part 3 of article 263, articles 264, 265 and 267 of the Civil Procedure Code of the Russian Federation, it follows that the courts can accept applications for establishing facts and consider them in a special proceeding if the applicant’s right is indisputable, but the lack of proper confirmation of circumstances significant for the exercise of this right prevents the exercise of a person’s rights, and if the establishment of a fact is not associated with the subsequent resolution of a dispute about the right, subordinate to the court.

Thus, since in this case there is a dispute about the applicant’s right to real estate, the application of the administration of the city of Mariupol of the Donetsk People’s Republic to recognize the right of municipal ownership of an ownerless real estate, by virtue of Part 3 of Article 263 of the Civil Procedure Code of the Russian Federation, cannot be considered in a special proceeding, and therefore is subject to consideration in a claim procedure, according to the norms established for this type of legal proceedings.
In connection with the above, the court considers it legitimate to leave the application of the administration of the city of Mariupol of the Donetsk People's Republic to recognize the right of municipal ownership of an ownerless real estate without consideration.
Leaving the application of the Administration of the city of Mariupol without consideration due to the existence of a dispute about the right does not prevent the applicant from filing a lawsuit.
