According to Part 1 of Art. 130 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, immovable things (real estate, real estate) include land plots, subsoil plots and everything that is firmly connected to the land, that is, objects whose movement without disproportionate damage to their purpose is impossible, including buildings, structures, unfinished construction projects.
Real estate includes residential and non-residential premises, as well as parts of buildings or structures intended to accommodate vehicles (car spaces), if the boundaries of such premises, parts of buildings or structures are described in the manner established by the legislation on state cadastral registration.
Part 3 of Article 218 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation provides that in cases and in the manner provided for by this Code, a person may acquire ownership of property that does not have an owner, of property whose owner is unknown, or of property that the owner has abandoned or to which he has lost ownership on other grounds provided by law.
According to Part 1 of Art. 225 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, ownerless is a thing that does not have an owner or the owner of which is unknown or, unless otherwise provided by law, the owner of which has renounced the right of ownership.
The procedure for considering applications for recognizing a movable thing as ownerless and recognizing the right of ownership of an ownerless immovable thing is regulated by the rules of Chapter 33 of the Civil Procedure Code of the Russian Federation.
According to clause 6, part 1, art. 262 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation, in a special proceeding, the court considers cases of recognizing a movable thing as ownerless and recognizing the right of municipal ownership of an ownerless immovable thing.
According to Part 2 of Art. 293 Chapter 33 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation, the court, having recognized that the immovable thing does not have an owner or the owner of the immovable thing is unknown and it has been registered in the prescribed manner, makes a decision to recognize the right of municipal ownership or the property of a federal city of Moscow, St. Petersburg or Sevastopol for this thing.
Article 2 of the Law of the Donetsk People's Republic of March 21, 2024 No. 66-RZ, establishes that the signs of ownerless property in relation to residential premises are: failure to pay for residential premises and utilities within one year before the day of identification of residential premises that have signs of ownerlessness; lack of information about the registered ownership of residential premises in the Unified State Register of Real Estate; non-use of residential premises, which, among other things, creates a threat to its safety (or an apartment building if the premises is an apartment, a room in an apartment building), safety for the life and health of other persons (including the rights holders of other premises in an apartment building if the premises is an apartment, a room in an apartment building).
Thus, the law provides for the recognition of the right of municipal ownership of ownerless property and there are necessary and sufficient conditions for recognizing an ownerless immovable property as an object of municipal property if it is confirmed that payment for residential premises and utilities has not been paid within one year before the date of identification of the residential premises; there is no information about the registered ownership of residential premises in the Unified State Register of Real Estate; non-use of residential premises, which, among other things, poses a threat to its safety (or an apartment building if the premises is an apartment, a room in an apartment building), safety for the life and health of other persons (including copyright holders of other premises in an apartment building if the premises are an apartment, a room in an apartment building) and the specified real estate object is duly registered as an ownerless real estate object by the Office of the Federal State Registration Service, cadastre and cartography for the Donetsk People's Republic.

Since no one has declared their rights to them since the day the disputed object was registered, the administration of the Donetsk urban district of the Donetsk People's Republic has the right to apply to the court for recognition of the right of municipal ownership to it.
