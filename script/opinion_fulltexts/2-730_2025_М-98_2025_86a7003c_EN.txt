By virtue of Part 1 of Article 3 of the Civil Procedure Code of the Russian Federation, an interested person has the right, in the manner established by the legislation on civil proceedings, to apply to the court for the protection of violated or disputed rights, freedoms or legitimate interests.
In accordance with clause 6, part 1, art. 262 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation, by way of special proceedings, the court considers cases of recognizing a movable thing as ownerless and recognizing the right of municipal ownership of an ownerless immovable thing.
According to the provisions of Art. 263 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation, cases of special proceedings are considered and resolved by the court according to the general rules of claim proceedings with the features established by this chapter and Chapters 28 - 38 of this Code. The court considers cases of special proceedings with the participation of applicants and other interested persons. If, when filing an application or considering a case in a special proceeding, the existence of a dispute about the right is established, the court issues a ruling to leave the application without consideration, in which it explains to the applicant and other interested parties their right to resolve the dispute in the manner of adjudication proceedings.
From the above provisions of the law it follows that courts can accept applications and consider them in a special proceeding if there is no dispute about the law within the jurisdiction of the court.
