Having examined the presented materials of the case, evaluating in accordance with Art. 67 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation, the relevance, admissibility, reliability of each evidence separately, as well as the sufficiency and interconnection of evidence in their totality, the court comes to the following.
According to Article 4 of the Federal Constitutional Law of the Russian Federation of October 4, 2022 No. 5-FKZ, “On the admission into the Russian Federation of the Donetsk People's Republic and the formation within the Russian Federation of a new subject - the Donetsk People's Republic” (hereinafter referred to as Law No. 5-FKZ), legislative and other regulatory legal acts of the Russian Federation are valid on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic from the date of admission of the Donetsk People's Republic to the Russian Federation of the People's Republic and the formation of a new subject within the Russian Federation, unless otherwise provided by this Federal Constitutional Law.
Regulatory legal acts of the Donetsk People's Republic are valid on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic until the end of the transition period or until the adoption of the corresponding regulatory legal act of the Russian Federation and (or) the regulatory legal act of the Donetsk People's Republic.
According to Part 7 of Article 12 of Law No. 5-FKZ, management of property located on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic, which has signs of ownerlessness and is not classified as federal property, the property of a constituent entity of the Russian Federation or municipal property, is carried out by the authorities of the Donetsk People's Republic in the manner established by the Government of the Russian Federation, unless otherwise provided by this Federal Constitutional Law.
The procedure for recognizing such property as ownerless, established by the regulatory legal acts of the Donetsk People's Republic, is applied until January 1, 2028, in agreement with the federal executive body exercising the functions of managing state property.
In accordance with clause 6, part 1, art. 262 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation, by way of special proceedings, the court considers cases of recognizing a movable thing as ownerless and recognizing the right of municipal ownership of an ownerless immovable thing.
By virtue of p.ch. 1, 2 tbsp. 264 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation, the court establishes the facts on which the emergence, change, or termination of personal or property rights of citizens and organizations depends. The court considers cases to establish the fact of ownership and use of real estate.
According to paragraph 3 of Art. 218 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, in cases and in the manner provided for by this Code, a person may acquire the right of ownership to property that does not have an owner, to property whose owner is unknown, or to property that the owner has abandoned or to which he has lost the right of ownership on other grounds provided by law.
In accordance with the provisions of Art. 225 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, ownerless is a thing that does not have an owner or the owner of which is unknown or, unless otherwise provided by law, the owner of which has renounced the right of ownership.
Ownerless real estate is accepted for registration by the body that carries out state registration of rights to real estate, upon application from the local government body in whose territory they are located.
After a year has passed from the date of registration of an ownerless immovable property, and in the case of registration of a linear object, after three months from the date of registration, the body authorized to manage municipal property may apply to the court with a request to recognize the right of municipal ownership of this thing.
According to Part 2 of Art. 290 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation, an application for recognition of ownership of an ownerless immovable thing is submitted to the court at its location by the body authorized to manage municipal property or property owned by the federal city of Moscow, St. Petersburg or Sevastopol.
Within the meaning of this norm, the local government body is the only body that has the right to submit an application for registration of property as ownerless, and therefore is obliged to do so.
According to the legal meaning of the above rules, it follows that the establishment of the fact of municipal property has legal significance for the applicant; the emergence, change, or termination of personal or property rights depends on it, and therefore this fact can be established in a special proceeding.
At the same time, in accordance with Art. 2 of the Law of the Donetsk People's Republic "On the specifics of identifying, using and recognizing the right of municipal property of municipalities of the Donetsk People's Republic to residential premises that have signs of ownerless property located on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic" dated March 21, 2024 No. 66-RZ, the signs of ownerless property in relation to residential premises are:
- failure to pay for residential premises and utilities within one year from the date of identification of residential premises that have signs of ownerlessness;
- lack of information about the registered ownership of residential premises in the Unified State Register of Real Estate;
- non-use of residential premises, which, among other things, creates a threat to its safety (or an apartment building if the premises is an apartment, a room in an apartment building), safety for the life and health of other persons (including the rights holders of other premises in an apartment building if the premises is an apartment, a room in an apartment building).
In case of receiving information about residential premises that have signs of ownerless property, the authorized body in accordance with Art. 5 of the Law performs the following actions:
1) requests information on the official website of the Federal Tax Service of the Russian Federation (https://www.nalog.gov.ru/) - about the availability in the Unified State Register of Legal Entities of information about a legal entity that is a possible owner of residential premises (if it is necessary to establish the status of a legal entity);
2) sends requests to confirm information about identified residential premises that have signs of ownerlessness:
a) to the territorial body of the federal executive body carrying out state cadastral registration, state registration of rights, maintenance of the Unified State Register of Real Estate - about the existence of registered rights to residential premises that have signs of ownerlessness, and their restrictions (encumbrances), as well as about the existence of registered rights to a land plot, if the residential premises are integrally connected with the land plot;
b) to state and municipal enterprises that provide services to legal entities and individuals in the field of housing and communal services - on the fulfillment of the burden of maintaining residential premises that have signs of ownerlessness;
c) to the relevant bodies for accounting of state and municipal property, which maintain registers of federal property, state property of the Donetsk People's Republic and municipal property;
d) if necessary, to the civil registry authorities about the availability of an act record of the death of the last owner of a residential premises that has signs of ownerlessness, to other institutions, organizations, enterprises;
3) within 10 calendar days from the date of discovery of a residential premises that has signs of ownerlessness, places on the official website of the local government body on the Internet, as well as in accessible places at the entrance to the residential premises, an announcement about the identification of a residential premises that has signs of ownerlessness, and about the need for personal appearance before the authorized body of the person (persons) who considers themselves its owner or has (have) rights to it, within 30 calendar days from the date of placement of such an advertisement with the following documents:
a) a passport of a citizen of the Russian Federation or other identification document;
b) title (title) documents for residential premises;
4) after 30 calendar days from the date of publication of the announcement, in the event of the absence of the person (persons) who considers themselves the owner of the residential premises, and after receiving information from the website (if necessary, obtaining such information) specified in paragraph 1 of this article, responses from the authorities specified in paragraph 2 of this article, indicating that the residential premises, which has signs of ownerless property, do not have an owner:
a) within 5 working days, draws up a report on the impossibility of identifying the owner of this residential premises;
b) organizes, if necessary, in the prescribed manner, the carrying out of cadastral work in relation to residential premises that have signs of being ownerless, as well as assigning a postal address to it;
c) if necessary, collects other documents confirming the presence of signs of ownerless property in the residential premises, including documents indicating the absence of residents in such residential premises (inspection reports, extracts from house books, personal accounts, etc.);
d) organizes a commission inspection of a residential premises that has signs of ownerlessness (with an on-site visit), with the involvement of the necessary representatives of the executive bodies of the Donetsk People's Republic and territorial bodies of the federal executive authorities, organizations (as agreed) and with the drawing up of an appropriate act, which indicates the details of the act, the date of the inspection, information about the persons involved, the location, technical and other condition of the residential premises, an inventory of furniture, household appliances and other movable property located in the residential premises, as well as other information indicating the presence of signs of ownerlessness of the residential premises.
If free access to the interior of the inspected residential premises is possible, the act provided for in paragraph one of this subclause reflects information about the internal condition of such premises.
In the absence of free access to the interior of the inspected residential premises, if the non-use of such premises creates a threat to its reliability and safety (or an apartment building if the premises are an apartment in an apartment building), or safety for the life and health of other persons (including the rights holders of other premises in an apartment building if the premises are an apartment in an apartment building), then access to such residential premises for the purpose of its inspection and drawing up an act provided for in paragraph one of this subclause, is carried out by opening it in the presence of representatives of the relevant departments (branches, points) of the police in the manner determined by the local government bodies of the Donetsk People's Republic.
The act provided for in paragraph one of this subclause is accompanied by photo and video photographs of the residential premises and the movable property located in it.
