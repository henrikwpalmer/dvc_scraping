“On some issues arising in judicial practice when resolving disputes related to the protection of property rights and other property rights” it is stated that a person who considers himself the owner of real estate in his possession, the right to which is registered for another entity, has the right to apply to the court with a claim for recognition of ownership rights. Recognition of ownership of property that is not in the direct possession of the plaintiff is possible only by filing a claim to recover property from someone else’s illegal possession. When considering a vindication claim, it is possible to establish the good faith of the acquisition of property and its proper owner, the connection of right and actual ownership, as well as the protection of the owner by the rules of limitation, which guarantees to all parties to the dispute the protection of their rights, interests, as well as the stability of civil circulation.
According to Part 3 of Art. 218 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, in cases and in the manner provided for by this Code, a person may acquire the right of ownership to property that does not have an owner, to property whose owner is unknown, or to property that the owner has abandoned or to which he has lost the right of ownership on other grounds provided by law.
In accordance with Part 3 of Art. 225 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, ownerless real estate is accepted for registration by the body that carries out state registration of rights to real estate, upon application from the local government body in whose territory they are located.

A standard procedure has been established for identifying, recording and accepting ownerless real estate and escheatable property into municipal ownership (hereinafter referred to as the Standard Procedure).
According to clause 2.2. According to the standard procedure, the administrations of districts and cities of the Donetsk People's Republic independently carry out actions to identify, record and accept ownerless real estate into the municipal ownership of the administrative-territorial units of the Donetsk People's Republic on the territory of which they are located.
According to clause 2.9 of the Standard Procedure, within 10 calendar days from the date of discovery of an object of real estate that has signs of ownerlessness, an announcement is posted on the official website of the administration on the Internet about the identification of a real estate item in the relevant administrative-territorial unit of the Donetsk People's Republic that has signs of ownerlessness, and about the need for the appearance of a person (persons) who considers themselves its owner or has (have) ownership of it rights, contact the administration with documents excluding the possibility of considering the specified real estate object ownerless. If the summoned person(s) fail to appear, the specified immovable thing will, at the request of the administration, be registered with the body that carries out state registration of real rights to real estate and their encumbrances, as ownerless. According to Art. 290 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation, an application to recognize a movable thing as ownerless is submitted to the court by the person who took possession of it at the place of residence or location of the applicant. An application for recognition of ownership of an ownerless immovable property is submitted to the court at its location by the body authorized to manage municipal property or property owned by the federal city of Moscow, St. Petersburg or Sevastopol.
According to Part 1 of Article 291 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation, an application to recognize a movable thing as ownerless must indicate which movable thing is subject to recognition as ownerless, its main features must be described, as well as provide evidence indicating that the owner has renounced ownership of it, and evidence indicating that the applicant has taken possession of this thing.
