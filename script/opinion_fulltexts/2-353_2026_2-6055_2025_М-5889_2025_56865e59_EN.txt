Clause 1.1. Decree of the Head of the Donetsk People's Republic No. 140 of May 10, 2023 “On the specifics of acquiring the right of state and municipal property to ownerless real estate on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic” (hereinafter referred to as Decree No. 140 of May 10, 2023), it is established that the body and officials of the Donetsk People's Republic exercising the powers assigned by the legislation of the Russian Federation to the powers bodies of local self-government, before the completion of the formation of bodies of local self-government, the local government body of a municipal formation in the Donetsk People's Republic, authorized to manage municipal property, has the right to apply to the court with a demand for recognition of the right of municipal ownership of ownerless buildings, structures (except for linear objects), premises, objects of unfinished construction that do not have an owner or the owner of which is unknown or the ownership of which the owner has refused, located on the territory of such municipal formation, after three months from the date of their registration as ownerless real estate by the body carrying out state registration of rights to real estate, unless otherwise provided by Decree No. 140 of May 10, 2023.
According to Part 1 of Art. 130 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, immovable things (real estate, real estate) include land plots, subsoil plots and everything that is firmly connected to the land, that is, objects whose movement without disproportionate damage to their purpose is impossible, including buildings, structures, unfinished construction projects.
Real estate includes residential and non-residential premises, as well as parts of buildings or structures intended to accommodate vehicles (car spaces), if the boundaries of such premises, parts of buildings or structures are described in the manner established by the legislation on state cadastral registration.
Part 3 of Article 218 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation provides that in cases and in the manner provided for by this Code, a person may acquire ownership of property that does not have an owner, of property whose owner is unknown, or of property that the owner has abandoned or to which he has lost ownership on other grounds provided by law.
According to Part 1 of Art. 225 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, ownerless is a thing that does not have an owner or the owner of which is unknown or, unless otherwise provided by law, the right of ownership to which the owner has refused.
The procedure for considering applications for recognizing a movable thing as ownerless and recognizing the right of ownership of an ownerless immovable thing is regulated by the rules of Chapter 33 of the Civil Procedure Code of the Russian Federation.
According to clause 6, part 1, art. 262 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation, in a special proceeding, the court considers cases of recognizing a movable thing as ownerless and recognizing the right of municipal ownership of an ownerless immovable thing.
According to Part 2 of Art. 293 Chapter 33 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation, the court, having recognized that the immovable thing does not have an owner or the owner of the immovable thing is unknown and it has been registered in the prescribed manner, makes a decision to recognize the right of municipal ownership or the property of a federal city of Moscow, St. Petersburg or Sevastopol for this thing.
Since from the day the disputed object was registered, no one declared their rights to them, the applicant had the right to apply to the court for recognition of the right to municipal ownership.
In addition, an application to recognize an ownerless immovable property as an object of municipal property was submitted by the body authorized to manage municipal property.
