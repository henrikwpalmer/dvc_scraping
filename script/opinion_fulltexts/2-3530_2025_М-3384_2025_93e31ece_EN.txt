According to Part 1 of Art. 130 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, immovable things (real estate, real estate) include land plots, subsoil plots and everything that is firmly connected to the land, that is, objects whose movement without disproportionate damage to their purpose is impossible, including buildings, structures, unfinished construction projects.
Real estate includes residential and non-residential premises, as well as parts of buildings or structures intended to accommodate vehicles (car spaces), if the boundaries of such premises, parts of buildings or structures are described in the manner established by the legislation on state cadastral registration.
Paragraph 3 of Article 218 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation provides that in cases and in the manner provided for by this Code, a person may acquire ownership of property that does not have an owner, of property whose owner is unknown, or of property that the owner has abandoned or to which he has lost ownership on other grounds provided by law.
According to paragraph 1 of Art. 225 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, an ownerless thing is a thing that does not have an owner or the owner of which is unknown or, unless otherwise provided by law, the right of ownership to which the owner has refused.
By virtue of clause 3 of Art. 225 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, ownerless real estate is accepted for registration by the body that carries out state registration of rights to real estate, upon application from the local government body in whose territory they are located. After a year has passed from the date of registration of an ownerless immovable property, and in the case of registration of a linear object, after three months from the date of registration, the body authorized to manage municipal property may apply to the court with a request to recognize the right of municipal ownership of this thing.

Based on Part 3 of Article 1 of the Federal Law of the Russian Federation dated July 13, 2015 No. 218-FZ “On State Registration of Real Estate” (hereinafter referred to as Law No. 218-FZ), state registration of rights to real estate is a legal act of recognition and confirmation of the emergence, change, transition, termination of a certain person’s right to real estate or restrictions on such a right and encumbrance of real estate (hereinafter referred to as state registration of rights).
In accordance with Part 3 of Article 3 of Law No. 218-FZ, the competence of the rights registration body when carrying out state cadastral registration and state registration of rights includes, among other things:
checking the validity of the documents submitted by the applicant and the availability of the corresponding rights of the person or authority who prepared the document;
checking the availability of previously registered and previously declared rights;
registration of ownerless real estate in the manner established by the regulatory body.
Thus, by placing real estate at the address:
<address>
to be registered as ownerless, the territorial body of the federal executive body carrying out state cadastral registration, state registration of rights, maintaining the Unified State Register of Real Estate, confirms the fact that state registration as ownerless was carried out lawfully.
By virtue of Art. 210 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, the owner bears the burden of maintaining the property belonging to him, unless otherwise provided by law or contract. The Constitutional Court of the Russian Federation in paragraph 3.1 of the ruling dated November 2, 2006 No. 444-O explained that this obligation includes, among other things, the incurrence of expenses for maintaining property in proper condition (major and current repairs, insurance, registration, security, etc.) and the fulfillment of other requirements emanating from state and local government bodies authorized by law.
According to Art. 12 Federal Law dated
DD.MM.YYYY
No. 390-FZ “On Security”, local government bodies, within their competence, ensure the implementation of the legislation of the Russian Federation in the field of security.
According to paragraph 5 of Section I of Procedure No. I/32-4, the main goals and objectives of identifying ownerless real estate objects and registering municipal ownership rights to them are: involving unused property in free civil circulation; ensuring normal and safe technical operation of the property; improvement and proper maintenance of the territory of the Donetsk urban district.
Subparagraph 2 of paragraph 14 of Section II of Procedure No. I/32-4 establishes, inter alia, the need for personal appearance at the Department of a person (persons) who considers himself the owner of an immovable thing that has signs of being ownerless or who has rights to it with the following documents:

Clause 1.1 of clause 1 of Decree No. 140 establishes, inter alia, that the local government body of a municipal formation in the Donetsk People's Republic, authorized to manage municipal property, has the right to apply to the court with a demand for recognition of the right of municipal ownership of ownerless buildings, structures (except for linear objects), premises, objects of unfinished construction that do not have an owner or the owner of which is unknown or from the ownership of which the owner refused, located on the territory of such a municipal entity, after three months from the date of their registration by the body carrying out state registration of rights to real estate.
According to clause 6, part 1, art. 262 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation, in a special proceeding, the court considers cases of recognizing a movable thing as ownerless and recognizing the right of municipal ownership of an ownerless immovable thing.
By virtue of Part 2 of Art. 293 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation, the court, having recognized that the immovable thing does not have an owner or the owner of the immovable thing is unknown and it has been registered in the prescribed manner, makes a decision to recognize the right of municipal property or the property of the federal city of Moscow, St. Petersburg or Sevastopol to this thing.
Taking into account the circumstances of the case, the court considers the application to be satisfied in full.
