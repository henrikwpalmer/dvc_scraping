In accordance with Part 5 of Art. 167 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation, the court considers it possible to consider the case in the absence of the parties.
Having examined the materials of the civil case, evaluating the evidence according to its inner conviction, based on a comprehensive, complete, objective and direct study of the evidence available in the case, its relevance, admissibility, reliability of each evidence separately, as well as the sufficiency and interconnection of the evidence in its entirety, the court comes to the following.
In accordance with Part 3 of Art. 218 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, in cases and in the manner provided for by the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, a person may acquire the right of ownership to property that does not have an owner, to property whose owner is unknown, or to property that the owner has abandoned or to which he has lost the right of ownership on other grounds provided by law.
Parts 1, 3 of Article 225 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation stipulate that an ownerless thing is a thing that does not have an owner or the owner of which is unknown or, unless otherwise provided by law, the right of ownership to which the owner has refused. Ownerless real estate is accepted for registration by the body that carries out state registration of rights to real estate, upon application from the local government body in whose territory they are located.
According to the Federal Constitutional Law of October 4, 2022 No. 5-FKZ “On the admission of the Donetsk People's Republic into the Russian Federation and the formation of a new entity within the Russian Federation - the Donetsk People's Republic” (hereinafter referred to as Law No. 5-FKZ), the Agreement between the Russian Federation and the Donetsk People's Republic on the admission of the Donetsk People's Republic to the Russian Federation and the formation of a new entity within the Russian Federation, The Donetsk People's Republic is considered admitted to the Russian Federation on September 30, 2022.
In accordance with Art. 4 of Law No. 5-FKZ, legislative and other regulatory legal acts of the Russian Federation are in force on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic from the date of admission of the Donetsk People's Republic to the Russian Federation and the formation of a new subject within the Russian Federation, unless otherwise provided by this Federal Constitutional Law.
Regulatory legal acts of the Donetsk People's Republic are valid on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic until the end of the transition period or until the adoption of the corresponding regulatory legal act of the Russian Federation and (or) the regulatory legal act of the Donetsk People's Republic.
In accordance with Part 7 of Art. 12 of the said Federal Constitutional Law, management of property located on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic, which has signs of ownerlessness and is not classified as federal property, the property of a constituent entity of the Russian Federation or municipal property, is carried out by the bodies of the Donetsk People's Republic in the manner established by the Government of the Russian Federation, unless otherwise provided by this Federal Constitutional Law. The procedure for recognizing such property as ownerless, established by the regulatory legal acts of the Donetsk People's Republic, is applied until January 1, 2028, in agreement with the federal executive body exercising the functions of managing state property.
In order to implement Part 7 of Art. 12 of this Federal Constitutional Law, the Decree of the Head of the Donetsk People's Republic dated May 10, 2023 No. 140 “On the peculiarities of acquiring the right of state and municipal property to ownerless real estate on the territory of the Donetsk People’s Republic” was adopted, which established that the body and officials of the Donetsk People’s Republic exercising powers classified by the legislation of the Russian Federation as the powers of local self-government bodies, until the completion of the formation of local authorities self-government, a local government body of a municipal formation in the Donetsk People's Republic, authorized to manage municipal property, has the right to go to court with a demand for recognition of the right of municipal ownership of ownerless buildings, structures (except for linear objects), premises, objects of unfinished construction that do not have an owner or the owner of which is unknown or the owner of which has refused ownership, located on the territory of such a municipal formation, after three months from the date their registration as ownerless real estate by the body carrying out state registration of rights to real estate, unless otherwise provided by this Decree (clause 1.1 of Decree No. 140).
On the date of admission of the Donetsk People's Republic to the Russian Federation, Decree of the Head of the Donetsk People's Republic dated April 28, 2021 No. 116 “On the identification, registration and acceptance into municipal ownership of ownerless real estate and escheatable property” was in force on the territory of the subject, which approved the Standard procedure for identifying, recording and accepting ownerless real estate and escheatable property into municipal ownership, currently in force time as amended, taking into account the changes made by Decree of the Head of the Donetsk People's Republic No. 64 of February 27, 2023, which came into force on August 18, 2023.
Clause 1 of the Standard Procedure establishes that it was adopted to determine a unified procedure for identifying, registering, managing and accepting into municipal ownership ownerless real estate and escheat property identified on the territory of the corresponding administrative-territorial unit of the Donetsk People's Republic.
The subject of regulation of this Procedure are residential premises, garden houses, outbuildings intended to satisfy citizens' household and other needs, having signs of ownerless things, escheated property in the form of residential premises, land plots, as well as buildings, structures, and other real estate objects located on them, shares in the right of common shared ownership of these real estate objects, as well as movable property identified on such real estate objects.
The specified Standard Procedure provides for the registration as ownerless property of residential premises, garden houses, outbuildings intended to satisfy citizens' household and other needs, that is, real estate that does not have an owner or the owner of which is unknown, or the ownership of which the owner has refused, including by ceasing to bear the burden of maintaining such property (in particular, through non-payment of utilities for the last three years), provided that the owner has been present for the last six months outside the territory of the Russian Federation (clause 2.3 of the Standard Procedure).
According to para. 2 clause 1.3 of the Standard Procedure, an individual or legal entity may renounce the ownership of property owned by him by declaring this or taking other actions that definitely indicate his removal from the possession, use and disposal of property without the intention of retaining any rights to this property, including by ceasing to bear the burden of maintaining such property (in particular, through non-payment of utilities for the last three years), provided that the owner has been outside the territory for the last six months Russian Federation.
By the Decree of the People's Council of the Donetsk People's Republic of March 21, 2024, within the framework of the powers and tasks established by the Decree of the Government of the Russian Federation of December 29, 2022 No. 2501 “On approval of the features of management and disposal of individual property located in the territories of the Donetsk People's Republic, Lugansk People's Republic, Zaporozhye region and Kherson region, located in state or municipal ownership, as well as the delimitation of property between the Russian Federation, each of the specified constituent entities of the Russian Federation and its municipalities", Law No. 66-RZ was adopted "On the specifics of identifying, using and recognizing the right of municipal property of municipal entities of the Donetsk People's Republic to residential premises that have signs of ownerless property located on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic", which established the specifics of the procedure for identifying, recording and using residential premises that have signs of ownerlessness property located on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic, recognition of the right of municipal ownership of municipal formations of the Donetsk People's Republic to this property, as well as the procedure for disposing of this property.
In accordance with Part 2 of Art. 1 of this Law, its effect extends to residential premises in the form of residential buildings, apartments, rooms.
According to Art. 2 of Law No. 66-RZ, the signs of ownerless property in relation to residential premises are: failure to pay for residential premises and utilities within one year before the date of identification of a residential premises that has signs of ownerlessness; lack of information about the registered ownership of residential premises in the Unified State Register of Real Estate; non-use of residential premises, which, among other things, creates a threat to its safety (or an apartment building if the premises is an apartment, a room in an apartment building), safety for the life and health of other persons (including the rights holders of other premises in an apartment building if the premises is an apartment, a room in an apartment building).
In case of receiving information about residential premises that have signs of ownerless property, the authorized body in accordance with Part 3 of Art. 5 of Law No. 66-RZ, within 10 calendar days from the date of discovery of a residential premises that has signs of ownerlessness, places on the official website of the local government body on the Internet, as well as in accessible places at the entrance to the residential premises, an announcement about the identification of a residential premises that has signs of ownerlessness, and about the need for personal appearance before the authorized body of the person (persons) who considers themselves its owner or has (have) rights to it, within 30 calendar days from the date of placement of such an advertisement with the following documents:
a) a passport of a citizen of the Russian Federation or other identification document;
b) title documents for residential premises.
Part 4 of the same article provides that after 30 calendar days from the date of publication of the announcement, in the event of the absence of a person (persons) who considers themselves the owner of the residential premises, and after receiving information indicating that the residential premises, which have signs of ownerless property, do not have an owner:
a) within 5 working days, draws up a report on the impossibility of identifying the owner of this residential premises;
b) organizes, if necessary, in the prescribed manner, the carrying out of cadastral work in relation to residential premises that have signs of being ownerless, as well as assigning a postal address to it;
c) if necessary, collects other documents confirming the presence of signs of ownerless property in the residential premises, including documents indicating the absence of residents in such residential premises (inspection reports, extracts from house books, personal accounts, etc.);
d) organizes a commission inspection of a residential premises that has signs of ownerlessness (with an on-site visit), with the involvement of the necessary representatives of the executive bodies of the Donetsk People's Republic and territorial bodies of the federal executive authorities, organizations (as agreed) and with the drawing up of an appropriate act, which indicates the details of the act, the date of the inspection, information about the persons involved, the location, technical and other condition of the residential premises, an inventory of furniture, household appliances and other movable property located in the residential premises, as well as other information indicating the presence of signs of ownerlessness of the residential premises.
By virtue of Art. 9 of Law No. 66-RZ, the procedure for recognizing ownerless residential premises as municipal property is terminated upon personal announcement of the owner of such premises and confirmation of ownership of such premises on the basis of the documents provided for in paragraphs. pp. “a” and “b” clause 3 of Art. 5 of this Law (passport of a citizen of the Russian Federation or other identification document; title documents for residential premises).
In accordance with Part 1 of Art. 10 of Law No. 66-RZ, after three months from the date of registration of the residential premises as ownerless by the territorial body of the federal executive body carrying out state cadastral registration, state registration of rights, maintenance of the Unified State Register of Real Estate, the authorized body applies to the court with an application for recognition of the right of municipal ownership of the municipal formation to this residential premises in the manner prescribed by the legislation of the Russian Federation.
Similar provisions are set out in clause 3.1 of the Standard Procedure, approved by Decree of the Head of the Donetsk People's Republic No. 116 dated April 28, 2021, which establishes that after three months from the date of registration of an ownerless real estate property, the administration is obliged to file a claim in court to recognize the right of municipal ownership of this thing.
Moreover, by virtue of clause 3.2 of the Standard Procedure, if before the acceptance of an ownerless immovable property into municipal ownership, a person (persons) who considers themselves to be its owner appears, it is the responsibility of such person to prove ownership in court.
By-laws of the Donetsk People's Republic regulating the issues of identifying, staging, accounting for ownerless property are aimed at resolving issues of introducing state management of ownerless real estate, ensuring their safety and determining the optimal way to manage them in accordance with established standards before the owner is declared or before the form of ownership of the said property is established in court.
Chapter 33 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation regulates the procedure for considering cases of recognizing a movable thing as ownerless or recognizing ownership of an ownerless immovable thing.
According to clause 6, part 1, art. 262, part 2 art. 290 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation, an application for recognition of ownership of an ownerless immovable thing is considered by the court in a special proceeding.
Article 292 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation stipulates that the judge, when preparing a case for trial, finds out which persons (owners, actual owners and others) can provide information about the ownership of the property, and also requests the relevant organizations about the information available about it. An application to recognize a thing as ownerless or to recognize the right of ownership to an ownerless immovable thing is considered by the court with the participation of interested persons.
