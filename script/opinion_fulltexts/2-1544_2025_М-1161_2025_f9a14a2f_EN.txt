According to Art. 35 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation, persons participating in the case must conscientiously use their procedural rights and not abuse them.
If any of the persons participating in the case and notified of the time and place of the court session fail to appear, if they do not provide information about the reasons for the failure to appear or the court recognizes the reasons for their failure to appear as disrespectful, the court will consider the case without their participation (Part 3 of Article 167 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation).
The parties have the right to ask the court to consider the case in their absence and to send them copies of the court decision (Part 5 of Article 167 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation).

According to Part 1 of Art. 263 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation, cases of special proceedings are considered and resolved by the court according to the general rules of claim proceedings with the features established by this chapter and chapters 28-38 of this Code.
If, when filing an application or considering a case in a special proceeding, it is established that there is a dispute about a right within the jurisdiction of the court, the court issues a ruling to leave the application without consideration, in which it explains to the applicant and other interested parties their right to resolve the dispute in the manner of claim proceedings (Part 3 of Article 263 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation).
Within the meaning of the above norms, a legal fact is established in a special proceeding, when there is no need to resolve a dispute about the subjective right itself, the existence of which depends on the presence or absence of this fact.
Taking into account the above, the court comes to the conclusion that there is a dispute about the law.
Taking into account the foregoing, the court comes to the conclusion that the application of the Administration of the Donetsk City District of the Donetsk People's Republic must be left without consideration, since from the evidence provided it is clear that there is a dispute about the right, the resolution of which is possible through litigation.
