In accordance with Part 3 of Art. 35 of the Constitution of the Russian Federation, no one can be deprived of their property except by a court decision.
In accordance with Art. 213 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, citizens and legal entities can own any property, with the exception of certain types of property, which, in accordance with the law, cannot belong to citizens or legal entities.
According to Art. 225 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, ownerless is a thing that does not have an owner or the owner of which is unknown, or a thing to which the owner has renounced the right of ownership.
The requirements of Article 226 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation indicate that movable things abandoned by the owner or otherwise abandoned by him for the purpose of renouncing ownership of them (abandoned things) can be turned into their property by other persons (point one). And paragraph two of the said article states that a person who owns, possesses or uses a plot of land, a body of water or another object where there is an abandoned thing, the cost of which is clearly less than three thousand rubles, or abandoned scrap metals, defective products, driftwood from an alloy, dumps and drains formed during the extraction of minerals, industrial waste and other waste, has the right to turn these things into his property by starting to use them or taking other actions, indicating that the thing has become a property. Other abandoned things become the property of the person who took possession of them if, at the request of this person, they are recognized by the court as ownerless.
According to Art. 236 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, a citizen or legal entity may renounce the right of ownership of property belonging to him by declaring this or taking other actions that definitely indicate his removal from ownership, use and disposal of property without the intention of retaining any rights to this property.
By virtue of Part 1 of Art. 291 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation, in an application to recognize a movable thing as ownerless, it must indicate which movable thing is subject to recognition as ownerless, its main features must be described, as well as evidence indicating that the owner has renounced the right of ownership of it, and evidence indicating that the applicant has taken possession of this thing.
In accordance with Art. 293 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation, the court, having recognized that the owner has renounced the right of ownership of the movable thing, makes a decision to recognize the movable thing as ownerless and transfer it to the ownership of the person who has taken possession of it.
Abandoned items become the property of the person who took possession of them if, at the request of this person, they are recognized by the court as ownerless.
The refusal of the owner of a vehicle to renounce the right of ownership of it can be expressed in the commission of both actions and inactions, which definitely indicate the owner’s removal from ownership, use and disposal of property without the intention of retaining any rights to this property.
In accordance with clause 1 of the Regulations on the Territorial Administration of the Federal Agency for State Property Management in the Donetsk People's Republic, approved by order of the Federal Agency for State Property Management dated June 23, 2023 No. 131, the Territorial Administration of the Federal Property Management Agency in the Donetsk People's Republic carries out the functions of managing state property, functions of organizing the sale of privatized federal property, the sale of property seized in pursuance of court decisions or acts of bodies that provided the right to make decisions on the circulation, recovery of property, functions for the sale of confiscated, movable, ownerless, confiscated and other property converted into state ownership in accordance with the legislation of the Russian Federation.
