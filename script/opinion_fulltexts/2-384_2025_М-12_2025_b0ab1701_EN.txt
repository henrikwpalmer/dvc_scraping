The court, having examined the case materials and assessed the totality of the evidence presented, considers the stated demands to be justified and subject to satisfaction, for the following reasons.
According to Article 56 of the Civil Procedure Code of the Russian Federation, each party must prove the circumstances that it refers to as the basis for its claims and objections.
By virtue of Article 67 of the Civil Procedure Code of the Russian Federation, the court evaluates evidence according to its internal conviction, based on a comprehensive, complete, objective and direct examination of the evidence available in the case.
According to Part 3 of Article 218 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, in cases and in the manner provided for by this Code, a person may acquire the right of ownership to property that does not have an owner, to property whose owner is unknown, or to property that the owner has abandoned or to which he has lost ownership on other grounds provided by law.
In accordance with Part 1 of Article 225 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, an ownerless thing is a thing that does not have an owner, the owner of which is unknown, or the right of ownership to which the owner has refused.
Based on the provisions of Part 3 of Article 225 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, ownerless real estate is accepted for registration by the body that carries out state registration of rights to real estate, upon application from the local government body in whose territory they are located.
According to Article 130 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, immovable things (real estate, real estate) include land plots, subsoil plots and everything that is firmly connected to the land, that is, objects whose movement without disproportionate damage to their purpose is impossible, including buildings, structures, unfinished construction projects. Real estate includes residential and non-residential premises, as well as parts of buildings or structures intended to accommodate vehicles (car spaces), if the boundaries of such premises, parts of buildings or structures are described in the manner established by the legislation on state cadastral registration. Items not related to real estate, including money and securities, are recognized as movable property. Registration of rights to movable things is not required, except in cases specified in the law.
Recognition of ownership of an ownerless immovable property is carried out in a special proceeding upon the application of the body authorized to manage municipal property, in accordance with Articles 290-293 of the Civil Procedure Code of the Russian Federation.
In accordance with Part 1 of Article 4 of the Federal Constitutional Law No. 5-FKZ of October 4, 2022 “On the admission of the Donetsk People's Republic into the Russian Federation and the formation of a new subject within the Russian Federation - the Donetsk People's Republic” (hereinafter referred to as Law No. 5-FKZ), legislative and other regulatory legal acts of the Russian Federation are in force on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic from the date of admission of the Donetsk People's Republic to the Russian Federation of the People's Republic and the formation of a new subject within the Russian Federation, unless otherwise provided by Law No. 5-FKZ.
According to Part 2 of Article 4 of Law No. 5-FKZ, the regulatory legal acts of the Donetsk People's Republic are valid on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic until the end of the transition period or until the adoption of the relevant regulatory legal act of the Russian Federation and (or) the regulatory legal act of the Donetsk People's Republic.
At the same time, Article 21 of Law No. 5-FKZ establishes the specifics of regulating relations in the sphere of real estate turnover on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic.
In accordance with paragraph 2 of part 1 of Article 21 of Law No. 5-FKZ, until January 1, 2028, on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic, regulatory legal acts of the Donetsk People's Republic may establish features for regulating property relations, including land turnover, in agreement with the federal executive body performing the functions of managing state property.
By virtue of Part 7 of Article 12 of Law No. 5-FKZ, the procedure for recognizing property that is not classified as federal property, the property of a constituent entity of the Russian Federation or municipal property as ownerless, established by the regulatory legal acts of the Donetsk People's Republic, is applied until January 1, 2028, in agreement with the federal executive body performing the functions of managing state property.
In pursuance of the above norm, Decree of the Government of the Russian Federation of December 28, 2022 No. 2474 was adopted “On approval of the Rules for the management of bodies of the Donetsk People's Republic, Lugansk People's Republic, Zaporozhye region and Kherson region having signs of ownerlessness and not classified as federal property, property of a constituent entity of the Russian Federation or municipal property, located on the territory of these constituent entities of the Russian Federation”, Donetsk Law of the People's Republic of March 21, 2024 No. 66-RZ "On the specifics of identifying, using and recognizing the right of municipal ownership of municipalities of the Donetsk People's Republic to residential premises with signs of an ownerless creature located on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic", amendments were made to the Decree of the Head of the Donetsk People's Republic of April 28, 2021 No. 116 "On identifying, recording and accepting into municipal ownership ownerless real estate and escheat property."

According to Article 2 of the Law of the Donetsk People's Republic of March 21, 2024 No. 66-RZ “On the specifics of identifying, using and recognizing the right of municipal property of municipalities of the Donetsk People's Republic to residential premises that have signs of ownerless property located on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic” (hereinafter referred to as Law No. 66-RZ), the signs of ownerless property in relation to residential premises are:
1) failure to pay for residential premises and utilities within one year from the date of identification of residential premises that have signs of ownerlessness;
2) lack of information about the registered ownership of residential premises in the Unified State Register of Real Estate;
3) non-use of residential premises, which, among other things, creates a threat to its safety (or an apartment building if the premises is an apartment, a room in an apartment building), safety for the life and health of other persons (including the rights holders of other premises in an apartment building if the premises is an apartment, a room in an apartment building).
In accordance with Article 3 of Law No. 66-RZ, local government bodies of the Donetsk People's Republic, represented by bodies authorized by them, independently carry out actions to identify, record and accept residential premises that have signs of ownerless property into municipal ownership of the municipalities of the Donetsk People's Republic, on the territories of which they are located, on the grounds provided for by law.
In accordance with Part 1 of Article 10 of Law No. 66-RZ, after three months from the date of registration of a residential premises as ownerless by the territorial body of the federal executive body carrying out state cadastral registration, state registration of rights, maintenance of the Unified State Register of Real Estate, the authorized body applies to the court with an application for recognition of the right of municipal ownership of the municipality to this residential premises in the manner prescribed by the legislation of the Russian Federation.
