In accordance with paragraph 3 of Article 218 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, it is determined that in cases and in the manner provided for by the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, a person can acquire ownership of property that does not have an owner, of property whose owner is unknown, or of property that the owner has refused or for which he has lost ownership on other grounds provided by law.
Clause 1 of Article 225 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation states that an ownerless thing is a thing that does not have an owner or the owner of which is unknown or, unless otherwise provided by law, the right of ownership to which the owner has refused.
In accordance with the requirements of Article 236 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, it is noted that a citizen or legal entity may renounce the right of ownership of property belonging to him by declaring this or taking other actions that definitely indicate his removal from ownership, use and disposal of property without the intention of retaining any rights to this property. Relinquishment of the right of ownership does not entail the termination of the rights and obligations of the owner in relation to the relevant property until the acquisition of ownership of it by another person.
By virtue of the provisions of Article 226 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, it is determined that movable things abandoned by the owner or otherwise abandoned by him for the purpose of renouncing the right of ownership of them (abandoned things) can be turned into their property by other persons in the manner prescribed by paragraph 2 of Article 226 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation. Abandoned items become the property of the person who took possession of them if, at the request of this person, they are recognized by the court as ownerless.
Part one of Article 291 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation states that the application for recognition of a movable thing as ownerless must indicate which movable thing is subject to recognition as ownerless, its main features must be described, as well as evidence indicating the owner’s renunciation of ownership of it, and evidence indicating that the applicant has taken possession of this thing.
Part one of Article 293 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation states that the court, having recognized that the owner has renounced the right of ownership of the movable thing, makes a decision to recognize the movable thing as ownerless and transfer it to the ownership of the person who has taken possession of it.
It follows from the above that the circumstances to be established when resolving claims to recognize a thing as ownerless are the presence or absence of an owner of a movable thing, its unknown nature, as well as the fact that the owner, when establishing it, renounced his right to the thing.
A decision to recognize an abandoned item as ownerless can be made only if indisputable evidence is presented indicating that the owner has renounced his rights to it. In this case, the refusal of the thing must correspond to the actual will of the owner and should not be forced. In itself, the inaction of the owner of a thing does not indicate abandonment of it. To recognize a movable thing as ownerless, the person who has taken possession of it must provide evidence that the ownerless thing does not have an owner or its owner is unknown, or the owner has renounced the right of ownership of this thing.
