Under such circumstances, taking into account the provisions of Part 1 of Article 167 of the Civil Procedure Code of the Russian Federation (hereinafter referred to as the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation), the court considers it possible to consider this case in the absence of the parties who failed to appear.
Having examined the case materials, the court considers the claims of the administration of the Donetsk urban district of the DPR to be satisfied on the following grounds.
In accordance with Art. 8 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, civil rights and obligations arise from the grounds provided for by law and other legal acts, as well as from the actions of citizens and legal entities, which, although not provided for by law or such acts, but due to the general principles and meaning of civil legislation give rise to civil rights and obligations.
In accordance with paragraph 3 of Art. 218 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, in cases and in the manner provided for by this Code, a person may acquire the right of ownership to property that does not have an owner, to property whose owner is unknown, or to property that the owner has abandoned or to which he has lost the right of ownership on other grounds provided by law.
According to paragraph 1 of Art. 225 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, ownerless is a thing that does not have an owner or the owner of which is unknown or, unless otherwise provided by law, the right of ownership to which the owner has refused.
In accordance with paragraph 3 of Art. 225 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, ownerless real estate is accepted for registration by the body that carries out state registration of rights to real estate, upon application from the local government body in whose territory they are located. After a year has passed from the date of registration of an ownerless immovable property, the body authorized to manage municipal property may apply to the court with a request to recognize the right of municipal ownership of this thing. An ownerless immovable property, not recognized by a court decision as having come into municipal ownership, may be again taken into possession, use and disposal by the owner who abandoned it, or acquired into ownership by virtue of acquisitive prescription.
In accordance with the provisions of Art. 293 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, the court, having recognized that the owner has renounced the right of ownership of the movable thing, makes a decision to recognize the movable thing as ownerless and transfer it to the ownership of the person who has taken possession of it. Having recognized that the immovable thing does not have an owner or the owner of the immovable thing is unknown and it has been registered in the prescribed manner, the court makes a decision to recognize the right of municipal property or the property of the federal city of Moscow or St. Petersburg to this thing.
According to paragraphs 1 and 2 of Art. 130 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, immovable things (real estate, real estate) include land plots, subsoil plots and everything that is firmly connected to the land, that is, objects whose movement without disproportionate damage to their purpose is impossible, including buildings, structures, unfinished construction objects. Items not related to real estate, including money and securities, are recognized as movable property.

In accordance with Art. 12 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation, justice is carried out on the basis of competition and equality of the parties.
By virtue of Art. 210 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, the owner bears the burden of maintaining the property belonging to him, unless otherwise provided by law or contract.
By virtue of Art. 56 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation, each party must prove the circumstances to which it refers as the basis for its claims and objections, unless otherwise provided by federal law.
There were no claims from the defendant or third parties regarding the rights to the disputed apartment, no disputes regarding ownership and use were declared, and it was not recognized as ownerless.
In accordance with Article 14 of the Federal Law of October 6, 2003 N 131-FZ “On the General Principles of the Organization of Local Self-Government in the Russian Federation,” issues of local importance of the settlement include: ownership, use and disposal of property that is municipally owned by the settlement.
In such circumstances, since in relation to the disputed real estate the owner renounced the ownership of it, other persons did not declare their rights to the residential premises, which are the subject of this dispute, the court comes to the conclusion that there are legal grounds for recognizing the movable property as ownerless and recognizing the right of municipal ownership to it.
Taking into account the evidence presented, the court finds the plaintiff's claims to be satisfied.
