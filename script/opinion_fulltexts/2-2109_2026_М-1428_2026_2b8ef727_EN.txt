Having examined the case materials, the court comes to the conclusion that the application must be granted for the following reasons.
According to the provisions of Article 3 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation, an interested person has the right, in the manner established by the legislation on civil proceedings, to apply to the court for the protection of violated or disputed rights, freedoms or legitimate interests.
In accordance with Part 2 of Article 290 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation, an application for recognition of ownership of an ownerless immovable property is submitted to the court at its location by the body authorized to manage municipal property or property owned by the federal city of Moscow, St. Petersburg or Sevastopol.
Part 2 of Article 293 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation provides that the court, having recognized that the immovable thing does not have an owner or the owner of the immovable thing is unknown and it has been registered in the prescribed manner, makes a decision to recognize the right of municipal property or the property of a federal city of Moscow, St. Petersburg or Sevastopol to this thing.
By virtue of paragraph 1 of Article 225 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation (hereinafter referred to as the Civil Code of the Russian Federation), an ownerless thing is a thing that does not have an owner or the owner of which is unknown or, unless otherwise provided by law, the right of ownership to which the owner has refused.
Paragraph 3 of Article 218 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation establishes that in cases and in the manner provided for by this Code, a person may acquire the right of ownership to property that does not have an owner, to property whose owner is unknown, or to property that the owner has abandoned or to which he has lost the right of ownership on other grounds provided by law.
According to Article 4 of the Federal Constitutional Law of the Russian Federation of October 4, 2022 No. 5-FKZ “On the admission into the Russian Federation of the Donetsk People's Republic and the formation within the Russian Federation of a new subject of the Donetsk People's Republic” (hereinafter referred to as the Law of October 4, 2022 No. 5-FKZ), legislative and other regulatory legal acts of the Russian Federation are valid on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic from the date of adoption in the Russian Federation of the Donetsk People's Republic and the formation of a new subject within the Russian Federation, unless otherwise provided by this Federal Constitutional Law.
Regulatory legal acts of the Donetsk People's Republic are valid on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic until the end of the transition period or until the adoption of the corresponding regulatory legal act of the Russian Federation and (or) the regulatory legal act of the Donetsk People's Republic.
According to Part 7 of Article 12 of the Federal Constitutional Law of the Russian Federation of October 4, 2022 No. 5-FKZ, the management of property located on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic, which has signs of ownerlessness and is not classified as federal property, the property of a constituent entity of the Russian Federation or municipal property, is carried out by the authorities of the Donetsk People's Republic in the manner established by the Government of the Russian Federation, unless otherwise provided by this Federal Constitutional Law. The procedure for recognizing such property as ownerless, established by the regulatory legal acts of the Donetsk People's Republic, is applied until January 1, 2028, in agreement with the federal executive body exercising the functions of managing state property.
According to paragraph 1.2 of the Decree of Chapter No. 140 of May 10, 2023 “On the specifics of acquiring the right of state and municipal ownership of ownerless real estate on the territory of the Donetsk People’s Republic,” the executive body of the Donetsk People’s Republic authorized to manage state property has the right to apply to the court with a demand for recognition of the right of state ownership of objects defined in subparagraph 1.1 of this paragraph (with the exception of residential premises, economic buildings intended to satisfy citizens' household and other needs) after three months from the date of their registration as ownerless real estate by the body carrying out state registration of rights to real estate.
In accordance with the Decree of the Government of the Donetsk People's Republic of June 22, 2023 No. 45-5 “On recognizing real estate located on the territory of the Donetsk People’s Republic as ownerless, with the exception of residential buildings, residential premises, garden houses, outbuildings intended to satisfy citizens’ household and other needs,” the executive body of the Donetsk People’s Republic authorized to manage state real estate is determined The Ministry of Property and Land Relations of the Donetsk People's Republic has approved the Procedure for recognizing real estate located on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic as ownerless, with the exception of residential buildings, residential premises, garden houses and outbuildings intended to satisfy citizens' household and other needs (hereinafter - the Procedure), which regulates legal relations and establishes the procedure for recognizing property on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic as ownerless, as well as recognizing the right state ownership of the Donetsk People's Republic of ownerless immovable property.
Clause 7 of the Procedure establishes that no later than the next working day after receiving an application for the identification of an ownerless immovable property from the body that identified the object, the DPR SPFU sends to the executive body of the Donetsk People's Republic implementing state policy in the field of mass communications information about the identified ownerless immovable property, with the aim of posting this information on the official website of the Donetsk People's Republic in the information and telecommunications network "Internet" to identify and inform property owners.
According to paragraph 9 of the Procedure, from the moment of sending an application for the identification of an ownerless immovable property, in order to ensure the safety of the specified property, the body that identified the object takes measures to ensure the safety of the property, and also conducts an inspection of the ownerless immovable property, during which it checks the condition of the ownerless property, and also indicates the technical characteristics (in the absence of initial data on the physical characteristics of the immovable property, it carries out full-scale measurements of the object).
An application from the DPR MIZO for registration of an identified ownerless immovable property is submitted to the authorized territorial body of the federal executive body that carries out state cadastral registration, state registration of rights, maintenance of the Unified State Register of Real Estate, no later than ten days from the date of receipt of comprehensive information on the requests specified in paragraph 8 of this Procedure, based on the order of the DPR MIZO (clause 11 of the Procedure).
In accordance with paragraph 12 of the Procedure, after three months from the date of registration with the authorized territorial body of the federal executive body carrying out state cadastral registration, state registration of rights, maintenance of the Unified State Register of Real Estate as an ownerless immovable property, with the exception of linear objects (including their integral technological parts), the DPR SPFU has the right to apply to the judicial authorities with an application for recognition of state ownership rights to ownerless immovable thing.
As follows from the case materials and established by the court, by Decree of the Head of the Donetsk People's Republic of December 13, 2024 No. 695 “On the formation (on the creation) of the Ministry of Property and Land Relations of the Donetsk People's Republic”, the MIZO of the DPR was formed and the Regulations on the Ministry (hereinafter referred to as the Regulations) were approved.
In accordance with subclause 3.1.51 of clause 3.1 of the Regulations, the MIZO of the DPR is empowered to apply to the court with demands to recognize an immovable property as ownerless and to recognize the right of state ownership of the Donetsk People's Republic to ownerless immovable things (with the exception of linear objects).
According to subclause 5.1.5. clause 5.1.
