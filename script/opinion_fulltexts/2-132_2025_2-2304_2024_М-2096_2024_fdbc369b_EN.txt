According to Part. 1 - 3 tbsp. 67 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation, the court evaluates evidence according to its internal conviction, based on a comprehensive, complete, objective and direct examination of the evidence available in the case. No evidence has predetermined value for the court. The court evaluates the relevance, admissibility, reliability of each evidence separately, as well as the sufficiency and interconnection of the evidence in its entirety. The Resolution of the Constitutional Court of the Russian Federation dated June 5, 2012 N 13-P states that the assessment of evidence and the reflection of its results in a court decision is one of the manifestations of the discretionary powers of the court necessary for the administration of justice, arising from the principle of independence of the judiciary, which, however, does not imply the possibility of the court evaluating evidence arbitrarily and in contradiction with the law. In accordance with Art. 195 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation, a court decision must be lawful and justified. According to paragraph 3 of the Resolution of the Plenum of the Supreme Court of the Russian Federation dated December 19, 2003 N 23 “On the court decision”, the decision is justified when the facts relevant to the case are confirmed by evidence examined by the court that meets the requirements of the law on their relevance and admissibility, or by circumstances that do not require proof (Articles 55, 59 - 61, 67 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation), and also when it contains exhaustive conclusions of the court arising from the established facts. The court's conclusions about facts of legal significance for the case should not be general and abstract; they must be demonstrated in a convincing manner in the court ruling, otherwise the objectives and meaning of the proceedings are violated.
According to Part 3 of Art. 263 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation, if, when filing an application or considering a case in a special proceeding, the existence of a dispute about the right is established, the court issues a ruling to leave the application without consideration, in which it explains to the applicant and other interested parties their right to resolve the dispute in the manner of claim proceedings.
Within the meaning of this norm, the absence of a dispute about the law is a prerequisite for the application of procedural rules of special proceedings.
The fact of recognition of the right of municipal ownership of a real estate object registered as ownerless may be established by a court according to the rules of special proceedings on the basis of comprehensively verified data, provided that there is no dispute about the right.
Guided by paragraph 1 of Art. 225 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, according to which ownerless is a thing that does not have an owner or the owner of which is unknown, or, unless otherwise provided by law, a property right to which the owner has refused. Ownerless real estate is accepted for registration by the body that carries out state registration of rights to real estate, upon application from the local government body in whose territory they are located.
Within the meaning of this legal norm, the registration of an object of real estate as ownerless, as well as the subsequent recognition of the body authorized to manage municipal property, the right of ownership of an ownerless real estate in court on the basis of Art. 225 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation is possible only in the absence of legal claims of other persons to such property.
At the same time, the absence of state registration of the ownership rights of a company or another person to a disputed object cannot in itself be a basis for declaring it ownerless.
Within the meaning of these norms, this application is considered in special proceedings, when there is no need to resolve a dispute about the subjective right itself, which is subject to unconditional determination by the court.
