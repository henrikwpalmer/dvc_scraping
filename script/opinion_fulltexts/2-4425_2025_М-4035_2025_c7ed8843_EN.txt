Having examined the case materials, the court comes to the following conclusion.
According to Article 3 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation, an interested person has the right, in the manner established by the legislation on civil proceedings, to apply to the court for the protection of violated or disputed rights, freedoms or legitimate interests.
In accordance with paragraph 6 of part 1 of Article 262 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation, in a special proceeding, the court considers cases of recognizing a movable thing as ownerless and recognizing the right of municipal ownership of an ownerless immovable thing.
By virtue of Part 1 of Article 292 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation, when preparing a case for trial, the judge finds out which persons (owners, actual owners and others) can provide information about the ownership of the property, and also asks the relevant organizations about the information available about it.
As follows from Article 293 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation, the court, having recognized that the owner has renounced the right of ownership of the movable thing, makes a decision to recognize the movable thing as ownerless and transfer it into the ownership of the person who has taken possession of it. The court, having recognized that the immovable thing does not have an owner or the owner of the immovable thing is unknown, and it has been registered in the prescribed manner, makes a decision to recognize the right of municipal ownership or the ownership of the corresponding city of federal significance to this thing.
Part 3 of Article 263 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation stipulates that if, when filing an application or considering a case in a special proceeding, the existence of a dispute about the right is established, the court issues a ruling to leave the application without consideration, in which it explains to the applicant and other interested parties their right to resolve the dispute in the manner of claim proceedings.
Within the meaning of these norms, this application is considered in special proceedings, when there is no need to resolve a dispute about the subjective right itself, which is subject to unconditional determination by the court.
