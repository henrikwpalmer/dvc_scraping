According to Part 1 of Art. 3 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation, an interested person has the right, in the manner established by the legislation on civil proceedings, to apply to the court for the protection of violated or disputed rights, freedoms or legitimate interests.
In accordance with Art. 56 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation, each party must prove the circumstances to which it refers as the basis for its claims and objections, unless otherwise provided by federal law. The court determines what circumstances are important for the case, which party must prove them, and brings the circumstances up for discussion, even if the parties did not refer to any of them. Each person participating in the case must disclose the evidence to which he refers as the basis for his claims and objections to other persons participating in the case, within the period established by the court, unless otherwise established by this Code.
Article 60 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation provides that the circumstances of the case, which in accordance with the law must be confirmed by certain means of proof, cannot be confirmed by any other evidence.
Circumstances established by a court decision that has entered into legal force in a previously considered case, binding on the court. The specified circumstances are not proven again and are not subject to challenge when considering another case in which the same persons are involved, also in cases provided for by this Code (Part 2 of Article 61 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation).
Parts 1, 2, 3 of Article 67 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation provide that the court evaluates evidence according to its internal conviction, based on a comprehensive, complete, objective and direct examination of the evidence available in the case. No evidence has predetermined value for the court. The court evaluates the relevance, admissibility, reliability of each evidence separately, as well as the sufficiency and interconnection of the evidence in its entirety.
By virtue of Part 2 of Art. 195 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation, the court bases its decision only on the evidence that was examined at the court hearing.

In accordance with subparagraph 3.1 of paragraph 3 of the Decree of the Acting Head of the Donetsk People's Republic of February 27, 2023 No. 64 “On some issues related to the management of property that has signs of ownerlessness, located on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic, and the recognition of such property as ownerless” (hereinafter referred to as Decree No. 64), city and district administrations are bodies for managing property that has signs of ownerlessness and not classified as federal property located on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic, in accordance with the Rules approved by the Decree of the Government of the Russian Federation of December 28, 2022 No. 2474 “On approval of the Rules for the management of bodies of the Donetsk People's Republic, Lugansk People's Republic, Zaporozhye region and Kherson region having signs of ownerlessness and not classified as federal property, property of a constituent entity of the Russian Federation or municipal ownership of property located on the territories of the specified constituent entities of the Russian Federation.
Clause 1.2 of the decision of the Donetsk City Council of the Donetsk People's Republic dated November 30, 2023 No. I/8-3 “On issues of legal succession in the municipal formation of the Donetsk City District of the Donetsk People's Republic” (hereinafter referred to as decision No. I/8-3) determined that the legal successor of property rights and obligations of the bodies exercising on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic on the day of admission of the Donetsk People's Republic to the Russian Federation Republic and the formation of a new subject within the Russian Federation, the powers assigned by the legislation of the Russian Federation to the powers of local government bodies are exercised by the administration of the city district of Donetsk of the Donetsk People's Republic - in terms of property necessary to ensure its activities, as well as other property that is in the municipal ownership of the municipal formation of the city district of Donetsk of the Donetsk People's Republic (treasury, property transferred to operational management, economic management).
Clause 3.1.11 of the Regulations on the Department of Municipal Property of the Administration of the Donetsk City District of the Donetsk People's Republic (hereinafter referred to as the Department of Municipal Property), approved by the decision of the Donetsk City Council of the Donetsk People's Republic dated January 6, 2024 No. I/14-5, determines that the Department of Municipal Property carries out work on the transfer of ownerless (escheated) real estate to the municipal property of the city district Donetsk.
The procedure for identifying, recording and accepting into the municipal ownership of the municipality the Donetsk City District of the Donetsk People's Republic ownerless real estate of the housing stock is determined by the procedure for identifying, accounting and accepting into the municipal ownership of the municipality the Donetsk City District of the Donetsk People's Republic ownerless real estate of the housing stock, approved by the decision of the Donetsk City Council of the Donetsk People's Republic dated July 4, 2024 No. I/32-5 (hereinafter referred to as Procedure No. I/32-5), which was developed in accordance with the Constitution of the Russian Federation, the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, the Constitution of the Donetsk People's Republic, the Law of the Donetsk People's Republic of March 21, 2024 No. 66-RZ.
According to clause 2.1 of Section II of Procedure No. I/32-5, the city district administration, represented by the municipal property department, independently carries out actions to identify, record and accept into municipal ownership ownerless real estate located on the territory of the Donetsk urban district. Information about residential premises that have signs of ownerlessness may be received by the municipal property department from individuals and legal entities, including public authorities, who have become aware of such property.
According to clause 2.2 of Procedure No. I/32-5, the signs of ownerless real estate in relation to residential premises are: failure to pay for residential premises and utilities within one year before the date of identification of residential premises that have signs of ownerlessness; lack of information about the registered ownership of residential premises in the Unified State Register of Real Estate; non-use of residential premises, which, among other things, creates a threat to its safety (or an apartment building if the premises is an apartment, a room in an apartment building), safety for the life and health of other persons (including the rights holders of other premises in an apartment building if the premises is an apartment, a room in an apartment building).
Due to the fact that the administration of the urban district of Donetsk of the Donetsk People's Republic, in accordance with the above provisions of the law, is vested with the authority to manage municipal property, in connection with which, the court comes to the conclusion that the authorized body applied to the court to recognize the residential premises as ownerless and to recognize the right of municipal ownership of the municipality to this residential premises.
Clause 1 of Art. 225 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, it is determined that ownerless is a thing that does not have an owner or the owner of which is unknown or, unless otherwise provided by law, the right of ownership to which the owner has refused.
Clause 3 of Art. 218 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, it is stated that in cases and in the manner provided for by the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, a person can acquire ownership of property that does not have an owner, of property whose owner is unknown, or of property that the owner has refused or for which he has lost ownership on other grounds provided by law.
Article 2 of the Law of the Donetsk People's Republic of March 21, 2024 No. 66-RZ, establishes that the signs of ownerless property in relation to residential premises are: failure to pay for residential premises and utilities within one year before the day of identification of residential premises that have signs of ownerlessness; lack of information about the registered ownership of residential premises in the Unified State Register of Real Estate; non-use of residential premises, which, among other things, creates a threat to its safety (or an apartment building if the premises is an apartment, a room in an apartment building), safety for the life and health of other persons (including the rights holders of other premises in an apartment building if the premises is an apartment, a room in an apartment building).
