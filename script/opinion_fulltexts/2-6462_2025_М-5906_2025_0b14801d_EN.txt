Having examined the case materials and assessed the evidence presented in its entirety, the court comes to the conclusion that the stated requirements have been satisfied for the following reasons.
According to parts 1, 2 of Article 4 of the Federal Constitutional Law of October 4, 2022 No. 5-FKZ “On the admission of the Donetsk People's Republic into the Russian Federation and the formation within the Russian Federation of a new subject - the Donetsk People's Republic”, legislative and other regulatory legal acts of the Russian Federation are valid on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic from the date of admission of the Donetsk People's Republic into the Russian Federation and the formation within the Russian Federation Federation of a new subject, unless otherwise provided by this Federal Constitutional Law. Regulatory legal acts of the Donetsk People's Republic are valid on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic until the end of the transition period or until the adoption of the relevant regulatory legal act of the Russian Federation and (or) the regulatory legal act of the Donetsk People's Republic.
In accordance with Part 7 of Article 12 of the Federal Constitutional Law “On the admission of the Donetsk People's Republic to the Russian Federation and the formation of a new subject within the Russian Federation - the Donetsk People's Republic”, management of property located on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic, which has signs of ownerlessness and is not classified as federal property, the property of a subject of the Russian Federation or municipal property, is carried out by the authorities of the Donetsk People's Republic in the manner established by the Government of the Russian Federation, if otherwise not provided for by this Federal Constitutional Law. The procedure for recognizing such property as ownerless, established by the regulatory legal acts of the Donetsk People's Republic, is applied until January 1, 2028, in agreement with the federal executive body exercising the functions of managing state property.
According to Article 2 of the Law of the Donetsk People's Republic dated March 21, 2024 No. 66-RZ “On the specifics of identifying, using and recognizing the right of municipal property of municipalities of the Donetsk People's Republic to residential premises that have signs of ownerless property located on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic,” the signs of ownerless property in relation to residential premises are: 1) failure to pay for residential premises and utilities within one year before the day of identification of a residential premises showing signs of ownerlessness; 2) lack of information about the registered ownership of residential premises in the Unified State Register of Real Estate; 3) non-use of residential premises, which, among other things, creates a threat to its safety (or an apartment building if the premises is an apartment, a room in an apartment building), safety for the life and health of other persons (including the rights holders of other premises in an apartment building if the premises is an apartment, a room in an apartment building).
Article 3 of the Law of the Donetsk People's Republic dated March 21, 2024 No. 66-RZ provides that local government bodies of the Donetsk People's Republic, represented by bodies authorized by them, independently carry out actions to identify, record and accept residential premises that have signs of ownerless property into the municipal ownership of the municipalities of the Donetsk People's Republic, on the territory of which they are located, on the grounds provided for by law.
In accordance with Article 10 of the Law of the Donetsk People's Republic of March 21, 2024 No. 66-RZ, after three months from the date of registration of a residential premises as an ownerless territorial body of the federal executive body carrying out state cadastral registration, state registration of rights, maintaining the Unified State Register of Real Estate, the authorized body applies to the court with an application for recognition of the right of municipal property of the municipality to this residential property premises in the manner prescribed by the legislation of the Russian Federation.
In accordance with Part 1 of Article 225 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, an ownerless thing is a thing that does not have an owner or the owner of which is unknown or, unless otherwise provided by law, the right of ownership to which the owner has refused.
According to Part 3 of Article 225 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, ownerless real estate is accepted for registration by the body that carries out state registration of rights to real estate, upon application from the local government body in whose territory they are located.
In accordance with paragraph 6 of part 1 of Article 262 of the Civil Procedure Code of the Russian Federation, in a special proceeding, the court considers cases of recognizing the right of municipal ownership of an ownerless immovable property.
According to Part 2 of Article 290 of the Civil Procedure Code of the Russian Federation, an application for recognition of ownership of an ownerless immovable property is submitted to the court at its location by the body authorized to manage municipal property or property owned by the federal city of Moscow, St. Petersburg or Sevastopol.
