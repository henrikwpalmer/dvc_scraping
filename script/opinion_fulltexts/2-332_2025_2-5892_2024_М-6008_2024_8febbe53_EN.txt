According to clause 6, part 1, art. 262 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation, by way of special proceedings, the court considers cases of recognizing a movable thing as ownerless and recognizing the right of municipal ownership of an ownerless immovable thing.
Clause 1 of Art. 225 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation defines that an ownerless thing is a thing that does not have an owner or the owner of which is unknown or, unless otherwise provided by law, the right of ownership to which the owner has refused.
The grounds for acquiring property rights are provided for in Art. 218 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation. By virtue of Part 3 of Art. 218 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, in cases and in the manner provided for by the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, a person may acquire ownership of property that does not have an owner, of property whose owner is unknown, or of property that the owner has abandoned or for which he has lost ownership on other grounds provided by law.
Depending on the type of ownerless thing, the law establishes various grounds and procedures for acquiring ownership rights.
Thus, in order to recognize the right of municipal ownership of an ownerless immovable property in a special manner, a set of mandatory conditions listed above is necessary.
According to Part 3 of Art. 263 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation, if, when filing an application or considering a case in a special proceeding, it is established that there is a dispute about the right within the jurisdiction of the court, the court issues a ruling to leave the application without consideration, in which it explains to the applicant and other interested parties their right to resolve the dispute in the manner of claim proceedings.
Thus, the subject of proof in the case of recognizing the right of municipal ownership of an ownerless real estate also includes establishing the absence of a dispute about the right of ownership of real estate.
