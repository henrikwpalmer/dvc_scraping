The court, guided by the provisions of Part 3 of Art. 167 of the Civil Procedure Code of the Russian Federation considers it possible to consider an application in the absence of participants in the process who have not appeared.
Having examined the case materials, the court comes to the following conclusions.
In accordance with Part 3 of Art. 218 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, in cases and in the manner provided for by this Code, a person may acquire ownership of property that does not have an owner, property whose owner is unknown, or property that the owner has abandoned or to which he has lost ownership on other grounds provided by law.
By virtue of Art. 225 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, ownerless is a thing that does not have an owner or the owner of which is unknown or, unless otherwise provided by law, the right of ownership to which the owner has refused. Ownerless real estate is accepted for registration by the body that carries out state registration of rights to real estate, upon application from the local government body in whose territory they are located. After a year has passed from the date of registration of an ownerless immovable property, the body authorized to manage municipal property may apply to the court with a request to recognize the right of municipal ownership of this thing.
According to clause 6, part 1, art. 262 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation, cases of recognition of the right of municipal ownership of an ownerless immovable property are considered by the court in a special proceeding.
Part 2 of this article establishes that Federal laws may include other cases for consideration as special proceedings.
Article 4 of the Federal Constitutional Law of the Russian Federation of October 4, 2022 No. 5-FKZ, “On the admission into the Russian Federation of the Donetsk People's Republic and the formation within the Russian Federation of a new subject - the Donetsk People's Republic” (hereinafter referred to as Law No. 5-FKZ) determines that legislative and other regulatory legal acts of the Russian Federation are valid on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic from the date of admission to the Russian Federation Federation of the Donetsk People's Republic and the formation of a new subject within the Russian Federation, unless otherwise provided by this Federal Constitutional Law.
Regulatory legal acts of the Donetsk People's Republic are valid on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic until the end of the transition period or until the adoption of the corresponding regulatory legal act of the Russian Federation and (or) the regulatory legal act of the Donetsk People's Republic.
Part 7 of Article 12 of Law No. 5-FKZ establishes that the management of property located on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic, which has signs of ownerlessness and is not classified as federal property, the property of a constituent entity of the Russian Federation or municipal property, is carried out by the authorities of the Donetsk People's Republic in the manner established by the Government of the Russian Federation, unless otherwise provided by this Federal Constitutional Law. The procedure for recognizing such property as ownerless, established by the regulatory legal acts of the Donetsk People's Republic, is applied until January 1, 2028, in agreement with the federal executive body exercising the functions of managing state property.
Part 1 of Article 10 of the Law of the Donetsk People's Republic of March 21, 2024 No. 66-P3 “On the specifics of identifying, using and recognizing the right of municipal property of municipalities of the Donetsk People's Republic to residential premises that have signs of ownerless property located on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic” (hereinafter referred to as Law No. 66) determines that after three months from the date of placing the residential premises in as an ownerless territorial body of the federal executive body carrying out state cadastral registration, state registration of rights, maintaining the Unified State Register of Real Estate, the authorized body applies to the court with an application for recognition of the right of municipal ownership of the municipal formation to this residential premises in the manner prescribed by the legislation of the Russian Federation.
In accordance with Article 3 of Law No. 66, local government bodies of the Donetsk People's Republic, represented by the bodies authorized by them (hereinafter referred to as the authorized bodies), independently carry out actions to identify, record and accept residential premises that have signs of ownerless property into the municipal ownership of the municipalities of the Donetsk People's Republic, on the territory of which they are located, on the grounds provided for by law.
According to Part 1 of Article 4 of Law No. 66, information about residential premises that have signs of ownerlessness can come from individuals and legal entities, including public authorities, who have become aware of such property.
By virtue of Part 2 of Article 5 of Law No. 66, the Administration of G.O. Makeyevka, all the prescribed measures were taken to identify an immovable property that has signs of being ownerless.
Based on Part 3 of Article 1 of the Federal Law of the Russian Federation dated July 13, 2015 No. 218-FZ “On State Registration of Real Estate” (hereinafter referred to as Law No. 218-FZ), state registration of rights to real estate is a legal act of recognition and confirmation of the emergence, change, transition, termination of a certain person’s right to real estate or restriction of such a right and encumbrance of real estate (hereinafter referred to as state registration of rights).
In accordance with Part 3 of Article 3 of Law No. 218-FZ, the competence of the rights registration authority when it carries out state cadastral registration
and state registration of rights include, among other things:
1) checking the validity of the documents submitted by the applicant and the availability of the corresponding rights of the person or authority who prepared the document;
2) checking the presence of previously registered and previously declared rights;
3) registration of ownerless real estate in the manner established by the regulatory body.
