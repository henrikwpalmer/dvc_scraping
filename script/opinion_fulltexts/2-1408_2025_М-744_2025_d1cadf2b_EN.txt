The provisions of Art. 225 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation establishes that the ownerlessness of real estate presupposes the absence or unknownness of its owner, and also the condition for considering an application to recognize the property as ownerless is the absence of a dispute about the right.

The procedure for recognizing the ownership of an ownerless immovable property by the body authorized to manage municipal property is defined in Chapter 33 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation.
In accordance with the provisions of Art. 293 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation, the court, having recognized that the owner has renounced the right of ownership of the movable thing, makes a decision to recognize the movable thing as ownerless and transfer it to the ownership of the person who has taken possession of it.
By virtue of the provisions of paragraphs 1 and 2 of Art. 130 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, immovable things (real estate, real estate) include land plots, subsoil plots and everything that is firmly connected to the land, i.e. objects, the movement of which without disproportionate damage to their purpose is impossible, including buildings, structures, unfinished construction objects. Items not related to real estate, including money and securities, are recognized as movable property.
