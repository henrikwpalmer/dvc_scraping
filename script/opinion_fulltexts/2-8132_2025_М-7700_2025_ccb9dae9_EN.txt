According to Art. 262 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation, in the order of special proceedings, the court considers cases: 1) on the establishment of facts of legal significance; 2) about the adoption of a child; 3) on declaring a citizen missing or declaring a citizen dead; 4) on limiting the legal capacity of a citizen, on declaring a citizen incompetent, on limiting or depriving a minor aged fourteen to eighteen years of the right to independently manage their income; 5) on declaring a minor fully capable (emancipation); 6) on recognition of a movable thing as ownerless and recognition of the right of municipal ownership to an ownerless immovable thing; 7) on the restoration of rights to lost bearer securities or order securities (call proceedings); 9) on making corrections or changes in civil status records; 10) on applications for notarial acts performed or refusal to perform them; 11) on applications for the restoration of lost judicial proceedings. Federal laws may include other cases for consideration as special proceedings.
According to Part 1 of Art. 225 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, an ownerless thing is a thing that does not have an owner or the owner of which is unknown, or a thing for which the owner has renounced the right of ownership.
Based on paragraph 3 of Article 225 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, ownerless real estate is accepted for registration by the body that carries out state registration of rights to real estate, upon application from the local government body in whose territory they are located. After a year has passed from the date of registration of an ownerless immovable property, the body authorized to manage municipal property may apply to the court with a request to recognize the right of municipal ownership of this thing.
Clause 7 of Article 12 of the Federal Constitutional Law No. 5-FKZ of October 4, 2022 “On the admission of the Donetsk People’s Republic into the Russian Federation and the formation of a new entity within the Russian Federation - the Donetsk People’s Republic” (hereinafter referred to as Law No. 5-FKZ) establishes: management of property located on the territory of the Donetsk People’s Republic, which has signs of ownerlessness and is not classified as federal property, property subject of the Russian Federation or municipal property, is carried out by the bodies of the Donetsk People's Republic in the manner established by the Government of the Russian Federation, unless otherwise provided by this Federal Constitutional Law. The procedure for recognizing such property as ownerless, established by the regulatory legal acts of the Donetsk People's Republic, is applied until January 1, 2028, in agreement with the federal executive body exercising the functions of managing state property.

Based on Part 2 of Article 290 of the Civil Procedure Code of the Russian Federation, an application for recognition of the right of municipal ownership of an ownerless immovable property is submitted to the court at its location by the body authorized to manage municipal property.
According to subclause 3.1. paragraph 3 of the Decree of the Head of the Donetsk People's Republic of February 27, 2023 No. 64 “On some issues related to the management of property that has signs of ownerlessness, located on the territory of the Donetsk People’s Republic, and the recognition of such property as ownerless” (hereinafter referred to as Decree No. 64), administrations of districts and cities of the Donetsk People’s Republic are bodies for the management of property that has signs of ownerlessness and is not classified to federal property, property of the Donetsk People's Republic or municipal property located on the territory of the Donetsk People's Republic, in accordance with the Rules regarding real estate in the form of residential premises, residential buildings, outbuildings.
Clause 3.2 of Decree No. 64 establishes that until January 1, 2028, in relation to the property specified in subclause 3.1 of this Decree, the procedure for recognizing it as ownerless is applied, established by Decree of the Head of the Donetsk People's Republic of April 28, 2021 No. 116 “On the identification, recording and acceptance into municipal ownership of ownerless real estate and escheat property", taking into account the changes made by this Decree.
According to subclause 8 of clause 2.6 of the Regulations on the administration of the Snezhnoe urban district of the Donetsk People's Republic, approved by the decision of the Snezhnyansky city council of the Donetsk People's Republic of the first convocation dated November 10, 2023 No. 1/6-27, the administration forms municipal property, owns, uses and disposes of property owned by the Snezhnoye urban district, maintains a register of the treasury of municipal property, as well as accounting for the municipal treasury and management other property transferred to the management of the Snezhnoye urban district of the Donetsk People's Republic.
The body authorized to manage municipal property may apply to the court to recognize the right of ownership of ownerless real estate in a special proceeding, as defined in clause 6, part 1, art. 262 Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation.
According to clause 3.2. The standard procedure for identifying, recording and accepting into municipal ownership ownerless real estate and escheat property, approved by Decree of the Head of the Donetsk People's Republic No. 116 of April 28, 2021 “On identifying, recording and accepting into municipal ownership ownerless real estate and escheat property”, if before the ownerless real estate is accepted into municipal ownership, a person (persons) appears, considering (considering) themselves to be its owner, proof of ownership lies with such a person in court.
According to Part 3 of Art. 263 of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation, if, when filing an application or considering a case in a special proceeding, it is established that there is a dispute about the right, the court issues a ruling to leave the application without consideration, in which it explains to the applicant and other interested parties their right to resolve the dispute in the manner of claim proceedings.
The main difference between special judicial proceedings and claim proceedings is the absence of a dispute about the law, which entails the absence of a substantive legal claim of one party against the other.
